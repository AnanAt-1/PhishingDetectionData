An economic relief program is a set of measures implemented by governments or organizations to provide financial assistance and support to individuals, businesses, or communities affected by economic downturns, crises, or disasters. The primary goal of such programs is to alleviate economic hardship, stimulate economic growth, and promote recovery.

WHO ARE ELIGIBLE

1 Low income families and individuals
2 Those who are residing in US legally
3 Those who has no criminal records and law abiding citizens
4 Those who are earning less than $150k annually

IMPORTANCE OF THIS PROGRAM

1. *Mitigate Economic Hardship*: Provide immediate financial assistance to those affected by economic crises.
2. *Stimulate Economic Growth*: Encourage economic activity, create jobs, and boost economic recovery.
3. *Promote Financial Stability*: Help individuals and businesses manage debt, avoid bankruptcy, and maintain financial stability.
4. *Protect Vulnerable Populations*: Ensure that low-income individuals, families, and small businesses receive necessary support during economic downturns.
