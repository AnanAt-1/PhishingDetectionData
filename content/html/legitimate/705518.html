Student Health Services at Brown University

Student Health Services provides wellness services and medical care to currently enrolled Brown University students.

The Student Health Services Pharmacy will be closed on Sunday, May 11th. 
**Beginning on Monday May 12th, Student Health Services will close at 5 p.m. on weekdays.**

 

Student Health Services and its affiliated departments aim to provide high-quality, patient-centered primary care. All services are confidential, easily accessible and responsive to your individual needs.

Student Health Services recognizes that physical and emotional health and well-being are necessary for you to pursue your educational and personal goals at Brown University. Health Services staff members partner with students to provide holistic care that is respectful of personal, cultural, and social identities. Care options are designed to provide space for education, self-discovery, and intellectual growth.

Brown University Health Announcement

In June 2024, Lifespan and Brown University announced a new set of affiliation agreements that include a name change and rebranding from Lifespan to Brown University Health. Brown University Health is now one of the largest healthcare systems in Rhode Island, serving patients in R.I. and beyond. 

Brown Health is NOT affiliated with Brown University Student Health & Wellness. To contact a provider or hospital affiliated with Brown University Health, visit brownhealth.org. To learn more about Student Health and Wellness at Brown University, visit the link below. 

Student Health & Wellness at Brown

Part of the Division of Campus Life, the five departments of Student Health and Wellness work collaboratively to provide integrated and holistic care to support students' physical, mental, and emotional well-being. They include Brown Emergency Medical Services (BEMS), BWell Health Promotion, Counseling and Psychological Services (CAPS), Student Health Services, and Student Accessibility Services (SAS).
