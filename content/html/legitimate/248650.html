Noria VP of Services, Wes Cash, explains a base oil formulation you may not have heard of using gas to liquid (GTL) technology. Wes covers the process involved in creating a GTL base oil, how it differs from standard Group I or Group II oils, and beneficial fluid properties that GTL base oils can offer in turbine applications.

Wes gives a visual demonstration of how the GTL base oil performs against a conventional Group II base oil in terms of its ability to detrain or "de-gas" entrained air bubbles. He also discusses other useful properties of GTL base oils—such as resistance to degradation or its lower varnish formation tendency.
