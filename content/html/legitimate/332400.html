Final Four Fever

Posted on April 4, 2011 by ASUCoachCharli

Well we have a few more days to enjoy March Madness where we can watch and celebrate the teams that peaked at the right time and realized their dreams. Congratulations to all of you!

As we watch the games, we can see the tremendous toughness and cohesion that has allowed these Final Four teams to rise above the rest.   As much as I appreciate the efforts of the players and the staffs this time of year, I also admire the passion and commitment of the “fans!”

Well I am off to Indianapolis to do my part at our WBCA Convention….and yes, “cheer” for our Pac-10 team.

Stay Tuned,

Change is Hard

Posted on March 23, 2011 by ASUCoachCharli

I am emerging from the all- consuming depths of yet another season with a few thoughts on my heart….. one which I will expound on today.

Change is hard. I know everybody reading this can relate in some way no matter how driven and disciplined you might be. As we continue to mentor others and grow in our own right, it is amazing how much we experience  people ‘staying the same.’  All the best intentions do not guarantee growth at the end of the day.  

So what can we do to change our less productive habits so we can truly achieve the things we desire?

As a coach, this is the million-dollar question! I think this process and getting others to do it remains elusive. However, here are a few thoughts to consider:

First, you have to want to change and have the courage to admit you need to!
  
Second, you have to somehow get “yourself or your people” to truly “buy-in” to high performance standards in which nothing but the “best” every day is acceptable.  Many people spend time on “something” but are they fully engaged where their focus, commitment, and connectedness allows them to peak perform and thus get better? Get results? Are they forming winning habits or reinforcing unsuccessful ones?

Third, they have to embrace values that are going to challenge them to be honest, work hard, and be a learner so they can help themselves and/or others.  

Lastly,  it seems like everyone “says” the right thing in terms of what they want and who they want to be but they do not “live it.”  Walk the walk! We all have to be consistent in our actions and then we have a chance to change for the better!

Just some ramblings from a tired, reflecting Coach 🙂

Don’t Worry, Be Happy

Posted on December 17, 2010 by ASUCoachCharli

Does anybody remember the Bobby McFerrin song “Don’t Worry, Be Happy?” You can not help but smile and feel good when you hear the reggae beat and positive message.

(Look it up on I-Tunes)

Well, here in the real world…it is easier said than done.  Anybody else guilty of worrying about things that “might happen?” I think I read somewhere that 80 percent of the things we worry about never happen!

So as we move forward into the  heart of our Season, our challenge is to “stay in the moment.” Let’s not waste any energy on what might happen and certainly not any on what already happened.  Truly, the ability to focus on the task at hand and be fully engaged in what we are doing is what helps us to achieve excellence and enjoy our experiences.

Maybe you have already mastered this and if so….WAY TO GO!!!!  If not, let’s work towards “mastering it” starting TODAY!

Go Devils!

Spirit of Gratitude

Posted on December 1, 2010 by ASUCoachCharli

This Holiday Season I would like to reflect on Gratitude, as it is one of our most important Sun Devil Core values. What does Gratitude really mean? Most of us are thankful for the blessings we have in our lives. We have families we love, good health, friends to laugh and play with, freedom and Free Will to live our lives the way we want to. Most of us are very grateful for these blessings, but how often do we stop to focus and reflect on that? True gratitude isn’t the act of mechanically listing the blessings in our lives every night before we go to sleep. Gratitude is an Attitude. It’s a mindset of thankfulness and joy for the wonderful blessings in our lives. It’s a heartfelt acknowledgement that our lives would not be as happy as they are now if we didn’t have those blessings. 

One of my favorite verses on gratitude is Luke 12:48 “To whom much is given, much is expected.”  We even had this printed on a team shirt one season.  Additionally, with my kids at home I am always looking for ways to instill this value into their every day life and choices.  I was making pancakes for my boys one morning and immediately my oldest sons Conor and Liam start to fight over who will get the 1st pancake.  So I capitalize on the moment- great teaching point – and I said “you know, if Jesus were here he would say let my brother have the 1st pancake and I will wait.”  To which Liam immediately responded “Ok Conor, you be Jesus.”  Kids are selfish.  When we say “trinity” we think Father, Son, Holy Spirit, and they think “me, myself, and I.”

 1 Thessalonians 5:18 – “Give thanks in all circumstances, for this is the will of God in Christ Jesus for you.”  This verse is a reminder to make sure we see “our life as a gift…” we should encourage all around us to become rich in “being” not having.  Great example is Lou Gehrig’s famous farewell speech only a month before his death from MS.  A very quiet man, he went to the microphone crippled, his body barely able to function… he took the time to thank ushers, ticket takers, janitors, and all those that helped him do his job… he said “Today I consider myself the luckiest man on the face of the earth.”  Lou Gehrig totally “got it.”

He “got” that “true gratitude” is giving to others and focusing not on our “outer” self, how we look, what people think, and how much we have, but on our “Inner self-”  Our character, our spirit, our soul is how we honor ourselves, our family, and teammates.

It’s Not Easy Being Green

Posted on October 25, 2010 by ASUCoachCharli

I was not a rabid Muppets fan but I enjoyed the show and the seasonal movies as a child. My 6 year old Quinn is quite fond and sympathetic towards Kermit.  In the grand scheme of things we all probably agree that Kermit’s challenge of being a unique color pale in comparison to the challenge of being a truly great leader.

Here are a few fun facts about “leadership”…. First, it’s a hard and dirty job that requires constant time and energy. You cannot make people do what you want them to do, you must inspire them. Leadership is about being vulnerable so people trust you. It’s about telling people things that they may not want to hear in the right way at the right time so they respect you. It is about giving up your power and serving others. It is never about “you.” It is not about what you say….it’s about what you do.

Lastly, be like Kermit does have some great qualities….like being true to himself and being a genuine caring spirit. Good luck being the best leader you can be!

Good to Great!

Posted on September 10, 2010 by ASUCoachCharli
In honor of Labor Day this week, let us celebrate the value of a good honest’s days’ work.
A coach will undoubtedly ask you at some point in your career, “do you want to be great?” We all know you are expected to say “yes!” However, there are very few “GREAT” players and do you know why?
 
It is simply because it is really a lot of HARD WORK and sacrifice to be GREAT. It requires incredible amounts of physical, mental, and emotional energy daily and a disciplined lifestyle. Let’s face it, most people in our society even athletes choose to be “comfortable” and settle for “good” or less.
Do you push out of your comfort zone consistently….do you bring a focused effort every day that demands your best? How deep do you dig….are you easily satisfied that you “did a workout” versus being critical of if you got better and pushed yourself?

There are so many things that we cannot control as people and athletes but “working hard” is not one of them.

Hard Work will ALWAYS pay off! Our Sun Devils are all about “outworking” their opponents! So keep working HARD and dare to be great!

Secret of Success

Posted on August 13, 2010 by ASUCoachCharli

“Before everything else, getting ready is the secret of success.”-Henry Ford

As we begin a new school year and another season, we have a vision. Of course, our only chance to realize it is if we are fully and completely “prepared.”

I have read a multitude of books, watched film, and filled at least 3 yellow legal pads with notes. I have spent time with my staff, my players, and many of my peers in coaching…..I am getting my “ducks in a row.”

I do not know about you but I value being prepared.  If you are in a position of “leadership” it is your responsibility to not only prepare yourself but to make sure all those under you are “prepared!”  This is obviously a most challenging job….to get all our people to meet their responsibilities daily and to take on new challenges so that together we can position ourselves to realize our vision.

When we have something we truly want to pursue, half-hearted commitments will not do… success comes from proper preparation and  then daily efforts…practice, practice, and  more practice!

I was never a “Girl Scout” out there earning badges for a job well done but I was a camper who learned to survive with a shovel and a roll of toilet paper!  These days, I prefer a hotel to a tent and doing things the “easy way.” I would rather be prepared and have things be easier than “rough it” and struggle to survive.

So, are you prepared? Avoid activity for activity sake… Organize your game plan, prioritize work with a purpose for your desired results.

Go get ’em,

Charli

Sparky Update

Posted on August 3, 2010 by ASUCoachCharli
Well we have had Sparky for almost 2 months and we love him more each day.

Being a first-time dog owner, I knew I would have a lot to learn. I find myself making comparisons between raising little kids and puppies. Such as:

 1. It is so cute when they scoot on their butt instead of walking (unfortunately I learned for puppies it might mean they have worms).

 2. Snacks are to puppies as binkies are to babies: their greatest “want!”

 3. Much like little kids, you can buy a puppy a fancy toy and he/she will like it but give them an empty box and they would probably have just as much fun for “free.”

 4. You simply cannot give either too much attention!

 5. Boys are hard to potty train…..period!

Have a great weeek!
