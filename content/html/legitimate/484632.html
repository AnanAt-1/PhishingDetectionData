Parc de conservation du Mont-Saint-Bruno

Description | Reviews (0)

Advertisements

Trails: Mont-Saint-Bruno provincial park offers 25 km of hiking trails on one of the Monteregian hills that rise abruptly above the plains of the St Lawrence. The trails circle four hill-top lakes.

Access fee: Park entry pass

Nearest town: Saint-Bruno-de-Montarville, QC

Trail map: Available at the centre and on the park's website

Website: Parc du Mont-Saint-Bruno

Points of interest: Nature interpretation centre is located in an old flour mill

Other activities: Alpine skiing, cross-country skiing

Lodging: Montreal has many choices of accommodation

Camping: Not available in the park

Tourism info: Free 130-page guide booklet to Montérégie available from Tourisme Montérégie has listings of accommodations (rated 1 to 5 stars) and attractions.

Getting to the trails: Mont-Saint-Bruno park is located about 25 km east of Montreal. From Montreal take Hwy 20 through the Lafontaine tunnel, continue for about 11 km then take Exit 102 and go south on chemin des Vingt-Cinq to the park. Bus 99 from Longueuil Metro stops about a half-hour walk away from the park.

Interactive map: Parc du Mont Saint-Bruno

Weather forecast: St-Bruno
