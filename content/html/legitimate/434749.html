Ek Tha Tiger Create New Record To Earn Rs.100 crore Fastest

Ek Tha Tiger is a 2012 Bollywood romantic action thriller film starring Salman Khan and Katrina Kaif in the lead roles. Produced by Aditya Chopra and directed by Kabir Khan, the film marks the third collaboration of Kabir Khan with Yash Raj Films after Kabul Express (2006) and New York (2009). It is also Salman's first film with the company. The music for the film has been composed by Sohail Sen, along with a guest composition by duo Sajid-Wajid.
The pre-production for Ek Tha Tiger started in the second half of 2010, after the script had been completed. The principal photography commenced on September 2011 and ended in June 2012. The film had been shot in as many as five countries across three continents. Initially scheduled for a release on June 2012, the film faced delays and opened up on August 15, 2012, coinciding with the Independence day of India.Ek tha tiger has crossed INR100 crore (US$18.1 million) nett in India,which is Salman Khan's fourth film in INR 100 crore club. Ek Tha Tiger has since emerged as one of the highest grossing films of all time in Bollywood.

Hollywood Actor Joseph Leonard Gorden

Hollywood Actor Joseph Leonard Gordon-Levitt (born February 17, 1981) is an American actor whose career as both a child and adult has included television series and theatrical films. He is known for his roles in the 2009 indie (500) Days of Summer (for which he earned a Golden Globe nomination) and the 2010 science fiction film Inception, and for starring in the 2011 drama 50/50. Beginning in commercials as a young child, he made his film debut in 1992's Beethoven. Gordon-Levitt subsequently co-starred in the television sitcom 3rd Rock from the Sun (1996–2001) as the young Tommy Solomon. After a hiatus during which he attended Columbia University, Gordon-Levitt left television for film acting, appearing in various independent films, beginning with the 2001 film Manic, followed by critically acclaimed performances in 2004's Mysterious Skin, 2005's Brick, and 2007's The Lookout. He runs an online collaborative production company titled HitRECord.

Famous Actors Ben Bass

Birth Name: Benjamin Langer Bass
Date of Birth: August 14, 1968
Birth Place: Baltimore, Maryland

Famous Actors Ben Bass Biography
Born in Baltimore, Maryland, Bass studied acting at The London Academy of Music and Dramatic Art, where he appeared in numerous stage productions including "Hamlet," "Othello," "Comedy of Errors," "The Provoked Wife" and "King Lear." He has also trained with Ecole Jacques Lacoq in Paris, The Vancouver Academy of Music and Second City.

Bass settled in Toronto in 1987, beginning his professional acting career with an appearance in the hit television series "Airwolf." He then went on to appear in "21 Jump Street," "Bordertown," "No More Mr. Fall Guy," "Friends to the End," "Andrea Martin: Together Again" and "Street Legal."

His theatre credits include leading roles in the Stratford Festival's "Cyrano De Bergerac," "In The Ring" and "Shakespeare Gym." He has also starred in "As You Like It," "A Midsummer Night's Dream," "Alexandra: The Last Empress," "Are You Lonesome Tonight?," as well as "Romeo & Juliet" at the Citadel Theatre.

Bass earned a nomination for the Vancouver Jesse Award for Outstanding Support Performance in a Musical, for his performance as Tim in the Vancouver Arts Club Theatre production of "Angry Housewives."

Famous Actors Ben Affleck Biography
This dark-haired, handsome young actor first appeared in front of the cameras a the young tyke visiting the Mayan ruins in the PBS production The Voyage of the Mimi. As a a strapping young man, Affleck began his feature career often cast as the teen heavy in features ranging from School Ties (1992), in which he was one of the anti-Semetic preppies, to Dazed and Confused (1993), wherein he was cast as the senior who terrorizes freshman students on the last day of school and eventually meets his match.

After appearing in the ensemble of Kevin Smith's Mallrats (1995), Affleck starred opposite Joey Lauren Adams in Smith's surprisingly affable romance Chasing Amy (1997). As his profile increased, the actor slipped into more conventional leading man roles that traded on his dark good looks: as a smooth-talking Korean War vet in Going All the Way (1997) and as an heroic astronaut in Armageddon (1998).

In 1996, Affleck and long-time best buddy Matt Damon made headlines in the trades when Miramax purchased the rights to Good Will Hunting, a film script they co-wrote about a troubled math genius. Gus Van Sant was tapped to direct with Damon in the leading role and Affleck portraying his best friend. The resulting feel-good film proved to be a box-office success and garnered several Oscar nominations. Affleck and Damon went on to win the Best Original Screenplay award to sign a development deal with Miramax for future projects. On the acting front, Affleck played one of the team selected to combat an oncoming meteor in the big-budgeted Armageddon and then offered an incisive comic turn as an egotistical actor in the period romantic comedy Shakespeare in Love (both 1998).

Continuing his string of work, Affleck landed the role of a bartender amid the ensemble of hot players (e.g., Christina Ricci, Courtney Love, Paul Rudd, Jay Mohr) in 200 Cigarettes, reteamed with Damon as renegade angels in Kevin Smith's Dogma, and was paired with Sandra Bullock in the romantic comedy Forces of Nature (all lensed in 1998).
