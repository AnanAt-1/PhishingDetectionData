Missouri - Jackson County - Vacant / Not In Use
Jackson County Missouri (Vacant / Not In Use) has 50 places on the National Register of Historic Places including 2 places of National significance and 2 places of Statewide significance. Significant places include Blue Mills, Majors, Alexander, House, Emery, Bird and Thayer Building and Fire Department Headquarters; Fire station #2 and A.B.C. Storage and Van Company Building.

Several famous people are associated with these Jackson County historic places including Alexander Majors, John Wesley Sr. Jenkins and Frederick G. Bonfils.

Some of the country's most noteable architects helped create the Jackson County places including Van Brunt & Howe, Henry Van Brunt, Albert Turney, Rea & Lovitt Smith, John McKCknie, Frederick C. Gunn, Smith Charles A., Butler-Weaver Realty Company\, Hucke & Sexton and Thompson-Starrett Construction Co.. Prominent architectural styles found in Jackson Country are Late 19th And Early 20th Century American Movements, Late 19th And 20th Century Revivals and Colonial Revival.
