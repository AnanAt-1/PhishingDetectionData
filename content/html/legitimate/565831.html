Proposal for a Documentary Film

Proposal for a documentary film on massive job losses in Korea and the homeless, unemployed men who abandoned their families, dropped out of society, and live in the train stations of Seoul.

Background: In 1998 economic failure led to the sudden loss of employment for many thousands of Koreans. International Monetary Fund (IMF) demands for economic austerity led to the chaebols (family-owned conglomerates) illegally firings, while thousands of small businesses simply collapsed. Families soon exhausted their savings, there were no unemployment benefits, and other social safeguards are under-funded or non-existent. Dozens of suicides occurred and in Seoul many hundreds of men simply deserted their families and began to live on the streets. The greatest number of drop-outs live around the city's train stations and remain a regular, disturbing feature of the urban landscape.

Format: Begin with establishing shots of stations and men, including views of sleeping and eating arrangements. Accompanying narration provides background over a collage of media reports on the economic crisis, IMF demands, and government and labor union responses. The narration includes simple statistics, ties events to the broader Asian crisis, and notes the scale and suddenness of unemployment as well as the severe reactions, including suicides. This leads to interviews with jobless men. Questions will include why they chose this alternative and who they blame (government, IMF, chaebols). This is followed by the crisis responses of government and charitable organizations. Further interviews with homeless men about relations with their families are interspersed with interviews with wives, older children, and former workmates or supervisors. The film concludes with questions about the future: is there any hope of employment, retraining, or immigration or have they given up hope altogether?
