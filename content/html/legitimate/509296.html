GARY S. PAXTON - WILLIE WYNN - LACY J. DALTON

GARY S. PAXTON multi-faceted composer, producer, performer, who produced hits for The Association, and Tommy Roe, penned the gospel hit “He Was There All The TIme,” also sang and recorded the 1960, #1 hit, “Alley Oop.”

WILLIE WYNN beloved tenor with the OakRidge Boys 1958-73, who also sang with the Statesmen, Willie Wynn & the Tennesseans, Sweetwater, and is a member of the Southern Gospel Music Hall of Fame.

LACY J. DALTON creative singer / song writer who gave the world the classic ode to Nashville tunesmiths, “16th Avenue,” has shared the stage with everyone from Willie Nelson to the Greatful Dead.

ED ENOCH - DANNY & THE JUNIORS - JEANNIE SEELY

ED ENOCH member of the Gospel Music Hall of Fame, veteran lead / baritone singer for, and later, owner / manager of, the iconic Stamps Quartet, having a long-time association with the Elvis organization.

DANNY & THE JUNIORS the great 50s rock n’ roll pioneer group that brought the planet such memorable songs as the #1 chart topper, “At The Hop,” the classic,“Rock And Roll Is Here To Stay,” and “Twistin’ USA”.

JEANNIE SEELY country singer / songwriter, Grammy award-winner, and long-time member of the Grand Ole Opry known for many hit singles and for duets with, Jack Greene.

DONNIE SUMNER - THE ANGELS - TOMMY FAIRCHILD

DONNIE SUMNER singer, keyboardist, award-winning composer and arranger best known for his his composition, “The Night Before Easter,” and for his work with the Stamps Quartet, Voice and the Elvis organization.

THE ANGELS one of rock and roll’s favorite girl groups who created a national sensation with their #1 smash hit, “My Boyfriend’s Back,” and charted other hits like, Thank You And Goodnight” “Til.” and “Cry Baby Cry.”

TOMMY FAIRCHILD award-winning keyboardist and arranger with the Oak Ridge Boys 1958-1971 and the Blackwood Brothers 1971-1989, followed by some two decades with various Branson theaters.

TOMMY ROE - THE COURIERS - RON ROSMAN

TOMMY ROE multi-talented rock and pop singer, song writer, and musician who toured with the Beatles and is known for such # 1 Billboard chart-topping smash hits as “Sheila” and “Dizzy.”

THE COURIERS with three Dove Awards to its credit, this nationally and internationally known gospel group that originated in the 1950s has logged over four million miles touring.

RON ROSMAN keyboardist and vocalist during the 60s and 70s with rock group Tommy James and the Shondells charting such Top 5 hits as, “Mony, Mony,” “Crimson And Clover,” and “Crystal Blue Persuasion.”

BILL BAIZE - CARL GIAMMARESE - LOU CHRISTIE

BILL BAIZE singer, evangelist, songwriter with such groups as the Smitty Gatlin Trio, Prophets, and as “sky-high” tenor with J.D. Sumner and the Stamps Quartet during the group’s Elvis years.

CARL GIAMMARESE versatile musician, singer / song writer, lead guitarist, vocalist and founding member of the Buckinghams know for the meteoric 1967 #1 hit, “Kind of a Drag. “ followed by two more Top 10 hits that year.

LOU CHRISTIE dynamic pop vocalist know for his distinctive voice. First #1 hit in 1966 with “Lightening Strikes,” Among other hits, “Rhap-sody in the Rain,” and “I’m Gonna Make You Mine.”

ANN DOWNING

ANN DOWNING started her music career with the legendary Speer Family, later co-founded the famous Downings, received a Dove Award for Female Vocalist of the year, and is a member of the Gospel Music Hall of Fame.

On March 28th, 1993 Ernie Couch & Revival stepped onto the stage of a packed Nashville auditorium filled with over a thousand rowdy, cheering well-wishers to present the group’s inaugural concert. So, began a musical journey of hundreds-of-thousands of miles, and twenty years of life, presenting the “good news” through music to audiences coast-to-coast, and around the globe. Twenty Tour is a twentieth anniversary project on which Ernie and Jason are joined by over twenty special guests, from a broad spectrum of musical backgrounds, who have graciously given of their time and musical abilities to join in celebration of this extraordinary journey.

Tid-Bits about project guests...

- Born, Larry Wayne Stevens, in Coffeyville, Kansas, Gary S. Paxton has written and co-written over 2,000 songs, and first came to national attention as half of the early rock-n-roll duo of Skip & Flip.

- Within a year of co-founding the popular Downings, Ann Downing won the gospel music industry’s highest honor, a Dove Award, for Female Vocalist of the Year, and in the early 70s became one of the first artists to record an Ernie Couch composition.

SPECIAL GUEST - JAMES EARL JONES

James Earl Jones was born on January 17, 1931, in Arkabutla, Mississippi. As a child, Jones developed a sever stutter, which left him extremely self-conscious and shy around other children. He refused to speak in school until a teacher helped him out of his silence during his high school years. He went to the University of Michigan to study medicine, but soon discovered acting. After college, he served in the military during the Korean War. Following his service he moved to New York City to study at the American Theatre Wing, and found a job as a janitor to make ends meet. He went on to star in a long list of highly successful plays and movies, becoming widely known as the voice of Darth Vader in the Star Wars film franchise. Jones won Tony Awards for his performances in The Great White Hope and Fences, and garnered an honorary Academy Award in 2011. James Earl Jones appears on Hail, Hail Immanuel courtsey of Array Technologies, Olympia, Washington. Learn more about his New Testament project at... www.arraytechnologies.com

SPECIAL GUEST - PATTI PAGE

Patti Page, born Clara Ann Fowler in Claremore, OK, received her stage name while working on a Tulsa radio program sponsored by a local dairy, the Page Milk Company. Through the years, Patti Page, "The Singing Rage," has sold over one hundred million records, making her one of the top selling female artists of all time. She has garnered over one hundred hits, fifteen gold records, and four gold albums. As to her role in the history of recording, it should be noted that Patti Page, combined with the engineering and production skills of Mitch Miller, revolutionized the industry by being the first pop singer to overdub her own vocals. This event created a whole new dimension in sound recording. During the 1950s and 1960s she became the first and only female singer to have her own program on, what was at the time, all three of the major national television networks (ABC, NBC, and CBS).

Tid-Bit - When asked by Ernie about his earlist musical memory, Bev Shea shared the following. He said that when he was just a "pre-schooler" two musical evangelist came to his father's church in Canada for a series of meetings. The singers were staying with the Shea family at the church parsonage. One evening following the service, the guest singers said that they wanted to teach the Sheas a "brand new" song hot off the press. Bev said he recalled everyone gathering around the piano in the "parlor," as they were taught this new song. The title of the song was, "The Old Rugged Cross." The year was 1913. Even the classic hymns of the Church were "brand new" at some point in time!

Boots was born Homer Louis Randolph, III in Paducah, KY, and attended the Cinncinati Music Conservitory. His 1961 smash hit, "Yakety Sax," propelled Boots to national attention. T A K I N G XF L I G H T

SPECIAL GUEST - JAKE HESS

When Elvis was asked, early in his career, who his favorite singer was, he quickly replied, "Jake Hess." Jake garnered fans both nationally and internationally with his uniquie vocals punchated with "eye-wallin, finger jabbing" body language. Hovie Lister often introduced Jake by referring to him as being, "often immitated, but never duplicated," and such was the case. Jake is best remembered for his years with the Statesmen Quartet, his formation of the Imperials, work with the Master V, and for his part in the successful Gaither Homecoming video series. Jake had a great personality and sense of humor. Following the first lay down of his track, when recording, "He's My Friend," for EC&R's Taking Flight project, he was asked if he wanted to take another run at his part. With a big grim, Jake replied, "Nope. It might just get worse!" With Jake there was no, "worse." He was definately a singer's, singer.
