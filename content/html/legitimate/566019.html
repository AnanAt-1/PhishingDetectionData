    Restoration Center of Greater Lowell to open in 2026

    LOWELL — By 2026, the roughed out and under construction office building off Technology Drive, will be transformed into the Restoration Center of Greater Lowell, the first-of-its-kind crisis diversion program in Massachusetts.
    • A former office park building on Technology Drive will be transformed into the Restoration Center of Greater Lowell by the spring of 2026, through a partnership between Vinfen and Spectrum Health Systems, which will offer walk-in, outpatient and in-patient services for people with mental health and addiction issues. From left, Sheriff Peter Koutoujian, Vinfen President and CEO Jean Yang and Spectrum President and CEO Kurt Isaacson. (Melanie Gilbert/Lowell Sun)
    • The behavioral health provider Vinfen has identified an office park building on Technology Drive in Lowell as a space for the Restoration Center. The center would divert people with mental health and substance use disorders from an incarceration and hospital model to inpatient behavioral care. (Melanie Gilbert/Lowell Sun)
    • A former office park building on Technology Drive will be transformed into the Restoration Center of Greater Lowell by the spring of 2026. A board in the 27,000-square-foot under-construction space shows the schematic for a sober support service area with 10 beds, one of many areas in the center's design. The partnership between Vinfen and Spectrum Health Systems will offer walk-in, outpatient and in-patient services for people with mental health and addiction issues. (Melanie Gilbert/Lowell Sun)
    • A former office park building on Technology Drive will be transformed into the Restoration Center of Greater Lowell by the spring of 2026. A board in the 27,000-square-foot under-construction space shows the schematic for a living room area, one of many areas in the center's design. The partnership between Vinfen and Spectrum Health Systems will offer walk-in, outpatient and in-patient services for people with mental health and addiction issues. (Melanie Gilbert/Lowell Sun)
    • The community behavioral health center for the nonprofit provider Vinfen is located at 391 Varnum Ave. in Lowell’s Pawtucketville neighborhood. (Melanie Gilbert/Lowell Sun)
