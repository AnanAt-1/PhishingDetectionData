THE 1743 SOCIETY

Responding to a need for educated men in the Middle Colonies in the 18th century, the Reverend Dr. Francis Alison opened an academy near New London, PA. The first class of 12 young men--a class which included three Signers of the Declaration of Independence, George Read, Thomas McKean and James Smith, and one signer of the Constitution, Read--met in the home of the Rev. Alison in 1743.

The academy later relocated to Newark, where it evolved into the University of Delaware, one of the nation's leading public institutions of higher learning.

The University's 1743 Society, an annual giving association, pays tribute to the institution's origins.

By making a gift of a least $100, University benefactors can become part of the tradition of higher education excellence. The 1743 Society recognizes the following giving levels:

  • Fellows: $500–$999

  • Sponsors: $250–$499

  • Members: $100–$249

Listed are 1743 Society donors who have given at the level of Sponsor or above.

For additional information about the 1743 Society, contact Catherine E.C. Sullivan, Director of Annual Giving, at (302)831-2104 or at annualgiving@udel.edu.
