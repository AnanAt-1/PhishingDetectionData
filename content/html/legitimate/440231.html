Francois Lefebvre-Duplessis-Faber, chevalier de Saint Louis: (abt.1665 - 1712)
    He was the son of Pierre Lefebvre & Marguerite Rassaoc, born in France. Francois married Marie-Madeleine Chorel de Saint-Romain (daughter of Francois Chorel de Saint-Romain & Marie-Anne Aubuchon) at Champlain, Quebec in 1689 and their children were: Francois-Antoine (1689-1733), Marie-Madeleine (bapt.1691), Louise-Madeleine (bapt.1694), Jacquette-Perinne (bapt.1695), Pierre (bapt.1696) & Anne-Charlotte (bapt.1699).
     Francois began his military career about 1671, arriving in New France in 1686 with the "Troupes de la Marine.

Francois-Antoine Lefebvre-Duplessis-Faber-Regnard: (1689 - 1733)
     He was the son of Francois Lefebvre-Duplessis-Faber & Marie-Madeleine Chorel de Saint-Romain, born in Montreal. His father (Francois I) came to New France in 1687 with the "troupes de la Marine". Francois II married 1st.to Marie-Genevieve-Catherine Pelletier in 1713 at Montreal and married 2nd.to Marie-Madeleine Coulon de Villiers (daughter of Nicolas Coulon de Villiers & Angelic Jarret) at St.Joseph des Illinois in 1728 (Marie Madeleine also married Claude Marin in 1737 & Damours de Clignancourt).
     Francois-Antoine was the commander of Green Bay and associated with Nicolas Sarrazin, Rene Bourassa, Jacques Gamelin & Ignace Gamelin in the fur trade. In 1733 as the French Commander at that same post he was killed by the Sauk at their village in 1733.

Pierre Lefebvre: (abt.1696 - ?)
     He was the son of Francois Lefebvre-Duplessis-Faber & Marie-Madeleine Chorel de Saint-Romain, born in Quebec. Pierre served in the French military in Louisiana.

Charles-Denis Duplessis-Faber-Regnard, Sieur de Morampont: (1704 - ?)
     He was the son of George Duplessis-Regnard & Marie Leroy, born in Quebec. Charles-Denis married Genevieve-Elizabeth Guillimin at Quebec in 1742. He was at Michilimackinac in the 1740's.

Joseph-Francois Lefebvre: (abt.1674 - ?)
     He was the son of Pierre Lefebvre & Marie-Marguerite Gagne and married Marie-Anne Testard. The family apparently moved to Detroit about 1717, where their youngest children (Eusache & Jacques) were born in 1718 & 1719.

Louis Lefebvre dit Duchouquet: (
     He was the son of Jean Lefebvre & Nicole Leroux and married Angelique Perthuis (daughter of Pierre Perthuis & Claude-Damise) in 1700. They had sons: Louis-Joseph, Pierre & Joseph.
     Louis was hiring voyageurs for Detroit in 1715.

Louis-Alexis Lefebvre: (1703 - abt.1769)
     He was the son of Gabriel-Nicolas Lefebvre dit Lataille & Louise Duclos. Louis-Alexis married Ursule Duboise dit Brisebois in 1734.
     He was a voyageur in the west in 1722.

Pierre Lefebvre: (1707 - ?)
    He was the son of Gabriel-Nicolas Lefebvre dit Lataille & Louise Duclos. Pierre married 1st.to Genevieve de Trepagny in 1733 and married 2nd.to Marie-Anne Paplau dit Perigny in 1760.
     Pierre descends from the Lefebvre family (along with brother Louis-Alexis above), associated with the Lataille, Villemure, du Sablon & Despins family lines. He journeyed to Michilimackinac - 1736/37.

Noel Lefebvre dit Lasiseraye: (1700 - ?)
     He was the son of Michel Lefebvre dit Lasiseraye & Catherine Trottier and married Marie-Anne Gervais in 1722. In 1720 he was hired by Jean Magdelaine dit La Douceur for the "pays d'en haut" and in 1737 he was hired to go to Michilimackinac. In 1725 Pierre & Francois Sincerny were hired by Claude Caron & Philippe Leduc for Michilimackinac.
