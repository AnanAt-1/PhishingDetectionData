The Giant's Camp has been gone for a number of years now and discussing the issues is probably not in anyone's interest. However, the record should be correct if for nothing else than for posterity. I knew Jackie Leonard for many years and have only very fond memories of her and her generosity. No one and I mean no one every left the restaurant hungry...money or not. She fed many people who were needy and never asked anything in return.There was always a smile and a friendly atmosphere for all. She was a wonderful,compassionate,and caring person who I miss terribly. She never claimed to be the owner of the restaurant and often expressed her thoughtful feeling for Mrs Jeanie Tomaini.In all the years I went there I never saw Tina Tomaini. I should mention that a large picture of the "Giant" Mr. Al Tomaini hung in the establishment the entire time Mrs. Leonard operated it. I never heard any one speak bad of Mrs. Leonard and witnessed a large funeral when she passed. Whatever happened to the restaurant after her death did not involve JACKIE Leonard in any way. I know all her employees who were there for years remember her with love...Ms Ingrahm, Linda, Nish...etc . I am happy the community is coming together to honor the "Giant" and the Boot and hopefully Jackie Leonard.

I enjoyed going to Giant's Camp very much since I grew up not 5 miles away. My first few years of life were right next door (in the early 50's). I can remember all the animal exhibits and the great time I use to have meeting friends for lunch there. I haven't been there for over 20 years now, since I now live in VA. I met the giant as a little girl, but really can't remember him. My mom spoke well of him. But I can remember the atmosphere as always feeling cozy though there was a hussle & bussle of people coming and going. I still have a picture of me next to the giant's boots and statue taken in 1985. I am sorry to hear it has closed down. I would like to have visited again when I come for a visit.
