Bert and family in Napoleon, Ohio in 1933 and 1942

OBITUARY:

Bert C. Fruchey, 76, Napoleon, died early Saturday morning, October 1, 1977 at his home. He was born September 29, 1901, in Columbus Grove, the son of John and Hermie (Reppert) Fruchey. The deceased was owner and operator of the former Fruchey's Clover Farm Grocery Store. He was employed by Chief Supermarkets as manager of the Southside store until he retired in 1970. He was a member of St. paul United Methodist church, Napoleon., He was a life member of the Napoleon Elks Lodge #929. He was a 30 year member and chaplin of the Napoleon volumteer Fire Department. Surviving are his wife, Lucille (shock) Fruchey; one son, John E. Fruchey Napoleon; one daughter, Mrs. Harold (Margie) Blue, Napoleon; one brother, Charles, Parma; four grandchildren; and two great-grandchildren. Services were held today (Monday) at 2:00 p.m. at Walker Mortuary, Napoleon. Rev. Norman Newman officiated. Sunday evening, Memorial Elk services wre held at the mortuary. Interment was made in Truro Cemetery, Columbus Grove. The family suggests that memorials may be made to the donors choice. This article found in the Northwest Signal in Napoleon, October 3, 1977.
