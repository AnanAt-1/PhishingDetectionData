Our earliest evidence of gambling was discovered in Ancient China in the form of tiles which appeared to have been used for a lottery type of gambling. From around 200 BC, there are ancient versions of keno slips as a form of lottery to fund various works.

Oldest dice are dated to be from 3000 BC, and they were found in an Egyptian tomb. However, the first time the dice were mentioned in the history of gambling was in the writings of Sophocles from 500 BC. Gambling was forbidden in the ancient city of Rome and the people who were caught playing were punished by having to pay four times the amount wagered. Because of this, Romans invented the first gambling chips, pretending to the authority to be playing only for the chips.

The first playing cards appeared in the 9th century in China, but the rules and the games aren’t known, unfortunately. Most sources believe that these playing cards aren’t much like the playing cards we use today.
