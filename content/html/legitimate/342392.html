House of Commons - Bill C-344

February 18, 2002 - The House resumed from November 7, 2001 consideration of the motion that Bill C-344, an act to amend the Contraventions Act and the Controlled Drugs and Substances Act (marijuana), be read the second time and referred to a committee.

  • Ken Epp (Elk Island, Canadian Alliance)
  • Bernard Bigras (Rosemont--Petite-Patrie, BQ)
  • Chuck Strahl (Fraser Valley, PC/DR)
  • Mr. Svend Robinson (Burnaby--Douglas, NDP)
  • Mr. John Maloney (Erie--Lincoln, Lib.)

November 7, 2001

The Bill was read the second time and referred to a committee.

  • Keith Martin (Esquimalt--Juan de Fuca, Canadian Alliance)
  • Jeannot Castonguay (Parliamentary Secretary to the Minister of Health, Lib.)
  • Mr. Réal Ménard (Hochelaga--Maisonneuve, BQ)
  • Ms. Libby Davies (Vancouver East, NDP)
  • Mr. Peter MacKay (Pictou--Antigonish--Guysborough, PC/DR)
  • Ms. Judy Sgro (York West, Lib.)

May 4, 2001

Bill C-344, an act to amend the Contraventions Act and the Controlled Drug Substances Act (marijuana) to decriminalize small amounts of cannabis, was introduced in Parliament.
