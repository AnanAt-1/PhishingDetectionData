Bad Credit Credit Cards

Bad credit credit cards typically have higher interest rates but can certainly still be a very useful way for those with less than stellar credit histories to rebuild their credit score.

Credit cards for bad credit typically come in 2 different varieties: secured and unsecured. A secured credit card functions much like a debit card in that it is backed or “secured” by money that the cardholder puts into an account. An unsecured credit card is without this backing of funds deposited by the cardholder and has the closest similarity to a traditional credit card. Both types of bad credit credit cards play an important role in helping those with less than perfect credit take the necessary steps to regain a good credit score.
