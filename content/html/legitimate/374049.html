Building Blocks – Accenture Tower

Building Blocks – Accenture Tower

    Frank Jossi//October 27, 2011//

Listen to this article

Address: 333 S. Seventh St., Minneapolis

Website: Click Here

Year built: 1987

Square feet for lease: 625,072

Occupancy rate: 80 percent office

Lease costs: $10 to $20 per square foot, net

Owner: California State Teachers’ Retirement System (CALSTRS)

Property description: The 31-story tower was designed by Kohn Pedersen Fox and built by Golden Valley-based Mortenson Co. in 1987. The tower is flanked by two wings offering fewer floors. The façade is a mixture of white, dark gray, light gray and granite, with a dark brown square crown on the middle tower. Floor plates range from 13,200 to 23,500 square feet.

On the Third Avenue South side of the building there’s an open green area that CB Richard Ellis bought from Opus over the summer. Accenture also has a conference center, a fitness center (free to tenants), a 630-stall underground ramp and a concierge service.

The two-story atrium lobby has been partly remodeled and brightened a bit with new lighting. The renovation, still under way, included better signs for people navigating the skyways connecting to the Campbell Mithun Tower and the 701 Building.

Tenant mix: Accenture, the largest tenant, takes up 161,000 square feet. Other tenants are mainly professional services: the law firm Hinshaw & Culbertson, the engineering firm Michaud Cooley Erickson, the public relations firm Public Affairs Co. and the Business Journal. Himle Horner is moving in from Bloomington soon, too, said Bob Traeger, general manager of Accenture Tower for CBRE. Black Bamboo, a restaurant, is vacating the building, leaving a retail opening.

Intriguing tidbits: Accenture has earned many awards, among them the 2010 Toby for outstanding building of the year from the Building Owners & Managers Association (BOMA). On the sustainability front, it has been compliant with Energy Star since 2001 and has earned a LEED silver from the U.S. Green Building Council, the only multitenant office to earn that distinction in downtown Minneapolis, Traeger said.

At the suggestion of CALSTRS, Accenture instituted daytime cleaning that allows CBRE to dim the lights at night, saving $75,000 to $80,000 annually. Cleaning crews start early — at 5 a.m. — and work until nearly 2 p.m.

“We were the beta site in the CALSTRS portfolio, and now they’re going to roll this out to other buildings,” Traeger said. “It’s nice in that you get to know your cleaners; you can ask them to do this thing or that thing.”

After recently adding the conference and fitness centers, Accenture is renovating the lobby and an area just outside of it on Third Avenue South.

A skyway is also under construction between Accenture and the Ameriprise Financial Center on Third Avenue South.
