Iowa MLB Players | Research by Baseball Almanac

The University of Iowa baseball program started in 1890. Maury Kent was the first former Hawkeye to make it to the Major League level. Names in bold print have been inducted into the University of Iowa Athletics Hall of Fame.

Did you know that there were twenty-nine former University of Iowa ballplayers, and four additional students (Cap Anson, Bill McWilliams, Joe Lutz, and Bill Burbach) who did not play baseball for the team, that made it to the show? Send updates to Baseball Almanac.

Other notable alumni who have attended the University of Iowa include: Tom Brokaw (NBC Nightly News), George Gallup (Gallup Poll Founder), John Irving (Author), Ashton Kutcher (Actor), James Van Allen (Physicist), Andre Tippett (NFL Hall of Fame), Gene Wilder (Actor), Tennessee Williams (Playwright).

The University of Iowa chose its school colors in 1887, when fifty seniors met on the steps of the Old Capitol. When all was said and done, their colors became Old Gold, the color of Iowa's corn fields, and Black, the color of the soil itself.
