Erasure set to unveil Tomorrow’s World today

IT’S fitting that Erasure – the electro duo who formed in 1985 should return to the musical fray, when all things ’80s-like are back in vogue.

Lifestyle
Wales Online
19:15, 21 Mar 2013Updated 23:45, 21 Mar 2013

IT’S fitting that Erasure – the electro duo who formed in 1985 should return to the musical fray, when all things ’80s-like are back in vogue.

One of the most successful and globally adored partnerships in pop, they celebrate more than a quarter of a decade together with a new album, Tomorrow’s World, and an autumn UK tour.

‌

After selling 25 million albums and scoring more than 30 hit singles, Vince Clarke and Andy Bell are back with their 14th studio album – brim full of warm-blooded electro-pop and emotionally charged electro-soul that sounds as exciting as their younger chart acolytes.

‌

With digital melodies and electronic beats more dominant than ever, Erasure’s timeless anthems of hedonism and heartache look set to strike a universal chord once more.

Returning from a four-year sabbatical, Andy and Vince enlisted the services of rising British electro-pop star Vincent ‘Frankmusik’ Frank to produce the album.

This one-man-band’s glittering portfolio of remixes and collaborations includes Lady Gaga, Pet Shop Boys and Ellie Goulding. Currently based in LA, where he has his own studio, Frank was born in 1985 – ironically the year that Erasure formed.

“We chose Frank because he is someone who has a different approach to the music, who is sympathetic to synthesizers, and incredibly keen,” explains Vince Clarke. “He told me the music scene in London was just too small for him, so he made a conscious decision and moved to LA, with nothing! He just really likes that American pop thing – that whole Lady Gaga, full-on wall of sound.”

In the four years since their last album The Light At The End Of The World, Erasure have been busy.

Vince reunited with Alison Moyet to reactivate their legendary synth-soul duo Yazoo, rebuilt his studio at his house in Maine, and picked up an Outstanding Song Collection prize at the Ivor Novello awards in recognition of his 30 years as a songwriter. Meanwhile, Andy released album Non-Stop and lent his powerhousevoice to the TV reality show Pop Star To Opera Star.

‌

But even in their extended gap year, Erasure were never far from the charts, releasing their double retrospective Total Pop! – The First 40 Hits in 2009.

Erasure may have been synth-pop pioneers in the 1980s and 1990s, but they’ve never rested on their laurels. Unlike many of their former chart peers, Andy and Vince have consistently avoided the creative death knell of nostalgia tours. Both are huge fans of contemporary electronic music and club sounds.

“In the 1980s and early 1990s, if you counted the number of purely electronic bands in the charts, there wasn’t that much really,” Vince points out. “Now it’s like 90 per cent! Who would have thought that? This whole new electronic scene is really exciting, and I don’t think anybody sounds like the music from back then, just because they are using synthesizers.

“Erasure have never been about nostalgia. We are not thinking about records we made in the 1980s, even if other people are. We are thinking about the next record – and future records.”

Erasure play St David’s Hall, Cardiff on Sunday, October 30. Tickets are £30 from 029 2087 8444 or via the website www.stdavidshallcardiff.co.uk
