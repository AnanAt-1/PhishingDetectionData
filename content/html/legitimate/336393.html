Willie Davis, chief of staff to former Birmingham Mayor Richard Arrington, dies

  • Updated: Jun. 29, 2011, 6:27 p.m.
  • |Published: Jun. 29, 2011, 5:27 p.m.
By
  • Sherrel Stewart -- The Birmingham News

BIRMINGHAM, Alabama -- Willie Davis, the longtime chief of staff for former Birmingham Mayor Richard Arrington, has died, family friends say.

Former Jefferson County Commissioner Jeff Germany said Davis' family members called him this morning to tell him about his friend's death while Davis was in Africa.

"I believe he was on a medical missions trip," Germany said. "He was a good man and a truly good friend.  If I ever needed someone to be there with me, it was Willie Davis."

Arrington said Davis was a success story.

"He was a dear friend of mine and was my first chief of staff when I was mayor," Arrington said. "Willie was a very able person. He came out of Loveman's Village and was a success story."

Davis also worked in the administration of former Washington, D.C., Mayor Marion Berry.

"He was bright, able, a very clear politician," Arrington said. "He was one of those that we're going to miss."

In addition, Arrington said Davis was active in his community, including the youth baseball league.

News staff writer Joseph D. Bryant contributed to this report.
