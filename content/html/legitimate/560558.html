Lima Municipal Court

Attention: Lima Municipal Court is implementing a new Case Management System.  Please be patient as we work through this process over the next few months.

re:Search Ohio (registration required) is now available to search case data. Limited data is available at this time as we await additional software development.
  • New cases entered into the new software are available.
  • Most cases filed prior to 5/5/2025 are unavailable.
  • Charges and related data, Case Financials, Case Dispositions/Judgments are currently unavailable, but we are working with our software vendor to make this data available.
  • Case documents are unavailable on re:Search at this time.  This is something we hope to offer in the future.
  • For more information on how to use re:Search, please reference the User Guide.
Online Case Payments remains offline at this time. We are working diligently to get this working as soon as possible.

Contact the Court at (419) 221-5275 if you have any questions.

Municipal Court Jurisdiction

The Lima Municipal Court handles traffic, criminal, and civil matters originating in Allen County, Ohio.

At the Court House

Parking is located across Union Street between the Allen County Health Department and the bus station.  A parking meter is located at the west side of these parking lots.

  • WARNING: If your ticket indicates “Distracted Driving” (or similar language) in the remarks section, you must either pay an additional, mandatory fine of $100.00 online now or submit written evidence to the Clerk’s Office that you have completed a distracted driving safety course as defined in R.C. 4511.991, within thirty (30) days from the date you received your ticket. This one to two-hours online course is available at: www.drivertraining.ohio.gov Opens a New Window. . Failure to pay the additional fine or submit written evidence of completing the distracted driving safety course may result in a forfeiture of your driving privileges.  If you have any questions, please contact the Clerk’s Office at (419) 221-5275.  The Clerk of Court has prepared a document titled Instructions for the Distracted Driving 101 Course to help with the process.
  • DRIVING WHILE TEXTING (FIRST OFFENSE): If you have been charged with a first offense driving while texting violation (R.C. 4511.204), you may submit written evidence to the Clerk’s Office that you have completed a distracted driving safety course, as defined in R.C. 4511.991, within ninety (90) days from the date you received your ticket to remove two points from your driver’s license record and receive a refund of the $50.00 fine. (NOTE: You will still have a conviction for this violation on your driving record and you must still pay court costs.). Please allow up to 30 days to receive any refund. This one to two-hours online course is available at: www.drivertraining.ohio.gov. If you have any questions, please contact the Clerk’s Office at (419) 221-5275. The Clerk of Court has prepared a document titled Instructions for the Distracted Driving 101 Course to help with the process.
  • Important Information Concerning Jury Fraud Opens a New Window. . Lima Municipal Court will never call to ask for your private information. If you have questions about this or receive this type of call, please call the Court at (419) 221-5275 and ask for Tiffany May, Court Administrator.
  • Effective June 1, 2017:
    If you are delinquent on paying your fines and/or court costs and you do not have a payment plan with our office that you are in compliance with, then your account may be turned over to the Bureau of Motor Vehicles for imposition of a license forfeiture and registration block. This is in addition to your account being turned over to Capital Recovery Systems, Inc. (the official collection agency for The Lima Municipal Court since January 1, 2014).
