August 7, 2007 - Elderly Couple Injured on Hwy-63

An elderly couple (ages 78 and 83) were injured when a vehicle driven by a 47-year-old Jefferson City man attempted to cross Highway 63 at Route CC. The couple was taken to University Hospital in Columbia with moderate injuries.

August 5, 2007 - Woman & Grandson Injured by Wayward Tire

A 54-year-old woman and her 13-year-old grandson hit a rock embankment on Highway 70 near the 120 mile marker when they left the roadway after swering to avoid a wayward tire that had crossed the median into their path.  The two were taken to University Hospital in Columbia, Missouri with moderate injuries.

August 3, 2007 - Extensive Damage in Crash at US-63 and MO-124

An accident occurred on August 3, 2007 at approximately 12:10 pm when a vehicle attempted to cross US Higway 63 from MO Hwy 124 and was struck by another vehicle.  The investigating officer indicated that the driver crossing US-63 failed to yield to the other vehicle already on the highway.  Both vehicles sustained extensive damage.  And both drivers were taken by amublance to University Hospital with moderate injuries.
