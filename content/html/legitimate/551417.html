Rabbis of Quebec, Canada

Introduction

The Jewish vital records of the province of Quebec, Canada, were kept by the synagogues on behalf of the civil authorities from 1841 (when Jewish record keeping was first instituted) until 1994 (when the new civil code eliminated confessional [religious] record keeping). These include the synagogues of Greater Montreal (all suburbs), Quebec City, and Sherbrooke. An alphabetical list of all synagogues, with locations and time span of records, is available.

The Jewish Genealogical Society of Montreal has indexed all surviving records from 1841 to 1942. There is a total of about 75,000 records of which approximately 5,000 are deaths. The remainder are births and marriages. The following is excerpted from the project introduction:

Rabbi I. L. Colton was the principal mohel in the Montreal Jewish community from 1917 to 1954.  His notebooks listed circumcisions, some births of females, and marriages (685) at which he officiated...While solely for events at which he officiated, Rabbi Colton's notes from 1943 to 1954 are the only source of indices to Jewish births for this period. Entries from Cantor Mendelson's notebooks are incomplete but include circumcisions and some adoptions from 1938 to 1975 inclusive. Carbon copies of a number of Mendelson's letters certifying births have also survived.  These have been indexed and photocopies, when existing, are also available to researchers.

For more information about this indexing project, please see the Jewish Genealogical Society of Montreal Web site.

In the indexing process, the officiating Rabbi was noted as well as the synagogue register in which the act was recorded. Thus, the database tracks the names and movements of all rabbis in Quebec for 100 years. The database was created and contributed by Ruth Diamond of Montreal.
