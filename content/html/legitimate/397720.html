Alexandre Bilodeau; Canada’s Newest Obsession

16 02 2010

Before I begin, there is a confession I must make.  Prior to yesterday, Alex Bilodeau wasn’t a name I was too familiar with.  And I don’t want to speak for us all, but I think many people know, deep down, that the same goes for them.  Going from an average Olympian (I mean as average as an amazing Olympian can get!) to becoming our nation’s hero must have been quite the experience for this superb skier.  Now if only I had been able to ask him about this in person, when I was less than two feet away from his face! (Please don’t mind my descriptions, I’m just trying to be as accurate as possible!)

Now you may or may not be wondering how I got so close to this superstar, and if you don’t want to know, I shall respect that and you can stop reading now.

Last night, after we received some tragic news about our general admission tickets that were going to be canceled and refunded for the Snowboard X event at Cypress, Jasmine and I decided we would brave the infamous line up and attempt to ride the zip line across Robson Square.  With ambitions of an early start, delayed a little, due to fatigue, we made it there around 11 am.  We pretty much had all day to kill (well until 4:30 pm, but that was ages away!)  One man who worked at the zip line came by, warning us that it would be a six to seven hour wait.  No he was not joking. He was not kidding either.  But seriously, it really couldn’t take that long, could it?

Line-ups, of which you cannot see the end, are deadly.  Minutes passed, hours passed and we waited.  Riding across Robson Street is not something that you could experience everyday.  This was a total once in a lifetime opportunity so we decided to wait it out.  In the queue, we met some pretty awesome people.  They were all very supportive, humorous (especially Amandah!) and fun to pass the time with.  As the end of the line came into view, a couple of our new friends went to go get something warm for them all to drink, and came back with hot chocolate for me and Jasmine as well! Their generosity was quite overwhelming and it just proves, there are some pretty sweet and kindhearted people out there in the world, and we were very fortunate to meet a bunch.

But before we could see the end of the line, one of the attendants walked by and pointed to a tent in the distance.  We were informed that the back of the head we were staring at, under the tent, belonged to none other than Alex Bilodeau!  Since it was ok to take turns leaving the line, I hurried down the path and around to where CBC was interviewing him.  A small crowed was gathered and some girl fans where taking photographs of the back of his head.  A cheer went up every time he turned around.  Finally when the interview was over, he waved and high fived lots of the fans.  My camera and I sort of chased him to the next interview in a tent near by.  As he exited that one, he almost walked into me.  We’ll maybe that sounds a little exaggerated, however, if I hadn’t moved out of his path, the security would have surely helped me out.

And that is how I got less then two feet away from the face of Alex Bilodeau, our nation’s pride and joy.   Bilodeau addressed the crowed from a stage, before he was ushered down a flight of stairs, which I believe lead to the Robson International Media Centre.  His words, tinted with a French-Canadian accent, expressed his feelings of happiness (of course!) however he was very humble about it.  He emphasized on the fact that his win was the first of Canada’s wins, and there would still be many more to come.  He thanked all Canadians for their support and promised that the party had just begun.  That evening, unfortunately, we had to leave the zip line queue after almost 6 hours of waiting (and getting almost to the front) to attend the victory ceremony at BC Place.

History was made as Alexandre Bilodeau was awarded the very first gold medal to be won by a Canadian athlete on home soil. It was an incredibly magical moment to be a part of, even though it meant forfeiting the zip line.  On the very bright side, if we had not been in the line, I would not have been less than two feet away from the face of the hero himself.

Anyhow, as a side note, since I realized what a celebrity Bilodeau has become in the last couple of days, I thought I would conduct a little experiment.  I had seen facebook statuses in which girls proclaimed their love for the new idol.  For a short while, I set my msn name as “ m r s .  [ a ]  b i l o d e a u ,” just for the fun of it.  Within moments, I had someone tell me I was cracking her up, while another friend claimed I was stealing her identity!  It was some pretty funny stuff. 😀

Now just incase your name happens to be Jany-Eve Saucier (yes, very wishful thinking at the moment), hun, you’re gorgeous! I’m not trying to take your spot or anything, it’s all innocent fun and I wish you all the best! 🙂

I’m sorry, but no one can deny it, a lot of Canadians have fallen head over heels for this new, young and talented hero.  My prediction is, for the next little while, Alex Bilodeau shall remain our national obsession.
