Graeme Batty and Kieran Gray win Bush Blues spots

By Country Championships by Mike Driscoll
Updated November 6 2012 - 3:09am, first published November 28 2011 - 10:27am
By Country Championships by Mike Driscoll
Updated November 6 2012 - 3:09am, first published November 28 2011 - 10:27am
FacebookTwitterWhatsappEmail
Illawarra rep cricketer Graeme Batty.

Illawarra skipper Graeme Batty and teenage Southern Zone opener Kieran Gray have been selected in the NSW Country side to contest the Australian Country Championships in Geelong in January.Oak Flats opener Gray makes his debut for the Bush Blues after blasting a century for Southern against Central Coast in the NSW Country Championships at Wagga Wagga.Keira captain-coach Batty led Illawarra to the NSW Country Championships final and has made the Bush Blues squad for the third straight year.Illawarra have reached the NSW Country Championships final for the second time in three years after beating Southern Zone and Riverina in their pool. The final against Western Zone was washed out last Sunday and is likely to be played in February.Despite missing out on the final, Newcastle again dominate the Bush Blues squad with four players, while Western Zone and Central North each have three representatives. The NSW Country squad for the January 1-12 national carnival is: Jeff Cook (capt, Central North), Ethan Bartlett (Riverina), Graeme Batty (Illawarra), Tim Cox (Western), Kieran Gray (Southern), Tom Groth (Central North), Kaine Harmsworth (Newcastle), Declan Hoare (Western), Mark Littlewood (Newcastle), Andrew Maher (Newcastle), Simon Moore (Newcastle), Brendan Rixon (Central North), Pat Rosser (Western).
