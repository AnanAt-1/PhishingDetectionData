Salem police: Pennsville man attempted to steal hoagie, potato salad, fried chicken and chips from supermarket

  • Updated: Sep. 30, 2011, 6:45 a.m.
  • |Published: Sep. 30, 2011, 5:45 a.m.
By
  • Today's Sunbeam
LAXTON

SALEM

— Police said a Pennsville man attempted to steal a number of picnic-variety fixings from a local food market here last week.

Gordon Laxton III, 40, of Meadow Road, allegedly entered the Incollingo’s Market here on East Broadway on Sept. 24 at 4:24 p.m., and proceeded to conceal potato salad, cold fried chicken, an Italian hoagie,  potato chips, and few other items under his coat, according to Salem City Police.

Laxton then allegedly exited the market without paying for the goods and was immediately arrested, police said.

The items taken from the market had a total value of $24.57, police said.

Laxton was processed by police and lodged in the Salem County Correctional Facility in default of bail, set at $10,000.
