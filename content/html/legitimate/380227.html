    2016 Copa América in the U.S.

    By Jack Bell
    5:26 p.m. |Update

    On Thursday, Sunil Gulati, the president of the U.S. Soccer Federation told the Associated Press that there are many issues still to be worked out before Copa América could be played in the U.S. in 2016.

    “While the idea of a centennial tournament with some of the best teams in the hemisphere is certainly intriguing, it is not something we have agreed to host or participate in at this time,” he said. “As Concacaf stated, there are still a number of discussions that need to take place with Conmebol. U.S. Soccer would also need to be involved in discussions about hosting the tournament. We’re looking forward to those discussions in the near future.”

    The United States may have failed in its bid to land either the 2018 or 2022 World Cup, but it will play host to perhaps the next-best thing: the Copa América in 2016.

    At a meeting in Buenos Aires, Argentina, on Wednesday, representatives from Conmebol, the South American confederation, agreed to hold the tournament in the United States to celebrate the confederation’s 100th anniversary.

    Its statement said (with translation help from Google):

    In July 2016, Conmebol will complete its first century of life. In order to commemorate this with the pomp it deserves, the association presidents and members of the executive committee have decided to have a tournament of teams across America. This competition, which will be called the Centennial Copa America, will take place in the United States and will bring together 16 teams: the 10 South American, plus 6 from  Concacaf, including Mexico and the United States and the four top-placed from the  Concacaf Gold Cup.

    There are few other details.

    The United States has played in three of the South American championship tournaments — in 1993, 1995 and 2007 — with its best finish, fourth place, coming in 1995. Mexico has been invited to the past eight tournaments.

    In some quarters, the idea of combining Conmebol with Concacaf, the confederation of North, Central American and Caribbean nations, has been floated as a way to strength both confederations. There are only 10 countries in Conmebol, which has been dominated by Brazil and Argentina. Brazil will host the 2014 World Cup, the first time the tournament has been played in South America since 1978 in Argentina. Concacaf has been dominated by Mexico and the U.S.

    The U.S. has hosted the Concacaf Gold Cup (as it will next summer) every two years since 1991 (co-hosting with Mexico in 1993 and 2003).

    Jamaica Beats U.S. in World Cup Qualifier, 2-1

    By Andrew Keh
    Marc Serota/Getty ImagesClint Dempsey of the United States battled for the ball with Jamaica’s Ryan Johnson.

    Friday night’s 2014 World Cup qualifier between the United States and Jamaica will be broadcast exclusively on beIN Sport — a new channel carried only on Dish Network, DirecTV, and, in certain markets, Comcast — forcing many fans, this reporter included, over to the local sports bar to catch the action.

    But if you’re stuck at home or in the office, here’s the next best thing: From our perch at the pub, we will be providing live updates on the game as it develops.

    Some context, then?

    Taking place in Kingston, Jamaica, this match represents the first leg of a home-and-home series that will wrap up next Tuesday in Columbus, Ohio. These two teams are tied atop Concacaf’s Group A with four points apiece (though the Americans have the edge through goal differential). The top two countries in each of the three qualifying groups will gain entry to the region’s final qualifying round.

    The United States has never lost to Jamaica, going 10-0-8, but a positive result feels anything but assured. Each of the four previous qualifiers played between these teams on Jamaican soil ended as ties.

    On top of that, some star power will be lacking on the American side. Landon Donovan and Michael Bradley, normally automatic selections on the team, are out with injuries. And Clint Dempsey, whose fraught transfer saga ended last week with an intra-London move from Fulham to Tottenham Hotspur of the English Premier League, may not be in peak shape.

    In all, it should make for a compelling night of soccer.

    Estadio Azteca: Into the Lion’s Den

    By Andrew Lewellen
    Alex Cruz/European Pressphoto AgencyThe United States last played a friendly match against Mexico in Estadio Azteca in 1984.

    On Wednesday, when the United States plays Mexico in a friendly in Mexico City, the Americans will try to earn their first victory on the home soil of their greatest rival: they are 0-23-1 in Mexico since the first game in 1937, a 7-2 drubbing. To win, they will need to beat Mexico in the Estadio Azteca, one of the most famed stadiums in the world: a 105,000-seat caldron perched at an elevation of 7,200 feet.

    The stadium hosted some epic soccer matches. In the 1970 World Cup, the stadium was the site of what has been called the Game of the Century, Italy’s 4-3 semifinal victory over West Germany. In the final, Brazil, which fielded Pelé, Gerson, Rivelino, Tostão and Carlos Alberto, beat Italy, 4-1, and staked its claim as the greatest team in soccer history.

    Futsal: U.S. Set for World Cup Qualifying

    By The New York Times
    U.S. SoccerCoach Keith Tozer, center, and the U.S. team during a recent exhibition in Guatemala.
     

    The day after the final of the European Championships, the United States opens World Cup qualifying in Guatemala City. Wait. Didn’t the U.S. just play a qualifier in Central America? True. These games involve the U.S. futsal team on the road to the FIFA Futsal World Cup in Thailand later this year.

    In the United States, indoor soccer reached the zenith of its popularity in the 1980s and early ’90s, when it was mostly played on ice hockey rinks covered with a thin carpet and jury-rigged goals carved into the glass and end boards. Not surprisingly, the United States put in strong showings in the first two Futsal World Cups, finishing third in 1989 and second in 1992.

    Futsal may be the de rigueur training method in many South American and European countries, but the five-on-five game, played on a field roughly the size of a basketball court with a smaller, weighted ball (think restricted-flight softball) is only now gaining a new generation of adherents in the United States. The idea is to develop close-ball skills, playing on the floor and not in the air, so players gain a better understanding of movement off the ball.

    New Home Jersey: Hoops ‘R’ U.S.

    By Jack Bell
    ′Images by NikeAlex Morgan, left, and Clint Dempsey in new U.S. national team uniforms, unveiled Monday.
    The new jerseys feature a light “tonal sash,” Nike said.
    The new women’s jersey creates “a uniquely feminine silhouette,” Nike said.

    If one color has been missing from recent iterations of the jersey worn by United States national team players — men and women — it is most certainly red.

    That oversight was remedied by Nike when it unveiled on Monday the new home shirts (shorts and socks, too) that will soon be worn by the national teams. In the “Hoops Edition,” the jerseys, above and at right, feature a body with red and white stripes. The color blue is reduced from dominant to complementary as the trim around the neck collar. And in continuing homage to what still remains as the seminal game in the history of the men’s national team — the 1950 World Cup win against England in Brazil — the jerseys have what Nike calls “a tonal sash to echo the same design” from the jerseys worn all those years ago.

    The women’s jersey is slightly more tailored than the men’s number while both versions will include the United States flag, the Stars and Stripes, inside the back of the neck. To complete the new home look, the players will wear blue shorts with white socks that have a blue band around the top (and addition cushioning on the sole).

    The men’s jersey will be available at retail beginning April 26; the women’s on July 1. The men will first wear the new shirts in their friendly international against Scotland in Jacksonville, Fla., on May 26; the women in their friendly international against China in Chester, Pa., on May 27.

    According to Nike, each of the new uniforms is made with an average of 13 recycled plastic bottles. The shorts are 100 percent Dri-FIT recycled polyester and the jersey has a minimum 96 percent Dri-FIT polyester. The company asserts that the new uniform is 23 percent lighter that previous numbers.

    Schedule for World Cup Qualifiers Released

    By Jack Bell
    Filip Horvat/Associated PressAgainst a foggy backdrop, U.S. players congratulated Edson Buddle (11) after his goal opened the scoring in Tuesday’s 3-2 win at Slovenia in an international friendly match.

    Last Saturday the United States learned the teams it would be playing in the next round of qualifying matches for the 2014 World Cup — Jamaica (which had already been seeded), Guatemala, and Antigua and Barbuda.

    On Wednesday, Concacaf, the sport’s regional governing body, released the full schedule for the next round, which will begin on June 2, with games continuing on and off until Match Day 4 on Sept. 11. Twelve nations have been divided into three groups with each team playing six matches, home-and-home, against the opposition. The top two teams in each group will advance to the final regional qualifying round, the so-called hexagonal, with the top three teams in that group advancing to the World Cup in Brazil. The fourth-place team will enter a playoff for another berth.

    “We’re excited to know the beginning of our path to qualify for the World Cup,” U.S.Coach Jurgen Klinsmann said in a statement, one day after the American team scored a 3-2 win in an international friendly at Slovenia. “We know it is going to be a great challenge. I am very pleased with the progress the team is making. There is a great spirit and a willingness to learn in this group, and I know that we will be ready.”

    The U.S. has never played Antigua and Barduba and will have an opportunity to snatch a quick three points in its first game against the No. 90 team in the FIFA rankings. The U.S. defeated Guatemala in both legs of semifinal round in advance of 2010 World Cup qualifying, incuding its first win at Guatemala. The U.S. is 12-4-5 against Guatemala. The U.S. has never won at Jamaica and has a 10-0-8 record against the Reggae Boyz.

    For U.S., News Is Not All Bad

    By Jack Bell
    Johan Ordonez/Agence France-Presse — Getty ImagesFreddy Garcia and Guatemala will face the U.S., Jamaica, and Antigua and Barbuda in the next round of World Cup qualifying in the Concacaf region.

    There was an interesting bit of news Friday for the United States men’s national team after another goalless outing in an international friendly — it will face a regional minnow, Antigua and Barbuda, when the Americans begin play next June in World Cup qualifying matches in the Concacaf region.

    With a game to go in the second round of regional qualifying, Antigua and Barbuda (Spanish for ancient and bearded) clinched a spot in the next round with a 1-0 win over visiting Haiti in St. John’s, the capital of the two-island nation that lies southeast of Puerto Rico.

    Jamaica and Guatemala, which also locked down a spot in the next round on Friday, will also be in the qualifying group with the United States.

    The next round of qualifying in Concacaf consists of three groups of four teams. Games in the third round will be completed in the autumn of 2012. The top two teams in each group advance to the final six-nation qualifying round, which has come to be known as the hexagonal, with play starting early in 2013. The top three finishers earn automatic berths in the 2014 World Cup in Brazil, with the fourth-place team entering a playoff against the winner of the Oceania region, which is likely to be New Zealand. According to the FIFA calendar, the dates for international matches next year are: June 1-5, June 8-12, Sept. 7-11 and Oct. 12-16.

    U.S.-France: 5 Matchups Worth Watching

    By John Godfrey

    Barcelona defender Eric Abidal will not be suiting up. Neither will Manchester City midfielder Samir Nasri, Manchester United defender Patrice Evra, nor Milan center back Philippe Mexès.

    Despite not having its entire A team available, however, the French national team will still be a heavy favorite when it faces the United States at 3 p.m. Eastern on Friday (ESPN2, ESPN3.com, Univision) at the Stade de France in Paris.

    And well it should be. France’s national team has talent to spare, and world-class players such as Franck Ribéry, Florent Malouda and Hugo Lloris should be available for the game. Moreover, Coach Laurent Blanc has adopted a slow-and-steady approach to transforming the squad that embarrassed itself at the 2010 World Cup, and his tactics are already paying dividends. Despite Blanc’s commitment to using younger players and focusing his efforts on preparations for the 2014 World Cup in Brazil, Les Bleus recently clinched a spot in the 2012 European championships and are generally on the upswing.

    The U.S., on the other hand, continues to struggle. The team has a long history of poor performances in Europe, having lost its last six matches on the continent. More troublesome, the squad has yet to coalesce under Coach Jurgen Klinsmann, suffering through a 1-3-1 record and scoring only two goals in five games. And when one factors in the absence of Landon Donovan, who has pulled out of the friendly so he can focus on the M.L.S. Cup title game nine days later, it does not bode well for the Stars and Stripes.

    Could the U.S. surprise France, ranked 15th in the world, and earn a result? Absolutely. And if the Yanks do get a tie or a victory, it will represent a huge step in the right direction for the entire program.

    Here is a quick look at some of the potential matchups that could prove decisive on Friday:

    Alex Livesey/Getty ImagesOguchi Onyewu is likely to have to deal with Real Madrid striker Karim Benzema.

    Karim Benzema vs. Oguchi Onyewu and/or Michael Orozco Fiscal

    With six goals in nine La Liga matches so far this season, Karim Benzema is one of the biggest stars on a Real Madrid squad filled with them. A technical wizard with a nose for the net, Benzema poses an extraordinary threat for a U.S. backline that sometimes seems in disarray. While the U.S. captain Carlos Bocanegra has the experience and wherewithal to stand up to a striker like Benzema, it is hard to imagine the lead-footed Oguchi Onyewu or the timid Michael Orozco Fiscal faring well in a one-on-one exchange with the 23-year-old Lyon native. If either – or both – get into the game, expect Benzema to call for the ball as often as possible.

    Thibault Camus/Associated Press Franck Ribery will be difficult to contain, even for Kyle Beckerman..

    Franck Ribéry vs. Kyle Beckerman

    Klinsmann’s devotion to Real Salt Lake midfielder Kyle Beckerman is both profound and public. “If you follow these last couple of games we played, we’ve discovered a player like Kyle Beckerman who is just doing a fantastic job for this team,” Klinsmann told reporters after the U.S. lost, 1-0, to Ecuador on Oct. 11. “He’s somebody who cleans up, he’s everywhere, he’s aggressive and he wins so many balls back. We don’t want to have him out of the team.” While some might argue that Beckerman is a step (or two) too slow for the international game, he will certainly have a chance to prove doubters wrong against France and Ribéry. The Bayern Munich attacker is on a ridiculous run of form, and can make the best No. 6’s in the world look silly. If Beckerman can neutralize Ribéry and hold down the real estate in front of the central defense, Klinsmann will certainly lavish the dreadlocked midfielder with even more praise.

    Florent Malouda vs. Steve Cherundolo

    When he is at the top of his game, the U.S. right back Steve Cherundolo is one of the unsung heroes of the United States squad – the kind of player who makes game-changing plays on both sides of the field. But Cherundolo, 32, struggled badly against Ecuador last month, and given his age, there have been some rumblings that the Hannover captain might be approaching his expiration date. If that is indeed the case, expect Chelsea left wing Florent Malouda to have a big game on Friday. Although just one year younger than Cherundolo, the left-footed Malouda is both physically imposing and technically skilled – a lethal combination. It should be fun to see these two veterans square off.

    Brandon Wade/Getty ImagesBrek Shea will train with Arsenal during the M.L.S. off-season.

    Brek Shea vs. Anthony Réveillère or Mathieu Debuchy

    Make no mistake about it: Brek Shea does not care if he lines up against Anthony Réveillère, Mathieu Debuchy, Cafu or the 101st Airborne Division. The 21-year-old from College Station, Tex., has a wonderfully naïve fearlessness that has served him well in M.L.S. and might do the same for him in the English Premier League. Now that Shea seems to have a lock on the left wing position for the U.S., his next challenge is to maintain the consistency he has shown thus far. Can Réveillère, a veteran with Lyon, or Debuchy, a midfielder/right back hybrid who plays for Lille, clamp down on Shea’s marauding ways? Absolutely. But do not bet on it.

    Jozy Altidore vs. Adil Rami

    The last time Jozy Altidore, 22, suited up for the United States in a friendly competition in Europe, he found himself isolated against Vincent Kompany, Belgium’s rugged, 6-foot-3 central defender. The evening did not go well for Altidore. Altidore could be in for more of the same on Friday, as he will have to contend with Adil Rami – France’s uncompromising central defender, who is also 6-3. When Altidore can outmuscle an opponent, he often thrives. When he feels physically dominated, he sometimes retreats into a shell. It will be interesting to see if Altidore can muster the will to compete for 90 minutes, and if Klinsmann can provide the striker with the tactical support necessary to threaten French goalkeeper Hugo Lloris.

    Will you be watching Friday’s game? Do you think the U.S. has a fighting chance against France? Share your thoughts in the comments.

    Substitutes: Bill Hamid, Michael Orozco Fiscal, Kyle Beckerman, Jeff Larentowicz, Sacha Kljestan, Jonathan Spector, Juan Agudelo

    Our man in Brussels, John Godfrey, Tuesday morning filed some outtakes from his conversation with Clint Dempsey:

    On his state of mind during the transfer window:

    “It wasn’t too stressful because I’m in a good situation with Fulham. I’m happy there. My daughter is in school and we’re all very comfortable. There was interest but nothing materialized so I have this season and another one at Fulham, and I look forward to having another good season. I’ve always dreamed of playing Champions League soccer but whether that’s in the cards for me, I don’t know.”

    technical quality to our side. “

    On Fulham’s slow start to the season:

    “Hopefully we can start getting more points, because right now we’ve played three games and we only have one point, and that’s just not good enough.”
