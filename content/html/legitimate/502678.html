There are fleeting touches of Impressionism in his music, for example in the orchestral pieces Metsässä sataa (Raining in the Forest, 1912) and Hiidet virvoja viritti (The Will-o'-wisp, 1912), but for the most part his style is a flowing, nationally tinted brand of Romanticism.
