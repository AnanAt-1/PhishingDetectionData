MLB Stats for Jim O'Rourke

Jim O'Rourke Baseball Stats | Baseball Almanac

Jim O'Rourke, the Major League Baseball player, was born on Tuesday, June 22, 1937, in Walla Walla, Washington. O'Rourke was 21 years old when he broke into the major leagues on June 16, 1959, with the St. Louis Cardinals. Jim O'Rourke stats, height, weight, career totals, schools, positions, and more historical research are in the Baseball Almanac.

"It would have been a complete route had it not been for (Jim) O'Rourke. The 6 foot, 1 inch, 200 pound senior center fielder clouted a triple, two home runs — one a bases loaded wallop over the 410 foot center field wall — and drove in eight runs. Some of the contingent of 22 major league scouts were fairly drooling over the prodigious blows delivered by the big fellow who had 11 home runs over the regular season." - Farris, Bruce. Fresno Bee Sports Writer. The Bee Sports. 2 June 1959. Page 7-C.

Jim O'Rourke Teams Played For, Uniform Numbers Worn, & MLB Contracts

James Patrick O'Rourke was a Major League Baseball player who had two Cups of Coffee with the St. Louis Cardinals (1959). Jim played baseball at University of Santa Clara, then professionally with the Drain Black Sox, a team he helped win the National Baseball Congress World Series (he was the series MVP).

Jim O'Rourke made his major league debut on June 16, 1959, pinch hitting in both games of a doubleheader (Game 1 / Game 2)against the Philadelphia Phillies in Busch Stadium. His Cardinals won each contest, but O'Rourke went hitless during both of his career at-bats. Later that same week, St. Louis sent him down to double-A, where he played with the Tulsa Oilers of the Texas League.

Did you know that Jim never returned to the majors, but he did played four seasons in the minor leagues, collecting 332 hits in 394 career games, including 60 doubles, 36 home runs, and 17 triples. After baseball, he returned home (Washington) where he owned and operated a property investment company named O'Rourke Realty and First Pacific Properties. On Monday, November 1, 2021, Jim died at home. He was 84 years old.
