The Coast

The coastline along the outer edge of the island has been split up and claimed by several stallions. There are a handful of herds that make their homes on the coast of Fuego Island. The coast is always cool in the summer, and the grass is normally lush and green.

The Canyon

The eastern part of Fuego is a desolate place. Large sandy cliffs form massive canyons full of paths and tunnels. In the middle is a rushing river, and the banks are steep and slippery. The canyon is a dangerous place to walk through, but the tunnels keep it cool in the summer.

Sub-boards: The Sky Stones, The Adobe Caves, Crumbling Cliffs, The Golden Caves

The Peaks

To the western side of Fuego, the land is treacherous and colder. Mountains rise up to the sky there, and the peaks are jagged, but close together. They form small, rocky valleys for each herd to call home. It is dry and snowless there, and not quite cold enough to freeze the rivers that run through the mountains, but it's a bit chillier then the rest of the island.

Sub-boards: Taste The Rainbow, The Chalk Stones

0 0 No posts have been made on this board.
The Valley

The valley is in the southern part of Fuego, and is quite massive. It is certainly large enough for several herds to call it home. Streams rush through the valley, though in the summer, they sometimes dry up. The grass is bright and thick, and trees dot the area for protection from the weather.
