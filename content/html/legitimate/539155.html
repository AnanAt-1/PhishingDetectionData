Del Headlines

DEG Metro Stars- There is a chance that Tore Vikingstad will not play for Düsseldorf this season. The Norwegian is still recovering from an operation.
The DEG has not yet applied for a licence for Vikingstad, implying that they may count on him not returning to active play this season. The Metro Stars currently have two empty import slots remaining.
Vikingstad was operated on his shoulder in April and is still recovering. The Norwegian has only done some light training on the ice so far, without any form of physical contact.
Düsseldorf will likely also start the season without captain Daniel Kreutzer, who is still recovering from Pfeiffer.

Hannover Scorpions- The future of the Hannover Scorpions is in doubt. Team and arena-owner Günter Papenburg is involved in a major conflict with the regional government and threatens to sell his DEL-license to another city.
Financial Times Deutschland reports on the case. The TUI-Arena in Hannover was built for the World Expo of 2000 and holds 10.500 spectators. The arena has made a loss ever since it opened its doors. The regional government handed Papenburg a 2,5 million euro loan to finance the building and now wants its money back.
The loan has to be repaid before the end of this month and Papenburg is refusing to pay, because that would put club and arena in major trouble.

AllStar Game- The DEL has awarded the 2008 All Star Game to Dresden. A remarkable move, as the Eastern German town does not have a DEL team.

Dresden has just opened the brand new Freiberger Arena, where the Four Points Cup was played. The DEL hopes that the All Star Game will help spark interest in hockey in the area, perhaps leading up to a new franchise in Dresden.
The All Star Game will be played on 2 Februari 2008. The best North American players will face the best European players in the match.

DEL Headlines

Berlin Polar Bears -will need to start the season without Denis Pederson. The Canadian forward is suffering from an inguinal hernia.

Ingolstadt Panther-has signed Brad Leeb to cover for injured Matt Higgins. The 28-year old Canadian forward is expected to join the team later this week.

Iserlohn Roosters-Chris Schmidt will no longer count against the Roosters' import quota. The Canadian's application for a German passport was approved this week.
Schmidt's parents left Germany in 1958, settling in Canada. A large part of his family still lives around the Frankfurt area.

Frankfurt Lions -will start the season without forward Derek Hahn. The Canadian broke his hand during a game against Augsburg Panthers on Friday.

DEL Headlines

DEG Metro Stars- Injuries to two key forwards have forced Düsseldorf to take on temporary replacement. Henrik Hölscher has signed a deal for one month with the DEG.
Hölscher had already been practicing with the Metro Stars and played in a number of exhibition games.

Duisburg Foxes-are parting ways with Sergei Zavodovsky. The team feels the defenceman is not living upto expectations.
The move comes as somewhat of a surprise, as Zavodovsky had already been part of the lineup last season.

Frankfurt Lions-Another Canadian import will receive a German passport. Frankfurt's captain Jason Young will no longer count against the Lions's import limit.

Krefeld Pinguine-could start the regular season without defenceman Daryl Andrews. The Canadian injured his elbow and required surgery.
Andrews was hurt in the Cup game against SC Riessersee on Tuesday. The 30-year old defenceman was immediately operated on in Garmisch-Partenkirchen.

Hamburg Freezers-Hamburg has signed defenceman Daniel Sevo to a contract extension. The German will stay with the Freezers until at least December.
The contract has an option which allows the Freezers to extend the contract until the end of the season.

NATIONAL TEAM- For the first time in the history of German hockey, the national team won a game against the Czech Republic on Czech ice. After falling behind 2-0 in the exhibition game, Germany stormed back to score a historic 4-2-win. John Tripp, Felix Schütz, Christoph Ullmann and Sven Felski were the scorers. Tomas Rolinek and Josef Straka scored for the Czechs.
The Czech bounce back in the second game and won by the same score 4-2.
