    ‘Slice’ Is Torn

    By Pete Thamel

    Few people will watch the game between U.C.L.A. and Pittsburgh with more mixed emotions tonight than the the Manhattan College coach Barry Rohrssen. Rohrssen, who is nicknamed Slice, just finished his first season at Manhattan after a seven-year stint at Pittsburgh as an assistant coach and associate head coach under both Ben Howland and Jamie Dixon. Howland and Dixon are the best of friends – their relationship stretching back to when Howland recruited Dixon as a high school player.

    “Ben and Jamie have true history,” Rohrssen said. He pointed out that much was made of the friendship between Tom Izzo and Tom Crean when Marquette and Michigan State played in the first round. But that relationship only spanned one coaching stop. Dixon worked with Howland at Santa Barbara, Northern Arizona and Pittsburgh.

    “It’s not just individually that they’re friends,” Rohrssen said. “Their families are the best of friends. This is a very encompassing thing.”

    As for Rohrssen, he’ll be watching the game from his office at Manhattan tonight. He wouldn’t come out and say he’d be rooting for Pittsburgh, but did admit “there are more variables for me on the Pittsburgh side.”

    Rohrssen, though, said it will be tough for him to see one team lose. “Along with their coaching styles, which people see as similar, their moral fabric and their character are very similar,” Rohrssen said.

    “They’re quality human beings…both good fathers and good husbands.”
