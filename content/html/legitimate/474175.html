Vada Pinson, the Major League Baseball player, was born on Thursday, August 11, 1938, in Memphis, Tennessee. Pinson was 19 years old when he broke into the major leagues on April 15, 1958, with the Cincinnati Redlegs. Vada Edward Pinson was a Major League Baseball player with the Cincinnati Redlegs (1958), Cincinnati Reds (1959-1968), St. Louis Cardinals (1969), Cleveland Indians (1970-1971), California Angels (1972-1973), and Kansas City Royals (1974-1975). Did you know that when Vada retired in 1975, he was only the second player — the first was Willie Mays — in Major League Baseball history with 250-or-more career home runs and 300-or-more career stolen bases?

Vada Pinson Rookie Card | 1958 Topps Baseball Card (#420 | Checklist)

Did you know that when Vada Pinson collected 205 hits in 1959, he was the third left-handed hitter in Reds' history with a 200-hit season? Two years later, Pinson collected 208 hits, then 204 hits in 1963, then 204 hits in 1965 — the first lefty in Reds' history with more than one 200-hit season, and he finished his career with four!

Did you know that when Vada Pinson won a Gold Glove in 1961, he was only the second Cincinnati Reds outfielder to win — the first was Frank Robinson (1958)? Pinson was one of three National League outfielders to receive the honor in 1961, joining Willie Mays (winning his fifth) and first time recipient Roberto Clemente.

Vada Pinson never won another Gold Glove, but defensively led the senior circuit in games played by an outfielder six times, putouts three times, assists three times, fielding percentage twice, and double plays twice! He roamed the outfield in 2,485 games, handling 5,360 total chances, yet committing only 101 errors!

Vada Pinson was one of the most dominant National League players during the 1960s and has the following Top 10 ranks during the decade (1960-1969): second in doubles (310), second in triples (93), third in at bats (6,086), third in hits (1,776), fourth in games played (1,516), fourth in singles (1,198), fifth in runs scored (885), fifth in stolen bases (202), fifth in total bases (2,797), and ninth in RBIs (792)!

Highly respected baseball historian Bill James ranked Vada Pinson as the #18th greatest center fielder of all time in The New Bill James Historical Baseball Abstract (Free Press Publishers. 13 June 200. Center Field. Page 781.) ahead of Hall of Famers Hack Wilson (#19), Hugh Duffy (#20), Max Carey (#23) Earle Combs (#34), and Lloyd Waner (#50).
