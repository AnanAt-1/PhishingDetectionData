Prof. Robert Fisher has been an academic in the School of Informatics (originally in the former Department of Artificial Intelligence) at University of Edinburgh since 1984 and a full Professor since 2003. He received his PhD from University of Edinburgh (1987), investigating computer vision in the former Department of Artificial Intelligence. His previous degrees are a BS with honors (Mathematics) from California Institute of Technology (1974) and a MS (Computer Science) from Stanford University (1978). He worked as a software engineer for 5 years before returning to study for his PhD.

Research Overview

Publication List. Google Scholar List. University PURE research activity entry. ResearchGate. LinkedIn.

He has been researching 3D scene understanding since 1982, and has worked on model based object recognition, range image analysis and parallel vision algorithms.

He is or has been the principal investigator of these projects:

  • a partner in euCognition: European Network for the Advancement of Artificial Cognitive Systems
  • DERMOFIT: A cognitive prosthesis to aid focal skin lesion diagnosis (Wellcome Foundation funded research project).
  • ChiRoPing: Dynamic shape capture of bats using a high speed 3D range sensor
  • Fish4Knowledge: investigating knowledge extraction from very large datasets (of fish), including its SecondLife Exhibition Hall
  • TrimBot2020: investigating the robotics and computer vision needed by a gardening robot for rose, hedge and topiary trimming
  • Advanced Care Research Centre - a University-wide activity focussing on improving care for ageing adults. My group is investigating what can be done with video analysis.

Popular image analysis datasets/databases

  • BEHAVE - Interacting Person Video Data with markup
  • CAVIAR - video sequences with tracking and behavior ground truth
  • CEILIDHDATA - video data and ground truth for 16 Ceilidh with two different dance patterns
  • DERMOFIT - 1300 images of 10 types of skin lesion (5 malignant)
  • FACECHANGE - frontal images of faces captured several times a day for several months
  • FORUMTRACKING - overhead camera person tracking dataset
  • EATSENSE - eating action
  • HIPR2 Image Catalogue - a small dataset of different types of images
  • HUMOLS - 101 videos capturing a single human subject in a motionless state
  • OFFICEDATA - 20 days of monitoring a person in their office
  • PIGDATA - 23 days (over 6 weeks) of daytime pig RGBD video captured from a nearly overhead camera
  • SURGICALTOOLS - RGBD images of five simulated surgical tools (two kinds of scalpels, two kinds of clamps, and one tweezers), synthetic and real images, total 64,728 images
  • Time-Lapse Videos of a Building Site
  • UTENSILS - 897 raw and binary images of 20 categories of kitchen utensil
  • WeightGait - multi-factorial dataset for simulated gait abnormality detection and assessment
