Legal Answers
Q. Can a judge order a polygragh test and when the defendant passes the test, not use the results in the case?
November 16, 2013
A: MCL 776.21 provides that a defendant charged with criminal sexual conduct shall be given a polygraph exam if he requests one. This law does not automatically allow the defendant to use the results at trial. A Judge will usually refuse to allow the passing results into trial, anymore than he will allow the failing results into evidence at the jury trial.
Q. Can a foreclosure commence by advertisement if the original lender never recorded an assignment of mortgage?
April 29, 2013
A: The Michigan Foreclosure by Advertisement statute requires that the mortgagee be the record holder of the mortgage. This is done by recording the assignment with the Register of Deeds. If that is not done, the the foreclosure sale might be set aside.
