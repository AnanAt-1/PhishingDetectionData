Hardgainer Myth #1
ALWAYS train 8-12 reps if you want your muscles to grow

Bigger bones equal bigger muscles.

One of the reasons we naturally skinny guys have problems putting on muscles is because we were born with tiny bones.

But the good news is that by getting stronger, your bones will actually grow and allow you to put on more muscle.

Getting stronger equals bigger bones, which leads to bigger muscles and more strength. It's a virtuous cycle that feeds itself and continues to go up!

Thus, you need to train in the lower rep ranges strategically using "Intelligent Program Design".

But don't worry about figuring out exactly what rep ranges, when to use them, or on what lifts.

This training will show you how to get stronger and grow your bones so you can pack on pounds of muscle.

Hardgainer Myth #2
Isolating Muscle-Groups is What REALLY Gets Them to Grow

They say Isolation workouts help to really target and put emphasis on the given muscle.

Let’s look at what an isolation workout really is:

It’s simply a type of exercise that targets ONE muscle group at a time. Now I know what you’re thinking – “Isolating a specific body part allows me to really contract that muscle to get a burn and if I concentrate on that area, it’ll grow faster”

But let’s look at the facts:

Unless you are promoting enough muscle fiber recruitment, burn or no burn, your workouts are practically worthless.

However, isolation workouts accompanied by certain compound exercises in a specific order will allow you to maximize fiber recruitment and increase strength and bone density.

Think of your pecs as a house, your triceps as a hut, and the workers as the muscle fibers.

If you do an isolation exercise, that's like only waking 10 workers to build the hut and 10 worker to build the house.

Now imagine if you recruited 100 workers to build the house then told 50 of them to build the hut.

Both jobs would get done faster!  Multi-Joint exercises are like recruiting 100 workers instead of 10. 

Hardgainer Myth #3
The Heavier You LIFT, The Bigger You Grow

Now before you assume this contradicts myth #1, read more.

Another big mistake skinny guy’s make is focusing on only 1/3 of each lift.

Let’s take the bench press for example, 99% of the guys in the gym are focusing on lifting the bar off of their chest.  So, the idea here is that the heavier you can lift, the more strain you put on your muscle – the more its forced to grow. WRONG!

Your muscles can’t see how much weight is on the bar! They can only tell how much tension and strain you are putting on under.

This approach is robbing you out of over 50% of your gains.

Look at it like this, out of a 10 rep set, most guys are using their muscle for about 10-15 seconds.

But if you take advantage of the entire lift, you will keep your muscles under tension over 30-45 seconds resulting in TRIPPLING muscle fiber recruitment while performing the SAME EXERCISE.
