Spam?

  • Originally, SPAM is spicy ham packaged in a handy dandy 12-ounce can from Hormel Foods that can be found in almost every food market in the United States.
  • Technically speaking, spam describes a particular kind of Usenet posting. Unfortunately this is not what PSFPOP3 is designed for...
  • If somebody is not speaking about neither of the above one, I'd bet my bottom dollar for the fact that he/she is referring to UBE (Unsolicited Bulk Email), UCE (Unsolicited Commercial Email), MMF (Make Money Fast), or MLM (Multi Level Marketting) types of eMail postings... Just the ones that drive every eMail user insane every now and then.

POP3?

  • POP3 describes the PostOffice Protocol and is defined in RFC-1939. Almost every mail client today supports POP3-protocol.
  • There is also another type of mail delivery protocol, IMAP, which actually is a far superior protocol. Unfortunately PSFPOP3 doesn't offer support for that yet, because of the facts that
    1. IMAP is a much harder protocol to implement and
    2. I personally don't have any possibilities to test it myself because I don't have a single IMAP-account.

PSFPOP3?

  • PSFPOP3 is a one man effort to the ongoing fight against spam. PSFPOP3 is a simple application for the Win32-platform that
    • fetches mail from users' POP3-accounts
    • filters them for spam
    • stores mail to wait for delivery
    • and finally delivers the mail to the users' mailbox
  • This means that PSFPOP3 acts as both client and server, which allows it to be independent of any specific mail clients. You can use PSFPOP3 with any mail client available.
  • You can download PSFPOP3 at psfpop3.zip (author's homepage - go and check out the nutcracker that created this one).
  • Multi-language userinterface will be implemented soon. Currently the application is available is english only.

Recent changes

30.09.2003

Due to the increased problems with floodmailing worms (Swen, Bugbear, Fizzer and others that have similar spreading tactics), I implemented a new handler for such messages (well, on my opinion they are nothing but irritating mail, and because PSFPOP3 can do something about them, why shouldn't it). The message is intercepted and the original message included as an attachement with some guidelines for how to act with messages containing suspicious attachements. Also the subject of the message is altered so that users can make rules on their mail clients not to download such messages from PSFPOP3 and thus saving precious bandwidth (this has no use for people running PSFPOP3 on the same machine they use to read their mail, but for me who runs around with a laptop and sometimes is forced to using slow dialup connections this is a blessing).

It should be noted that even with this new functionality, people should still use up-to-date anti-virus software for protection - PSFPOP3 is not intended as one; it doesn't do any further virus scanning on the file, it just supposes unpacked executable files sent as attachements to be something worth stopping (on the other hand, who would send an unpacked executable through eMail? I can't figure why someone would do such a thing).
