The Westerbork Serenade

The Westerbork Serenade (by David Natale) tells the true story of Jewish cabaret performers held by the Nazis in the Dutch transit camp of Westerbork. From 1942-1944, some of Berlin's greatest stars performed at Westerbork, thereby delaying their transport to death camps. Also See Web Page: WWW.WESTERBORKSERENADE.COM

Monday, April 5, 2021

Key City Public Theatre Offers Virtual Performance of the Westerbork Serenade

I have no way to say it except: "This past year sucked!"

However, the rise of "virtual theatre" will give you a unique chance to see my 2010 break-out Amsterdam performance of "The Westerbork Serenade"!

Key City Public Theatre in Port Townsend Washington is hosting

THE ART OF THE SOLO from April 29 to May 22.

"The Westerbork Serenade" runs April 29, May 1, May 7 and May 9 at 7pm Pacific time.

For each performance, I will give an intro and be live for a post show discussion.

Tickets are $10 for one show and $35 for a series pass (SEE BELOW) 

https://keycitypublictheatre.org/solo-show-series/ 

Others works in the series are the awesome multi-character performance of "Returning The Bones" by "Westerbork Serenade" director: Gin Hammond (4/30, 5/2-6-8); a wild movement focused "Gifts of War" (5/13-21-23); and a stunning performance of "The Odyssey" by the late and oh-so-great Charlie Bethel (5/16-20-22).

In honor of this "Westerbork Serenade" revival, below I have re-posted the podcast series from our trip to the Netherlands in 2010. Please check them out. and I'll see you after the show! -David
