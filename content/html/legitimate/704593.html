How to Make a Mimosa Bar for the Best Brunch Ever

Make your celebration extra special with this DIY cocktail bar.

By Lyndsey MatthewsUpdated: Mar 6, 2019

Country Living editors select each product featured. If you buy from a link, we may earn a commission. Why Trust Us?

Cheers! Spring is approaching, and the season gives us so many opportunities to celebrate: Easter Sunday, Mother's Day, bridal showers, baby showers—just to name a few! If you're the lucky one who gets to be the hostess with the mostest, there's no more festive way to toast to a special day than with a mimosa bar. Not only will it make your party one to remember, but it will also make your day less stressful when you're not bustling around the kitchen playing bartender the entire time. Now, what do you have to do to make a super cute DIY cocktail serving station? Just follow our easy tutorial!

How To Make Them

To take the guesswork out of making your own mimosa, set up a chalkboard sign with instructions that read:

  • Pour the Bubbly
  • Add the Juice
  • Top With Fruit

Have guests serve themselves according to their own preferences, but we recommend a 1:1 ratio of prosecco to juice for each mimosa.

How Much You'll Need

You can make 6-8 mimosas out of every standard 750 ml bottle of sparkling wine, so for a party of 20 people where each guest will have two or three drinks we recommend buying about 6-8 bottles of prosecco. To give guests options, serve four different kinds of juice—we picked orange juice, strawberry lemonade, pomegranate juice, and an orange-mango juice. Most people will opt for the classic orange and bubbly mix, so stock up on a larger bottle of OJ. Three smaller bottles of juice will be enough for the other varieties. As for the fruit, pick up a few pints of whatever is in season. We chose strawberries and blackberries because they were the freshest options at the grocery store.

How to Pick the Best Sparkling Wine for Mimosas

It really all comes down to personal preference. "If you like sweet drinks, choose a sweet prosecco rather than a dry to pair with the already sweet-but-tart orange juice," says Emanuele Siena, of Zonin prosecco. But if super-sweet cocktails aren't your thing, opt for a dry sparkling wine. To accommodate everyone's tastes, serve several different kinds of bubbly so that people can customize their cocktails.

Time to Decorate

Now for the fun part! For a colorful (and affordable!) mimosa bar backdrop, we decided on pretty tassel garlands. And because a party without cake is just a meeting, we whipped up this pink ombré cake with a mini tassel garland cake topper to match the backdrop.

The Details

Zonin prosecco, stemless champagne flutes, beaker glass pitcher, porcelain bowls, stainless steel beverage tub, pink napkins, wood tray, chalkboard, chalkboard markers.
