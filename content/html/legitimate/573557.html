    Nightmare on Bush Street: Mountain View house transformed into DC Cemetery for 20th year

    By Mercury News | themerc@bayareanewsgroup.com
    PUBLISHED: October 28, 2011 at 5:07 PM PDT

    It’s four hours until showtime and the Grim Reaper won’t budge.

    Slumped over in a downtown Mountain View driveway Friday afternoon, the guy’s not rising to his potential, literally. When animated, he stands to an imposing 12 feet tall, spreading out his arms.

    Welcome to the DC Cemetery, where props expert Brent Ross has been turning his parents’ Bush Street yard into a live nightmare for the past two decades.

    The family’s home is hard to miss. It stands out with its wrought-iron gates, gargoyles and a dozen or so skeletons hanging around Ross’ mother’s roses. In fact, you’d hardly know there was a house there at all, the façade is so convincing.

    Though it doesn’t have the walk-through haunted house element it did three years ago, the crew is still expecting thousands of visitors this weekend to their haunt, which won $50,000 from Fearnet.com‘s Scariest Home Haunted House contest in 2007.

    “Especially when Halloween comes on a weekend, it’s packed,” Ross said. “People stay for two hours because they can’t see what’s in front of them.”

    Luckily for the Rosses, the neighbors are supportive of the event — especially since the police and fire departments close off the nearest streets. The family has established a great relationship with the fire department, Ross said, adding that they’ve rarely checked on the event in recent years.

    Behind the host of characters and the temporary architecture, streams of cords cluttered the Rosses’ garage on Friday, each acting “like an umbilical cord” to individual props. Ross said he’s waiting on 200 pounds of dry ice as his former neighbor works on the power and his dad brings out the donation jars. They’ve spent the past three nights perfecting the lighting.

    “If he’s warped, it’s our fault,” said dad Mike Ross, adding that his family has lived there since 1983.

    Halloween was always special to the Ross family, and Brent and his four brothers received a $100 prop every Oct. 31. The spectacle grew from there, with Brent taking over in junior high, Mike Ross said.

    “It started out as a simple black light and a skull, and now it’s this monstrosity,” Brent Ross said.

    Now a San Jose resident and owner of DC Designs, Ross has made many of the props himself, particularly the metal ones, such as the gates.

    The four gargoyles were the first sculptures he attempted and he’s wired all of the animated props himself, such as a skeleton that plays an organ.

    But it becomes more work each year as his brothers and friends move away. This year, the crew got started on Sept. 28.

    “You’d never be able to do this in one year,” Ross said of the entire setup, which he’s built upon over the years. “This organ player’s probably got 100 hours in him.”

    Plus, the expenses have added up. The entire production costs about $10,000, and Ross pays about $500 each month to store the props. Donations help, but even in the best years, the event draws $2,000 to $3,000. In the worst years, it’s been closer to a couple of hundred.

    This year is particularly special. Not only does it mark the 20th anniversary of the DC Cemetery, but it’s also the revival of the event after Ross took last year off to spend time with his two young children.

    And yet, 300 to 400 people still showed up, Mike Ross said.

    This year, neighbors have brought by cookies for the crew and been pleased to see the haunt return, Brent Ross said.

    “We have teens here who say, ‘I’ve come every year since I was 3,’ ” he said. “(And I’m thinking), ‘Whoa, you just drove up in a car.'”

    Email Kristen Marschall at kmarschall@dailynewsgroup.com.

    IF YOU GO

    WHAT: The DC Cemetery
    WHEN: Today, 6 to 10 p.m.; Sunday, 6 to 9 p.m.; Monday (Halloween), 6 to 10 p.m. and possibly later; Tuesday, 6 to 8 p.m. Child-friendly haunts are on Sunday and Monday from 4 to 6 p.m.
    WHERE: 840 Bush St., Mountain View
    MORE INFO: www.dc-cemetery.com
    COST: Free, but donations are encouraged.
