1. CAPTAIN WILLIAM CREED was born Abt. 1739 in Limerick, Ireland of English parents,

and died April 11, 1809 in Charlottetown, Prince Edward Island (P.E.I.). He married (1) MARY

SPENCER. He married (2) ELIZABETH PRINCE 1783 in Providence, RI or Boston, MA,

daughter of CAPTAIN PRINCE and ELIZABETH ALLEN.

Notes for CAPTAIN WILLIAM CREED: Captain Creed and Mary Spencer were never actually

married; they lived together "as husband and wife" for more than 17 years. She died in

Providence, RI in 1821. He is buried at the Old Elm Street (now University Avenue) Cemetery,

Charlottetown, P.E.I. Elizabeth Prince was the daughter of Captain Job Prince and Elizabeth

Allen. She had been previously married to David Higgins who died April, 1783, and by him had

four sons. Elizabeth's ancestry can be traced back directly to Elder William Brewster (and

beyond). She died in P.E.I. in 1812 but an ancillary probate for her was taken out in Boston,

Suffolk County, MA, as she had inherited a portion of her father's estate.

3. JOB PRINCE CREED (CAPTAIN WILLIAM1) was born 1785 in Providence, RI, and died

October 30, 1867 in Sturgeon, P.E.I.. He married MARY ANN THISTLE in the Catholic

Church, Panmure Island, P.E.I..

Notes for JOB PRINCE CREED: Shunned by his father for having married a Roman Catholic

(Mary Ann Thistle) he was forced to move from Lower Montague to Sturgeon. His original

homestead is located on the left hand side of Route 17 a short distance beyond Dirty River,

heading away from Montague. Because they were of different faiths, they could not be buried

together. He is buried in the Old Pioneer Cemetery, Wightman's Point, P.E.I., while she is buried,

facing him, at the Catholic Cemetery, Panmure Island, P.E.I.

Notes for MARY ANN THISTLE: Buried on Panmure Island (where the only Catholic Church

was located), facing the grave of her husband, Job Prince Creed, who was buried in the Pioneer

Cemetery located at Wightman's Point (near St. Andrew's Lighthouse).
