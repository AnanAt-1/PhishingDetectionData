**UPDATED** My dear friend, Verne Langdon is gone.

﻿
"Family isn't a word or blood, it is love."- Verne Langdon 


﻿
A dear friend of mine died on NEW YEARS DAY. I met Verne Langdon in 1989. I was 17 and just about to graduate from high school. I was a fan of professional wrestling and was on the wrestling team in high school. I used to horse around and throw the younger wrestlers around, and slam chairs on their backs. My coach at the time told me he trained wrestlers at a gym where you could also learn to wrestle professionally. I was sold! Next thing I knew I was being introduced to the owner of Slammers Wrestling Gym. 

Toro vs Verne
Once I turned 18 I went to Sun Valley and enrolled in the University of Professional Wrestling. It sound silly today, because there are back yard losers, er, wrestlers putting their matches on YouTube, but back then there was no YouTube, or backyard wrestling. The stars of the day were still Hulk Hogan, and Ultimate Warrior, not The Miz, or John Cena. Anyhow, I started wrestling, and soon thereafter the gym began holding 'exhibitions' before a paying audience. For the next 12 years I not only wrestled professionally, but also cultivated a friendship with the owner, Verne that would last 21 years.

﻿
Verne and his rosebud
﻿
Over the years, he experienced ups and downs, and went to hell and back. I've had my troubled moments in life as well along the way, and we were there for one another the entire way. One of the most memorable ones occurred when the entire wrestling company he put together turned on him, in an unannounced takeover. I won't go into details here ( I will another time) but he was left wondering who he could trust in a matter of 24 hours. I was the only one, aside from 3 other individuals that, that he spoke to when the event occurred and together, we weathered the storm for the next five days. It was a dark time in his life, and probably strengthened our already strong friendship. After some time past myself and the three other individuals (Out of respect to them I'll let them tell their story) then rebuilt Slammers to sleeker, and meaner wrestling gym. We kept it going until financially it made better sense to close the doors. Wrestling had gone from headlocks to the head, to instead padlocks to the face, and Slammers would not be part of that.


Verne was a jack of all trades and a master of all of them. An accomplished musician, the man could romance you with his piano playing, and make you laugh with his vaudevillian beats! When he sat down to play you a song, he would compose a masterpiece out of thin air. One of my guilty pleasures in life, was having him call me every year for my birthday, and sing "happy birthday" to me over the phone with his piano accompanying him in the background. I'm sad that those calls will never be anymore. We were separated by distance, as he spent most of his life up north in Santa Barbara, and later moved to Arizona, but we always talked for hours on the phone.

He lived five lifetimes with all the various professions he had. Musician, Hollywood Make-up artist, Halloween Mask maker and co-owner of Don Post Studios, creator of various Universal Studios Attractions like Castle Dracula, and Land of a thousand faces, etc! I could not believe the day, while having casual conversations, I found out he was part of Castle Dracula! That was my favorite show when I was 7 at Universal Studios! The whole premise was insane...the universal monsters all gather for dinner at Castle Dracula, and the entire dinner party is crashed by the Incredible Hulk! Genius!!

His Make-up career was no different, as he was part of many famous productions including Planet of the Apes, Batman Returns, and the infamous Star Wars Christmas special, to name a few. Verne seemed to know everybody and anybody in the business. I was privileged to hear many Hollywood stories and secrets that would make any tell-all-book publisher drool; all of them more incredible and fascinating than the next one, and all of them my own special secret that was between Verne and I.
More recently, I had the pleasure of helping Verne during his appearances at Monsterpalooza in Burbank, and the pleasure of watching him as he met his fans. Verne is the perfect example of a cult giant in a small pool, that is the mask world. I used to tease him, and say that he was my "Bella," referring to the movie Ed Wood, and the relationship between Johnny Depp's character Ed Wood, and Martin Landau's Bella Lugosi.

A few days after the news of Verne's death, I went to one of the many spots he and I would go to and eat. I introduced Verne to King's Fish House for lunch. We went and Verne started making friends with the staff and acting like he was scouting the place in order to purchase it. We sat down and Verne immediately said "Order what ever you want, lunch is on me!" I told him to take his time, we'll be able to come back again, so no need to be excessive. We ordered oysters and shrimp cocktails, and when Verne took bites of the shrimp tails and suck down the oysters he gave me that famous Verne Langdon quote, "These are the best fucken oysters and shrimps I've ever tasted!" Well, in his honor, I went out and had oysters and a shrimp cocktail in his honor.

These are only a few of many stories and memories I share with Verne. I can honestly say he and I have been through many projects, and big plans together. Some of them we actually saw through to completion, and others were just plans we would make, secretly knowing in my mind that he probably wouldn't live to see most of them. The important thing is that we made each other an important part of life. When the two of us were together, it became the Verne and Edgar show. And anyone around us was fair game.

We could be apart for many months, and when we got together we'd pick up right where we left off. Verne was always there for me in fun, and very serious times. I could confide in him my worst thoughts, and never feel anything but love and understanding from him. We had an unusual way of showing love to each other. Anytime we called the other person on the phone, or greeted one another in person, we'd say "You Asshole," and give each other the biggest hug and kiss.

Verne Langdon New Years Eve (12.31.10)
Photo courtesy of Artie H.
I lost someone very special, and the void I feel right now, will probably never go away. Verne was there for me when I lost my father and from that day on, aside from everything else he was to me, became my second my father. On NEW YEARS DAY 2011, I lost my second father, mentor, brother, fellow artist, opponent & friend. There will never be another man like Verne. He was the ultimate optimist, pessimist, realist, and dreamer all wrapped into one. He taught me to follow my dreams, and not let anyone tell me it can't be done. My biggest thrill was casting Verne as the bartender in my first ever short film. He was there for 10 hours and although he was tired he wore a giant smile on his face. When we were done shooting his part he asked me how he did. I said he was the best fucken bartender I'd ever seen.

Rest in Peace Asshole. I love you now and forever. Get some drinks ready, I'll see you on the other side.
