Roadstar Wall Of Fame

 

Flashback to our friend Marlaina pointing to Ed's name on the Roadstar Wall of Fame.

The plaque above the list of names says:

This wall is dedicated to the men and women of Landstar who have devoted their lives to setting and maintaining the highest standards of safety and professionalism in the transportation industry.

It is through the efforts of these fine people that Landstar continues to provide the safest, highest quality, most innovative transportation solutions in America.

Roadstars, we salute you!

Not only do I salute my husband (not literally), but I am so proud of him.  Not just for achieving the highest award the company we are leased to bestows upon drivers, but proud of him for the daily level of safety he maintains when we are on the road.  

Way to go, Eddie!

Thursday, March 20, 2025

Who Are These Made For?

 

These fairy figurines were on display at a truck stop in Utah.  We see them all over.  There seem to be a LOT of people into this shit.  This, the dreamcatchers, and the t-shirts and blankets with wolves on them.  WTF?

A lot of shelf space was given to these little mystical creatures.  I just don't get it.  Why the truck stop?  Who are they buying this stuff for?  Do they really understand their clientele?  I suppose there are a lot of truckers bringing these figurines back to their wives and girlfriends. 

If Ed ever bought me one of these, I'd try to start a fire with it and watch it melt into a blob of plastic.  I find them creepy and a little odd. They just annoy me.

If you ever need anything like this, you know where to go.  Don't forget to pick up an eagle t-shirt and a wolf fleece blanket, too.  You might as well get the whole double-wide trailer starter pack.

Wednesday, March 19, 2025

My Mother's Hand

This is my mother's hand.  82 years old.  Lifelong nail-biter.  Gardener.  Watercolor artist.  Diaper changer.  Crocheter.  Master coloring book colorer.  Home cook.  Christmas cookie maker.  Wielder of a wooden spoon.  These hands, this hand, have done a LOT.

What I love about this photo is the way she has the blanket woven between her fingers.  She's been doing this since she was a kid.  When she's sleeping, sitting in her recliner with the silky blanket the cats love, or on a road trip with a lap blanket.  She's always done this and still does.  She likes to rub the fabric between her fingers - bonus points if it's silky, or soft and fuzzy.

For as long as she lives and for a lifetime after, I will always remember her doing this with her hand.  

Next time I see her, I'm going to make sure to hold that hand and remember all the things it has done.
