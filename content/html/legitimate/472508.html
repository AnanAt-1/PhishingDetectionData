Click to expand...

It might be that simple... it might not.


Using 2 subs with dissimilar outputs and LF extension *can* be problematic. The output of the stronger sub will be limited to the max output of the lesser sub. Once the lesser sub hits xMax, or starts to distort or go into compression, (all these are indications of it hitting it's limit), the max output of the *system* will be reached. If that output is less than the max output of the stronger sub by itself, then you'll be leaving headroom on the table.


In terms of LF extension, if the lesser sub has a subsonic filter set at or below it's LF extension, then sending it a full range signal will not be a problem, as it will just filter out the stuff it can't reproduce. However, if it doesn't have a subsonic filter, then it will waste amplifier power and driver excursion attempting to reproduce frequencies it is incapable of reproducing. This will further limit max output.


If you really want to use the lesser sub, it may be best for the system overall to set it's output lower than the stronger sub. Use it to just "fill in" some of the FR anomalies without asking it to contribute significantly to overall output. You may need some measurement capability to optimize this.


If you run Audyssey XT32 and use it to set the levels, and then find that the "system" is too limited by the lesser sub, you can either take it out, or you can try re-running the levels, then turning the lesser sub's level down 3 to 6 dB, and then running the EQ. That should give you 3 to 6 dB more headroom while still optimizing the EQ of the combined subs.

I am not familiar (yet) with XT32. However, if it is like the SubEQ or the AS-EQ1, it will ping the subs individually first to set the levels and distances, then it will ping the subs together to set the EQ filters. To do what I proposed above, you would lower the level of the lesser sub by 3 to 6 dB after the initial pings, but before the EQ pings. Audyssey will still account for the lesser sub in the EQ filters, but you'll have more headroom. The downside is that you won't get as much FR help from the lesser sub as you would have with equal levels for both subs.

I would urge some caution using the The PC-12 NSD with the PC-12 Plus and the AS-EQ1. The EQ1 will want to level-match them. If the NSD is in a spot where it gets less room reinforcement, or if it's significantly further away from the listening/measurement position, it will be set higher than the Plus sub. In this scenario, it will be the limiting factor in the total output of the system. You could end up with *less* output than before you added the second sub. Try to chose a placement where it gets as least as much reinforcement and/or it is closer than the Plus sub.
