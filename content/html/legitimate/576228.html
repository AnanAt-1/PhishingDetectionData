Ada Township plans resource center and maintenance building for Roselle Park

  • Updated: Nov. 06, 2011, 1:56 p.m.
  • |Published: Nov. 06, 2011, 12:56 p.m.
By
  • Jan Holst | jholst@mlive.com

Ada Township has revealed plans for Roselle Park, 1010 Grand River Drive, which already has miles of trails providing access to wildlife habitats.

Since beginning restoration in 2004, the land has been reclaimed from its previous commercial use. The most visible remaining evidences  of the former owner are the two silos.

But even those has been transformed and now offer both a children's climbing wall and an observation deck for visitors wishing to overlook the expanse of meadow grass and wetlands. In addition to the changes to the silos, improvements already done in the park include demolition of the Ada Beef facility, addition of a parking lot, 4 miles of trails, a boardwalk river overlook, canoe landing, The Woodrick Observation Deck, a day-lighted steam and prairie and wetland restoration.

Now a new and more comprehensive plan for the park has gained Township Board approval. Many items, such as the trail systems, benches, tree plantings, boardwalks, overlook and habitat improvements will be ongoing, said Ada Director of Parks and Recreation, Mark Fitzpatrick. "They will also be influenced by funding through grants, memorial gifts and donations of both funds and volunteer support by area service groups," he said. If anyone is interested in donating things such as a tree or a bench in memory of someone, they should contact Fitzpatrick at 676,9520 or email at mfitzpatrick@adatownshipmi.com.

The new Roselle Park plan includes several major projects as well, which will be phased in over time.

Among the major projects is construction of a Resource Center, with restrooms, adjacent parking, meeting room for 75 people, warming kitchen and veranda. Also planned is a new main entrance for the park across from Michigan Street. Fitzpatrick said that the process could be ready to begin serious fundraising for the Resource Center and new entrance within two years and be underway by 2014.

Boardwalk and low-impact nature trails are planned to extend from the Resource Center to the paved trail along the Grand River.
 
Officials are looking for sponsors to support and raise funds for a new Adventure Playground and upgrades for existing playground by the silos. "It will be a year or so before the playground gets started in that it still has to go through a design phase," said Fitzpatrick.
 
Following the construction of Resource Center, the township plans to move toward construction of an on-site maintenance building to aid in caring for the park, the Resource Building and adjacent trails. The current entrance and parking area is the proposed site for the maintenance facility. 
 
The final phase will be to construct the Picnic Area Gazebo and adjacent parking area. This area is specifically designed for large group gatherings.
 
The adopted master plan outlines additional enhancements for natural areas, riverbank stabilization, and native prairie restoration along the day-lighted stream. Also on the wish list are an elevated wetland observation deck in the south central area, additional benches, river overlooks and screening the park to private properties along Grand River Drive.

"These plans provide us direction for future development of the park site," said Fitzpatrick. "Over the next few years we will work to accomplish many of the goals set out in the plan."
