DIED: December 30, 2006.
HEIGHT: 5'11"
WEIGHT: 172. pounds.
CATCHES: Left.

WHAT OTHERS ARE SAYING:

"Plasse, as a rookie, was No. 2 behind the superb Ken Dryden last season and had a fine 2.58 goals-against average in 18 games. He'll see more action this year with Dryden gone. He's also the only pro goalie to score a goal - an open-net one while with Kansas City in the Central League in the 1970-71 season." (Montreal Gazette, October 12, 1973, p. 37)

"Plasse has been the third man this season in Scotty Bowman's troika system in the nets, although he did play more toward the end of the regular season. He's a more than adequate backup should anything happen to either Wayne Thomas or Michel Larocque, who figure to start ahead of him in goal throughout the Stanley Cup playoffs." (Montreal Gazette, April 10, 1974, p. 46)

TRANSACTIONS:

  • Selected by Montreal Canadiens in first round (first Montreal selection, first overall) during National Hockey League amateur draft, June 13, 1968.
  • Loaned by Canadiens to Kansas City Blues (CHL), October 1, 1970.
  • Sold by Canadiens to St. Louis Blues, December 11, 1970.
  • Sold by Blues to Montreal Canadiens, August 23, 1971.
  • Claimed (from Montreal) by Kansas City Scouts in National Hockey League Expansion Draft, June 12, 1974.
  • Traded by Scouts to Pittsburgh Penguins for G Denis Herron and Jean-Guy Lagace, January 10, 1975.
  • Transferred from Penguins (with Simon Nolet and the loan of Colin Campbell for the 1976-77 season [9/1/76]) to Colorado Rockies as compensation for Pittsburgh's signing of free agent G Denis Herron, August 7, 1976.
  • Signed as free agent with Quebec Nordiques, September 14, 1980.
  • Traded by Nordiques (with fourth-round selection (Ron Chyzowski) in 1983 entry draft) to Hartford Whalers for G John Garrett, January 12, 1982.

ACCOLADES:

  • Named to Quebec Junior Hockey League All-Star Second Team, 1967-68.

FIRSTS:

  • NHL debut: March 30, 1971, 4-3 victory at Los Angeles.

INJURIES:

  • Left game with eye injury, March 25, 1973. At 2:45 of the third period, Plasse (who had replaced starter Wayne Thomas in the second period) was clipped in the face with the stick of Chicago's Cliff Koroll. Plasse went to the ice, writhing for nearly five minutes before getting up and attempting to continue. Thomas re-entered the game at 6:12 of the third for Plasse, who appeared to be having trouble with his vision. After the game, Plasse took four stitches to the right eyelid.
  • Left game with head injury, March 2, 1974. Holding a 3-2 lead in the third period, Plasse stared down a rising slapshot from the right point off the stick of California's Hilliard Graves. Plasse stabbed at the puck with his glove, missed, and it struck him squarely high on the mask. Plasse dropped, and rolled inert on his right side and completely out of his crease. While his Canadiens teammates expected a whistle to stop play, instead California's Stan Weir swooped in for the loose puck, lifting the puck over Plasse's abandoned goal stick to tie the game. Michel Larocque replaced Plasse for the remainder of the game, while the injured netminder went to receive twelve stitches to close the wound over his right eye.
  • Left game with sprained ankle, March 26, 1975. Gary Inness entered the game for the Penguins at the 27:46 mark, and Pittsburgh defeated Montreal for the first time since December 25, 1971.
  • Suffered broken finger in right hand, December 2, 1977. The injury, which occurred in practice, kept Plasse out of action until late January.
  • Left game with severe muscle pull in upper leg / groin area, February 14, 1980. Plasse was replaced by Hardy Astrom 7:22 into the second period of Colorado's 5-3 loss to the New York Islanders.

DID YOU KNOW?

  • Michel was the first player selected in the 1968 NHL amateur draft, to the Montreal Canadiens.
  • On February 21, 1971, Michel became the first (known) professional hockey goaltender to score a goal, while with the Kansas City Blues of the Central Hockey League. From The Sporting News (March 6, 1971): "Goalies in the National Hockey League occasionally get credit for an assist, but none has ever scored a goal in the long history of the league. As far as available records show, it never happened in the minor leagues either, until Michel Plasse, Kansas City Blues' goalie, did it in a Central Hockey League game against Oklahoma City February 21. With 55 seconds to play, Oklahoma City took out its goalie and put six attackers on the ice in an effort to tie the Blues, who were leading, 2-1. Only 44 seconds were left when Plante took a poke at the puck and sent it down the ice into the unattended cage at the opposite end of the rink. The puck was presented to the 22-year-old goalie, who said "I'll keep it forever."
  • On February 1, 1977, Michel stopped 48 of 50 shots in a 5-2 Colorado win over the visiting New York Rangers.
