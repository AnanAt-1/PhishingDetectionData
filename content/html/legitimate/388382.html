Sharks, Bobby Williams ready to face Dallas Vigilantes

Don Coble

A year ago, Bobby Williams was getting ready for another NFL training camp. Now he's getting ready to make his first start for the Jacksonville Sharks of the Arena Football League.

And he couldn't be in a better place.

The current NFL lockout has crippled the career hopes of hundreds of players, especially those who've primarily worked on practice squads. The league has bigger issues than worrying about players on the margin, so Williams decided to make the best of a bad situation.

"Believe me, I needed the Sharks more than they needed me," he said.

Williams, a 2005 graduate of Raines High, will make his first start at Jacksonville Veterans Memorial Arena against the Dallas Vigilantes at 7 p.m. today.

The Sharks (13-2) already have clinched home-field advantage for the first two rounds of the playoffs. Jacksonville hopes to win its last three games and get a loss by Arizona to be able to host the ArenaBowl on Aug. 12.

Dallas (10-5) needs a win to qualify for the playoffs.

After playing on the practice squads for the Houston Texans and Buffalo Bills, Williams was signed three weeks ago when Jeron Harvey and Sale Key were placed on the injured list. Now he's a starter because Jamarko Simmons was signed by the Virginia Destroyers of the United Football League.

"I always wanted to come back, whether it's with the Sharks or with the Jaguars. I didn't care," he said. "Having this opportunity is great for me. I really needed this."

As long as the NFL is hammering out a new collective bargaining contract, teams aren't allowed to sign free agents. Williams didn't want to wait for a resolution, so he took a different path to his ultimate goal of playing in the NFL.

"Things didn't slow down for me with the lockout, it stopped," the 6-foot-3, 205-pound receiver said. "It's really a tough situation for guys like me to find work."

The indoor game was a tough adjustment, especially crashing into the sideline boards. The important thing, he said, is remaining relevant, compiling game film and creating statistics. When the NFL lockout is done, he should be in a better position to make a new deal.

"Bobby is good enough to play in the NFL," Sharks owner Jeff Bouchy said. "There's usually an adjustment by NFL players when they come to the arena league. Bobby has fit in really nice."

Jacksonville needs a new spark after a stunning 83-70 loss at San Jose last week that snapped the Sharks' league-record 13-game winning streak.

The winning streak, Williams said, proved to be a bigger distraction than expected. Now the team has a single focus - winning the championship.

"The circus act is over," he said. "Honestly, it's good the pressure of the winning streak is off. That's one less thing we have to worry about."
