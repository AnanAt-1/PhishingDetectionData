Free @thebig_sam campaign hots up

Posted on February 19, 2011 by Chariot on Fire

Breaking news from the football twittersphere – the popular Sam Allardyce parody account, @thebig_sam, has been suspended, sparking outrage among many of the site’s fans.

News of the World football correspondent Neil Ashton has revealed, via his own Twitter feed, that the former Blackburn boss took legal action through the League Managers Association to have the account shut down. The site – which imagine Allardyce’s masturbation habits as well as a penchant for sticking mint Cornettos up his arse – has angered the man affectionately known as Big Sam. He is reported to have labelled the posts “vulgar” and “disgusting”.

@thebig_sam has been sent to Twitter purgatory

Avid followers of the account have been taking to their keyboards and protesting to have the site reinstated, some even threatening self-immolation. Sports writer Iain Macintosh (who maintains one of the best kept Twitter feeds in football) heads up the campaign, firing off this diatribe earlier today.

Twitter have yet to release a statement confirming whether the @thebig_sam Twitter feed will be reinstated as a result of this protest. In the meantime, an impostor has opened an account under the @Not_TheBig_Sam and has been making a gallant, yet ultimately ham-fisted attempt to ape the original feed’s style.

In the event that there is no second coming, whenfootballerstweet brings you this round-up of the best shots fired from the big_sam canon …

Been working with gamma radiation on the lads. Trying to see if I can improve their physical strength. Results have been disappointing.

My new Uggs just arrived from Littlewoods. Instant fabulous footwear & no payment required until October 2011? Big Sam is ahead of the pack.

Gonna take advantage of the sun and pace up & down my garden, wearing only a pair of linen trousers. Let the neighbours know what I’m about.

Had a bit of a scare there. Thought I’d found a grey patch on my pubes. It’s okay though – it was only dried-in Yop.

Saw a lovely lass in the park. Told her she was as pretty as a Monet. And that I’d like to suck her shit into a point. Mardy cow walked off.

In a right bad mood today. Someone took a shit in my empty milk bottles again. That’s the fourth time in the last week it’s happened.

Trying to come up with a nickname for the wife’s genitalia. My preferred suggestions so far are “cookie”, “elderflower” and “cock-socket”.

Just had a wank with an oven glove on. The sensation was electric, but I’ve got bits of Findus Crispy Pancake all over my cock now.

Just had a Shepherd’s Pie sandwhich while the missus gave us a pedicure. Honestly, I often think I’ll be punished for living so lavishly

My mate Fusty Steve says I don’t have the guts to shove a Cornetto up my arse. We’ll see about that.

Have some of that, Steve. Whether it’s mind games, technological prowess or inserting ice creams into my anus – Big Sam can’t be beat.

Ninja farts, Marmite, steak eating and the punctuation police

Posted on February 18, 2011 by Chariot on Fire

Cillian Sheridan’s Twitter feed is a rarity among the slew of sites maintained by sportsmen – it’s intentionally funny. The former Celtic man’s musings are often wry observations of what life as a professional footballer is really like. We find that it’s not all champagne and super-injunctions – there’s a sense of melancholy there too. If Sofia Coppola were to script a footballer’s Twitter feed it would read like this.

“I think I deserve a medal of bravery. I just farted repeatedly in a disco. #balls,” the CSKA Sofia player wrote this week, in three sentences exploding those glamorous images of footballers in V.I.P lounges. Does Danielle Lloyd really spend her Saturday nights engulfed in guff?

Sheridan also shows that even professional athletes struggle with basic hand-eye coordination based tasks. “Hate when I lose a perfectly good piece of bread to hard butter,” he moaned last week.

The striker also has a keen eye for exposing life’s foibles. At times his Twitter feed plays out like a Seinfeld skit. This week he wrote: “Don’t understand why people applaud the pilot for landing. Surely it should be expected! If he crashlands do you just give him a pat on the back and say hard luck, I’m sure you’ll get it next time?!”

Meanwhile, pasty former Birmingham player Mikael Forssell isn’t one for sunbeds. “My teammates tell keep telling me 2 take off my white t-shirt before I shower.I keep telling them Im not wearing a white t-shirt…not funny,” the striker tweeted. Apologies if picturing that scene led to you visualising the ivory-white naked body of Mikael Forssell.

Robbie Savage’s current Twitter avatar is a picture of a tub of Marmite. This is an effective visual metaphor for one of two reasons: 1. He divides opinion. 2. He looks like shit (depending on how you feel about Ugg boots and teaming foppish waistcoats and blazers with jeans). Fashion aside, Savage’s Twitter continues to raise a titter. “I think gattuso was trying to knock jordorns teeth out then realised he didn’t have any !!come on spurs,” he said of the kafuffle after Tuesday night’s Champions’ League tie.

As well as being a platform for words, Twitter can be a great place to trade images. West Ham’s Danny Gabbidon has been doing this to good effect lately. One team bonding activity – watching defender Manu Da Costa eat a massive steak – was captured perfectly by these before and after pictures:

Not to be outdone, his team-mate Carlton Cole also served up a sublime picture from the club’s recent clay pigeon shooting excursion. Here is French defender Julian Faubert looking like the nu rave Joe 90.

Faubert: Mad Max at the disco

Many fans seemed miffed by the idea of the players indulging in some R&R – shouldn’t they be on the training ground trying to work out how to keep a clean sheet? “I hope all this fart arsing around means that you are going to score more goals and we’re going to win more games,” wrote Hammer’s fan Pedrodelfuego. But Cole made efforts to assuage these fears with this tweet: “Had a really good day w/ my team mates! Team spirit is gona b vital 4 da rest of da season! Team spirit is a vital weapon to have! Sometimes u cant just get this on match days n trainin pitch!”

Elsewhere, more words of wisdom from the inimitable Jody Morris: “So for all you guys out there who gets dominated in ya house..grow a set..enjoy bridget jones though yeah..for prob the 5/6th time!!” Would you dare touch the former Chelsea man’s remote?

And finally, we live in a brave new word of ROFLs, LMAOs and Finnegan’s Wake-like punctuation. But at least standards of literacy are being upheld by Rio Ferdinand, well according to probably-deranged fan Rubytuesday2810 anyway. Ferdinand re-tweeted a message she sent him earlier in the week: “I know its a bit of a cliche, but I can’t believe how literate you are for a footballer! Spelling too-impressive» haha!” At the risk of sounding pedantic, she missed an apostrophe in there …

Air Rooney, Liverpool baiting and bad karma

Posted on February 13, 2011 by Chariot on Fire

Taken in silhouette form it’s easy to imagine it becoming one of sport’s enduring images, as synonymous with football as the Air Jordan icon is with basketball. He hangs there for an eternity, suspended in space with all of the ease of a holidaymaker in a hammock. But suddenly time speeds up and Wayne Rooney, arse over tit, swings his boot and thuds the ball past a mesmerised Joe Hart.

Rooney’s called it his best ever goal while critics have lacquered it so thick with superlatives that you could be forgiven for thinking that they were describing the Second Coming. But one observer wasn’t so impressed. St. Johnstone midfielder Jody Morris laughed off suggestions that it was the best goal Old Trafford had ever seen. “Hahaha best goal ever at old trafford…do me a favour…what an insult,” Morris tweeted as the rest of the country readjusted their jaws. “I’ve seen a lot of good goals at old trafford…rooneys shinnie don’t get in my top ten!!!let alone best ever at the ground,” the former Chelsea player added.

Jody Morris: chief Liverpool tormentor

Morris’ Twitter site is a rare treat in a time when many professional football players resemble media-managed droids, spewing sound bites in every interview. His feed is mostly a deranged mix of incendiary rants and outpourings of an atavistic hatred towards Liverpool Football Club. This Saturday was a fine example: with St. Johnstone’s league fixture against Aberdeen called-off due to a waterlogged pitch, Morris spent his afternoon baiting Liverpool supporters. Delighted by the Anfield side’s dissapointing draw with Wigan, Morris stirred up his rivals with this post: “Oi liverpool muggy fans???where are you…hahahhaa embarrassing!!!”

Predictably, Morris’ tweet triggered an avalanche of abuse. An enraged BlaiseC5, who resides in – you guessed it – Liverpool, tweeting: “I hope your kids grow chickens heads.” While Wrenny84 gets even more creative with these insults: “You reek of a creep. Such a horrible little cretin. Pipe down or il f*****g knock u down u pest”. To be fair Morris was determined to give as good as he got, taking the time to personally reply to, and insult his adversaries. “Good 1 bus head..you look like an unshaven joe bugnor,” he told one rival fan.

This site strongly recommends that you check out Morris’ feed while you can. Surely it’s only a matter of time before he is either fined by the SFA (yesterday the BBC reported that English players were being warned by the English FA over their use of social networking sites), or bludgeoned to death by an angry Liverpool supporters’ club. Until that day comes, enjoy.

Now, juxtapose what you have just read with this tweet from Rio Ferdinand: “Twitter+facebook(social media)played a significant role in the revolution in Egypt.We are involved(if not directly)in a powerful #movement !” Wise words from the England defender there (although WFT can’t help but muse over how you can be involved with something without being directly involved with it).

Meanwhile, West Ham’s Carlton Cole, who burst his lip in a penalty box tussle yesterday, was pleased to assure fans that his injuries weren’t too serious. “My lips in better shape today! Still feel like ive had botox on one side of my face though,” he wrote.

And finally, Bolton’s Stuart Holden used his Twitter feed to send an open letter to whoever broke into his car. “Dear person that smashed my Side and Trunk windows in my car yest, hope u enjoyed finding nothing. What goes arond comes around! #badkarma,” the midfielder tweeted (while elsewhere in Bolton a piano dropped on thief’s head).

Dirk Kite, a Savage gypsy bride and what footballers read in the bath

Posted on February 9, 2011 by Chariot on Fire

Football’s own pantomime villain, Robbie Savage, has again been treating his Twitter followers to his unique brand of acerbic punditry. Commenting on Sunday’s Liverpool versus Chelsea match, Savage said the following of Dutch forward Dirk Kuyt: “It should be dirk kite not kuyt as he is towing a massive one!!! How slow.” Savage’s brutal post-match summary claimed another victim: Chelsea’s new record signing, Fernando Torres. Savage, unimpressed by the sulky Spaniard’s efforts, tweeting: “Ray wilkins just said I think Torres has done ok he’s been shite.”

As reported previously on whenfootballerstweet, Savage devotes a lot of time to maiming Twitter nemesis Rio Ferdinand’s reputation. The pair’s latest online duel culminating in Savage mocking Ferdinand’s injured calf. “Mate here’s a couple of new ones for sat for you don’t pull them again,” wrote the Welshman, before posting this image.

Bovine aid for Ferdinand

To be fair, Savage was provoked – Ferdinand earlier comparing him to a newlywed on Channel 4’s popular My Big Fat Gypsy Wedding. Worse still, Ferdinand wasn’t referring to the groom: “@RobbieSavage8 could pass as a bride on this programme!!”

Twitter is, at its best, like reading someone’s diary (at worst, as is the case with some celebrities, it’s more like reading the instruction manual for a dildo). Former Chelsea player Jody Morris shares some of his more intimate moments on his Twitter site. “Can’t beat a read in a steaming hot bath,” Morris told his followers yesterday. But what was Morris reading? What kind of books do footballers read while bathing? “This months FHM by the way!!! So yeah I know…not exactly a read as in “book”!!some great pics though,” the St. Johnstone player explained.

As well as book clubs searching for recommended reading, Sheffield Wednesday supporters will also be interested in Morris’ feed. Last Monday he had an intriguing conversation with current Wednesday striker Clinton Morrison regarding the merits of new boss Gary Megson. Megson, the uncle that nobody wants, has, according to Morrison, made a strong first impression: “@morriskid he seems good but it seems you mess with him he will just say see you later.”

The pair also discuss Megson’s training methods, Morrison tweeting that he expects “alot of hard work and running”. While Morris offers Wednesday players some consolation with this response: “haha I know a lot of players who have played under him..I heard he’s eased up abit on the running be fair!!”

Elsewhere, Cillian Sheridan keeps up his account of the exiled footballer’s life. The former Celtic player signed for Bulgarian side CSKA Sofia last August and, if his Twitter account is anything to go by, has had a mixed experience so far. At times, it seems like the striker would rather be elsewhere: “Can’t believe I’m missing the old firm. Instead off to another boring practice match #fuckpreseason.” But the same old gripes are apparent for goalscorers, even on distant shores: “I hate keepers.” Still, at least Sheridan has grasped the opportunity to immerse himself in different cultures: “Watching the #superbowl in Turkey with German commentary! Even Rosetta Stone would struggle.”

And finally, Times’ football writer Tony Barrett looks ahead to England’s international friendly against Denmark with boundless enthusiasm: “Is anyone even remotely excited by tonight’s England friendly. Rather watch Sunday league myself.”

Wilshere returns toys to pram for fear of being sent to live in Germany

Posted on February 5, 2011 by Chariot on Fire

Arsenal’s pubescent ne’er-do-well Jack Wilshere has deleted an earlier post he made criticising referee Phil Dowd after today’s 4-4 draw with Newcastle. The midfielder – who was only recently allowed out past ten o’clock unattended – was angered when Dowd sent-off teammate Abou Diaby in the 48th minute for pushing Newcastle’s Joey Barton, but allowed Kevin Nolan to stay in the game despite committing a similar offence later in the match.

In between mouthfuls of sherbet dip, an angered Wilshere wrote: “Inconsistent refereing needs to stop.its killing the game.if Diaby goes..whats the difference between that and Nolan on our keeper!?? #joke.”

Wilshere could face disciplinary action from the Football Association for openly criticising a match official. But whether the FA hs the power to ground Wilshere or to confiscate his Nintendo Wii is unclear.

A similar situation occurred when last month when former Liverpool striker Ryan Babel was fined £10,000 by the FA for a doctored photo he posted of referee Howard Webb in a Manchester United shirt, following a 1-0 defeat for Kenny Dalglish’s team at Old Trafford. Babel’s punishment didn’t end there though: he was also transferred to Bundesliga side Hoffenheim and sent to live in Germany.

Ryan Babel’s spelling bee, Gary Neville’s brain and Jack Wilshere’s hormones

Posted on February 5, 2011 by Chariot on Fire

Ryan Babel has moved to counter claims that he is not even a halfwit. Apparently some eagle-eyed observers have noted that the former Liverpool forward’s standard of literacy is somewhat lacking. An enraged Babel tweeting: “Anybody ever notice we go tooo many english teachers on twitter,, tellin ppl u spelt sumptin rong?” Clearly there is no better way to show up such pedants than by chastising them with a message containing a string of spelling errors.

Meanwhile, insufficiently bearded hate figure Gary Neville has mirrored other famed villains such as Richard Nixon and Gerry Adams by choosing to refer to himself in the 3rd person in interviews. Neville broke the unwritten code of ego etiquette during an interview aired on the BBC’s Football Focus programme. This led to Focus presenter Dan Walker opening up a Pandora’s box of Neville wrath when he posed the following question: “No swearing allowed but what did you think of Gary Neville referring to himself in the 3rd person on Focus? Is it ever acceptable?” The phrase  ‘a red rag to a bull’ springs to mind. Avid tweeter  Mike Martin – a committed Robbie Savage botherer – came up with this imaginative response: “Gary Neville is worse than nuclear war.” While Dave Smyth, a “proud England cricket fan” from Cheshire, questioned Neville’s sanity with this post: “The people I know who do it are officially bonkers see my job on my profile.” In case you’re wondering, Smyth works as a psychiatric nurse.

Elsewhere, cherubic Arsenal midfielder Jack Wilshere may face a slap on the wrists for this emotional tweet after seeing his side surrender a four goal lead to Andy Carroll-less Newcastle: “Inconsistent refereing needs to stop.its killing the game.if Diaby goes..whats the difference between that and Nolan on our keeper!?? #joke.”  The teenaged Wilshere, still battling with the onset of puberty, will soon learn that football, like life, is never fair.

And finally, new Huddersfield Town Danny Cadamarteri has shown that he is living, tweeting proof that footballers aren’t imbeciles. During his time at Dundee United Cadamarteri learnt a new language: Scottish. “Ali bali Ali bali bee sitten on ye mamis knee greetin for a wee bobee to buy some kuters candy ,” tweeted the former Everton wunderkind. Perhaps Cadamarteri isn’t quite the language savant he seems though – the above is a traditional Scottish song that the forward sang for his new teammates as part of an initiation ceremony. Describing the scene, Cadamarteri tweets: “Well was welcomed back with a bang last night…. Had to sing on a chair at the front of the room for the whole team and the staff ….nice.”

Gary Neville’s ‘tache, why Ugg boots are wrong and a chilling warning for Eddie’s Dad

Posted on February 5, 2011 by Chariot on Fire

The announcement of Gary Neville’s retirement sparked a flurry of Twitter activity. Manchester United team-mate Rio Ferdinand was one of the first on the scene, tweeting: “Nev is loyal, funny, dedicated and talented….a true man utd legend. Gave his all for the club he supported+won a load of medals.”

Despite Ferdinand’s efforts to eulogise his fellow defender’s career, the gesture was met with a wave of 140 character hate crimes from fans of rival clubs (most of which would make even a hardened sailor blush and are entirely unprintable). Chris Marksby, who describes himself as an “All Round Baffoon”, offers this blunt response: “He was terrible.” While a clearly incensed Paul Shanley (presumably a Liverpool supporter) tweets that Neville “looks like some1 took a bat to his kipper”.

Bewildering insults from fans aside, Neville’s fellow professionals were a lot kinder. Former Chelsea midfielder Jody Morris, now plying his trade with St. Johnstone, will be among those who will miss the spectacle of Neville’s mustachioed face goading Liverpool fans. “That’s a shame bout G neville..what a legend!!man utd your whole career doesn’t happen to many..utmost respect for him I must say,” Morris typed, no-doubt holding back the tears.

Meanwhile, Ferdinand had his irons in other fires yesterday. When he wasn’t rhapsodising Gary Neville’s glorious career he was being decidedly less kind about that of another former United player’s. Ferdinand making the following appraisal of Robbie Savage’s playing days: “Best moment in your career was pulling on the man utd youth team kit!!” A picture paints a thousand words, so instead of trying to shoehorn a suitable retort into 140 characters, Savage posted this iconic image with this caption: “Here’s the one where Rooney gives you a lift to work on the a34”.

This isn’t the first time that Ferdinand and Savage have jousted on Twitter, or indeed in person. The pair were famously involved in a post-match tussle in 2006 and the fight spilled onto the internet last year.  Ferdinand’s feelings for Savage are perhaps best encapsulated in this vitriolic post from November: “U seem 2 have a lot a trouble with haters u??? Must be your face?? Hair?? Fake tan?? Teeth?? Ugg boots?? Highlights??” While a clearly enraged Savage – no-doubt stung by the criticism of his Ugg boots – mocked the frequency of Ferdinand’s visits to the treatment table with this tweet: “Which bodily part have you injured now pal !”

Elsewhere, Wolves forward Steven Fletcher raised the ire of Scotland supporters by snubbing his latest international call-up via text – the rationale among the Tartan Army being that you never refuse a dance with your home nation, even if she is one of the most ungainly wallflowers at the international football disco. Fletcher posted the following as a way of justifying his decision: “Just like to clarify I didn’t say I retire just asked to b excused from this friendly and as per got taken to far!” Clearly the thought of never again representing his country won’t keep Fletcher awake at night, the former Hibernian forward also tweeting: “Hopefully b bk in squad 1 day but for now I’m goin for a sleep as we hav a massive game 2nyt.”

Ever wondered what footballers do with their vast expanses of free time? Former Birmingham striker Mikael Forssell gives us a valuable insight with this tweet: “OMG only one session today which means:Im gonna be the biggest favela-mallrat ever! I dont know why but I like hanging out in malls…creepy.” Not sure what is more confusing; why a 29 year-old man has similar hobbies to a 14 year-old girl, or how the mall he frequents is similar to a Brazilian shanty town. The mind boggles.

Meanwhile, the man responsible for that post has left all of his followers gasping for more. Former Liverpool striker Ryan Babel posting this cryptic message: “I wanted 2 tweet something but, nahh I slapped myself before I did it.. Would got me in trouble again LOL ( Shaking my head Again ).” You big tease.

And finally, Crystal Palace midfielder Neil Danns used his Twitter site to quash speculation that he has his heart set on a move away from Selhurt Park at the end of the season. In a series of tweets – conjoined here for your convenience – Danns refutes claims that he has a wandering eye: “My head has not been turned by any speculation, I have simply been playing through a bit of pain to do my part, so to all the people that claim they no the reasons why I’m not supposedly giving my all, or not signing a contract, can you stop lying, to all others your support is is brilliant”. Not without a hint of menace, Danns continues, “this isn’t a rant lool, I’m in good spirits and just want to clear it up for the real palace fans, so someone tell the user eddies dad on the cbbs forum, to stop lying , opinions are cool but lying is not.” Presumably Eddie’s Dad will be sleeping with one eye open tonight, or, at the very least, conjuring up another imaginative username.
