Some Descendants of Thomas Cole of Salem, Massachusetts

Some Descendants of Thomas Cole
of Salem, Massachusetts Who Moved to
Belknap Co., New Hampshire

Note:  This is NOT my Cole line, so I am no longer doing research on it, but for a short time I thought it might be mine, since my grandfather, Charles C. Cole, was born in Belknap Co., New Hampshire.  Prior to discovering this was not my Cole line, I had accumulated some information on it, which I now think might be helpful to other Cole research­ers, which is why it is here, since we all run into “blind alleys” in this hobby. This also resulted from a collaboration with another Cole researcher, who sent me copies of her research on this Cole line and I sent her copies of mine. There is a list of the 10 sources used in compiling this record located below the genealogical information. You can also use the find or search feature on your web browser to search for a specific name. There are 202 individual names in this record, which focuses mainly on the family members who moved from Salem to Boxford, Massachusetts, and from there to Belknap Co., New Hampshire.

For those unfamiliar with commonly used genealogical terms, “ca.” is an abbreviation for the Latin word circa which means “about.”  Also an intent to marry was usually filed with the local church about 4-6 weeks prior to the ceremony to give an opportunity for anyone who objected to the mar­riage to come forward with his or her objection.  Many times the intent to marry was filed in the records of the town of one person’s birth, but the marriage record will be found in another Massachusetts town, where the ceremony took place.

Second Generation

One authority says he moved to Boxford in 1717, and d. ‘very suddenly’ in 1737, aged 68 years; another says he was a cooper by trade and lived in Salem till about 1675, when he re­moved to Malden, and about 1684 to Lynn, where he died intestate, 8 Oct 1703. He made a will dated 5 Oct 1703, which was not probated, as it only had two wit­nesses.  Additionally the same source states:  “[John Cole] married (second), between 1675 and 1686, Sarah Alsbee, who was tried for witchcraft at Charlestown, and acquitted February 1, 1693. John Cole’s children by the first wife were: John, Thomas, Mary and Hannah; and by the second wife: Samuel and Anna.”

Third Generation

  1. John Cole, born 18 May 1668, Salem, Essex Co., Massachusetts, married Mary Eaton, born ca. 1668, who died 7 Oct 1746, Boxford, Essex Co., Massachusetts. John died 5 Feb 1737, Boxford. The following is from Appendix F, p. 400, of the book The History of Boxford, Essex Co., Massachusetts:

    “Cole — John of Lynn (ante of Malden), cooper by trade; m. Sarah ______, and had ch.: Samuel, b. 27 Dec., 1687, and Anna, 5 Aug., 1690. In 1717 Samuel came to Boxford with his father and for one hundred ten pounds, purchased of Ebenezer Burbank the farm on which his posterity have resided until within a few years (the farm of the late Mr. Manly H. Cole, in the West Parish). This was the tract of sixty-seven acres laid out to Thomas Leaver in 1666. John Cole, the father, d. ‘very sud­denly,’ 1737, a. sixty-eight years. Samuel died 1765, and his widow Susanna, 1785, a. ninety-five years. Samuel’s children were: Samuel, John, Rebecca, Susanna, and Mary. The last-mentioned Samuel (who was great-grandfather of Mr. D. M. Cole) had a family of fifteen children. John removed to Amherst, N.H. about 1763.”

Their children were:
