‘Meet Mattress Mack’ set Wednesday at Lee College

  • Lee College
  • Oct 7, 2011
  • Oct 7, 2011
  • 0

  • Twitter
  • WhatsApp
  • SMS
  • Email
  • Facebook

His fast-talking pitches, signature catch phrase, entrepreneurial spirit and philanthropic efforts have made Jim “Mattress Mack” McIngvale a Houston icon, and on Wednesday, Oct. 12, area students and residents will have the opportunity to meet the man, the myth and the legend, when Lee College hosts Meet Mattress Mack.

Sponsored by the Lee College Campus Activities Board, the free event features an opening reception, light refreshments, and a special presentation by the Houston entrepreneur. 
“It’s an honor to welcome Jim ‘Mattress Mack’ McIngvale to the Lee College campus,” said Student Activities Director Mike Spletter. “This is an opportunity for Lee College students, faculty, staff and friends to meet one of Houston’s most well-known business leaders and philanthropists, and learn more about how he built Gallery Furniture from the ground up.”
Armed with “$5,000 and a dream,” McIngvale and his wife, Linda, established Gallery Furn-iture in 1981. 
Thirty years later, their venture has morphed into one of the top furniture businesses in the country, according to Furniture Today.
With just two locations, Gallery Furniture sells the most furniture in the nation per square foot, and generated $100 million in annual sales in 2006.
In addition to his record-breaking sales and memorable commercials, McIngvale is also known as one of Houston’s most dedicated philanthropists. Examples of his charitable campaigns include:
• Providing Thanksgiving dinner for 25,000 people; 
• Donating 30 households of furniture each Christmas; 
• Buying and distributing 10,000 toys to children during the holidays; 
• Furnishing all 136 USO facilities, world-wide and hosting events for the Salvation Army; 
• Donating furniture to hundreds of teacher lounges in Houston area schools; 
• Helping raise more than $12 million for the Bush/Clinton fund used to aid both; 
• Hurricane Katrina and Tsunami victims; 
• Underwriting Reliant Stadium’s YMCA playground for Hurricane Katrina Evacuees, and housing several hundred evacuees during the crisis; 
• Serving as one of the largest contributors to the Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo; 
• Furnishing the George Bush Presidential Library at Texas A&M; 
• Providing a $4 million donation to help the Meneger Clinic provide mental health care in Houston; 
• One of the largest contributors to both the Baylor College of Medicine and Harvard Medical School.
“Mr. McIngvale is a living testament to the value of hard work and persistence,” Spletter continued. “We hope that once students hear his story, they’ll realize that if they apply themselves, if they never give up, they too will achieve great success.” 
Meet Mattress Mack will be held at 7 p.m. Wednesday, Oct. 12, in Tucker Hall. The event is free and open to the public. For additional information, contact the Student Activities Office at 281-425-6861.
