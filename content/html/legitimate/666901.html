    Richards - Jones a future captain

    Wednesday 5 October 2011 20:43, UK

    •

    Micah Richards believes Manchester United defender Phil Jones has the attributes to captain England in future.

    City stopper full of praise for Red Devils defender

    Micah Richards believes Manchester United defender Phil Jones has the attributes to captain England in future. Jones was snapped up by The Red Devils from Blackburn Rovers over the summer and the talented teenager has already made a huge impression during his short time at Old Trafford. The duo are in contention to fill the right-back role in Montenegro with John Terry and Gary Cahill expected to retain their partnership at the back, when England only require a point to seal their place at Euro 2012. Although Manchester City defender Richards knows the pair are enemies on club duty, but he cannot help but admire him while at England's training camp.

    Captain

    "Phil Jones is going to be England captain one day," Richards told The Guardian. "He's got everything: pace, reads the game well, is good on the ball. "He's at the right club as well because Sir Alex Ferguson promotes young players."
    Patience
    Richards has been in superb form for City this season and he is hoping to get the nod against Montenegro with Chris Smalling (groin) unavailable. The 23-year-old has won 12 caps for his country but only one since Fabio Capello's appointment and he is keen to show the Italian has he has developed and matured of late. "People always say there aren't many right-backs," he continued. "But Chris Smalling is doing a good job for Manchester United. I rate Glen Johnson and Kyle Walker's been doing very well. "I think I'm twice the player I was when I made my England debut at 19. There's no reason I can't get in there but it's about biding my time."
