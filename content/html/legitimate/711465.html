Who Needs to Complete the Indiana Driver Safety Program?

Did you get a letter in the mail from the BMV instructing you to complete an Indiana Driver Safety Program course or risk suspension of your license? Have your driving privileges already been suspended? If so, you are in the right place.

You can complete our course once every 3 years to meet court requirements or remove up to 4 points from your driving record. The Driver Safety Program can benefit Indiana Drivers in several ways.

According to Indiana law, drivers who are convicted of two or more traffic violations within one year may be required to complete a Driver Safety Program.

Drivers under 21 may be required to complete the course if they are convicted of two or more traffic offenses or are involved in two or more accidents. Failing to complete the Driver Safety Program within 90 days of these convictions can result in a suspended license.

Indiana courts may also allow you to complete the course to avoid having your license suspended.

Points for Indiana Traffic Violations

Every Indiana traffic ticket you receive comes with a point penalty, and these points will stay active on your Indiana BMV driving record two years from the conviction date of the citation. Tickets for speeding violations can add as many as 6 points to your record.

Fortunately, our online Driver Safety Program course will remove 4 points from your driving record! Keeping a clean record is extremely important because your driver's license can be suspended or placed on probation if you get 18 or more points within a two-year period.

We encourage you to do everything possible to keep your Indiana BMV driving record clean and free of any points. Save time and money by taking our hassle-free Driver Safety Program. Register for our course today and enjoy the benefits of a clean driving record!
