Seattle Seahawks introduce wide receiver T.J. Houshmandzadeh

  • Published: Mar. 03, 2009, 3:36 p.m.
By
  • Aaron Fentress | The Oregonian/OregonLive
The Associated Press
Wide receiver T.J. Houshmandzadeh is introduced to the media in Seattle on Tuesday after signing a free agent contract. He will switch from No. 84, which he wore in Cincinnati, to No. 18, the jersey number he wore at Oregon State.

Wide receiver T.J. Houshmandzadeh left Seattle for Minnesota on Saturday convinced after a two-day visit with the Seahawks that they were the team for him.

Still, he decided to honor his commitment to at least meet with the Vikings. That prompted a confident, yet cautious, Seattle coach Jim Mora to seek a little insurance that Houshmandzadeh would indeed return."I said, 'you know what I respect you as a man and I think you should honor your commitment and to go to Minnesota,'" Mora said. "'But before you do you should take that watch off and leave it here as collateral."

Houshmandzadeh, 31, said he slipped off the expensive watch without hesitation. Mora said he would have returned the diamond watch even if Houshmandzadeh had signed with the Vikings. However...

"I'm glad he was able to come back and pick it up in person," Mora said.

The Seahawks introduced Houshmandzadeh Tuesday during a press conference at the Virginia Mason Athletic Center in Renton, Wash., after signing the top receiver on the free agent market to a five-year contract worth $40 million with $15 million guaranteed.

The Associated Press
T.J. Houshmandzadeh said he felt most comfortable in Seattle. And the money wasn't bad either.

The former Oregon State star said he chose the Seahawks over the Vikings, and returning to Cincinnati where he played for eight seasons, because he felt at home in Seattle and expects the team to return to its recent winning ways after a 4-12 season in 2008.

"The last five years Seattle has basically owned the (NFC West) division," Houshmandzadeh said. "They had a bad year last year and you can look at the injuries and say that's probably the biggest reason why. So, I'm just looking forward to coming here and dong my part."

Seattle general manager Tim Ruskell said Tuesday that bolstering the wide receiver corps was a top priority this offseason.

However, prior to the free agency period beginning last Friday, Ruskell said he didn't expect to be a major player in the process. That changed the second Houshmandzadeh expressed interest in playing for Seattle.

"Free agency is a funny thing," Ruskell said. "You have to chase it and be very flexible."

Smiling widely on Tuesday was Mora, who said he envisions creating matchup problems for opposing defenses with the team's top trio of wide receivers, Houshmandzadeh, Deion Branch and Nate Burleson.

"T.J. is what we are looking for in a Seattle Seahawk," Mora said. "He's a man of high character. He's a guy that puts the team first. He's a pro in every sense of the word. He brings a physical presence to the field. He adds toughness to our team, a swagger, and as we know a load of productivity."

Over the past three seasons, Houshmandzadeh ranks first in the NFL in receptions (294), 11th in receiving yards (3,128), tied for sixth in touchdown receptions (25).

Ruskell said the signing of Houshmandzadeh would not affect how the team approaches April's draft. Seattle has the fourth-overall pick and many have speculated that it might select Texas Tech wide receiver Michael Crabtree.

"It really doesn't put any restrictions on us," Ruskell said.

Houshmandzadeh said he would wear No. 18 for the Seahawks, the number he wore at OSU.

Steven Nehl/The Oregonian
Houshmandzadeh became an unexpected star at Oregon State in 2000.
Humble beginnings

Born in Victorville, Calif., Houshmandzadeh dropped out of Barstow High School in Barstow, Calif., but earned his GED and played junior college football at Cerritos College (1997-1998).

When former Seattle Seahawks coach Dennis Erickson became the head coach at Oregon State in 1999, Houshmandzadeh said he hoped he would get an offer from the Beavers.

"Actually, coach Erickson, once he got the job at Oregon State it was made up in my mind that if they offered me a scholarship I was going to take it," Houshmandzadeh said. "And they did that. Everybody told me about his reputation and I had heard about him at Miami, but I was so young, and once he got the job it just made me want to go play for him."

When he arrived in Corvallis, however, his initial impression wasn't so great.

"Man, I wanted to go him," he said. "It rained too much. I'll never forget that when I got to Corvallis it rained like 30 days straight."

Houshmandzadeh became a starter alongside Chad Johnson, now Chad Ocho Cinco, in 2000 and helped lead the Beavers to the Fiesta Bowl championship by catching 48 passes for 730 yards and seven touchdowns.

Houshmandzadeh said Erickson's connections to the NFL probably helped him become a late-round draft pick.

Time in Cincinnati

Houshmandzadeh described his time in Cincinnati as a learning experience filled with growing pains and ups and downs. He entered the league with little fanfare as a seventh round pick in 2001.

The Associated Press
T.J. Houshmandzadeh entered the NFL in 2001 as a seventh-round pick by the Cincinnati Bengals.

"There's a lot of teams that passed up on me in the draft and so, they gave me an opportunity to play, albeit I felt it was a little too late when they did give it to me," Houshmandzadeh said.

He would catch just 62 passes for 720 yards and one touchdown during his first three seasons before having a break through year in 2004 with 978 receiving yards and four touchdowns on 73 receptions.

He leaves Cincinnati with 507 receptions for 5,782 yards and 37 touchdowns in eight seasons.

"I'll always be thankful to them," he said.

The Bengals had a high-powered offense for much of Houshmandzadeh's time there. But they were never able to win consistently and last year, in part due to an injury to quarterback Carson Palmer, Cincinnati fell to 4-11-1.

"It was tough to have the talent that we had and not kind of capitalize on the situations and opportunities we had in those last few years," Houshmandzadeh said.

For the first time in nine years, including one (2000) at Oregon State, Houshmandzadeh won't be playing alongside the often-flamboyant Ocho Cinco, who legally changed his name last season.

The Associated Press
Former OSU star wide receivers, T.J. Houshmandzadeh (left) and Chad Ocho Cinco spent eight years together in Cincinnati.

"His locker has been next to mine that long," he said. "It will be different but, like I say, you got to go on the practice field, you got to practice, you got to go out on that game field and you've got to beat that guy in front of you. So, that's not going to change. He can't help me beat a guy in front of me and I can't help him."

When asked if things will be quieter in Seattle without Johnson next to him, Houshmandzadeh said: "He was quiet in the locker room. He wasn't as loud as you guys think he was."

Instant impact expected

Seattle's wide receivers combined to miss 56 games because of injury last season. Quarterback Matt Hasselbeck missed nine games. The entire starting offensive line was on the injured reserve list before the end of the season.

That pretty much summed up Seattle's fall to 4-12 after winning four-straight NFC West Division titles.

But the franchise doesn't believe its run of excellence is done and that if healthy, it can make a run at the Super Bowl. Signing Houshmandzadeh gives the offense a reliable wide receiver, and a big target at 6-1, 197 pounds, something it has lacked.

Plus, Houshmandzadeh can play any wide receiver position, having excelled in the slot at Cincinnati. With slot receiver Bobby Engram expected to move on, acquiring a wide receiver that could effectively work the slot was a big get for Hasselbeck and the team.

As for adjusting to the West Coast offense, Houshmandzadeh said he's always felt he could fit well in that system.

"Me, personally, I think I can play in any offense," he said. "I can play in the triple-option if they need me to. So, I think I'll be fine."

The Associated Press
T.J. Houshmandzadeh will add size and versatility to Seattle's passing offense.

Mora said the team would lean heavily on Houshmandzadeh.

"We'll use him each week in the way that's best to win that game," Mora said. "We won't put any parameters on it we'll just try to be as creative as we can."

Houshmandzadeh said he was glad the free agency process was over.

"When I left to Minnesota I felt like this was the place I wanted to be," he said. "But I wanted to make sure that was the case so that's why I went to Minnesota."

He added that his wife Kaci, and two young daughters, Karrington and Kennedi, were quite taken with Mora and the city, which helped make his decision.

"They were asking about coach Mora when we left," Houshmandzadeh said.

Plus, Mora did have his watch.

Note: Seattle now has four pass catchers on its roster with ties to the state of Oregon, Houshmandzadeh, wide receivers Mike Hass (OSU) and Jordan Kent (UO), and tight end Joe Newton (OSU).
