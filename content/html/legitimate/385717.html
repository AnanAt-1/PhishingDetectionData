ABOUT OUR ORGANIZATION

ICLEI – Local Governments for Sustainability

ICLEI – Local Governments for Sustainability (known as ICLEI) is a global network working with more than 2500 local and regional governments committed to sustainable urban development. Active in 125+ countries, we influence sustainability policy and drive local action for zero emission, nature-based, equitable, resilient and circular development. Our Members and team of experts work together through peer exchange, partnerships and capacity building to create systemic change for urban sustainability.

WHO WE ARE

Our Network in Numbers

Local and regional governments across the ICLEI network represent a diversity of cities, towns and regions. Collectively, they form a dynamic force advancing sustainable urban development worldwide.

Our network
FEATURED CITY

Federal City of Bonn, Germany

Located along the Rhine River, Bonn is a global center for sustainability and international cooperation, hosting 17 UN agencies and over 170 climate and nature-focused institutions, including the ICLEI World Secretariat. An ICLEI Member since 2000, the city is led by Lord Mayor Katja Dörner, also Chair of ICLEI’s Climate Action and Governance Portfolio. Bonn is recognized as a Global Resilience Hub under the MCR2030 initiative and is known for forward-looking initiatives like Bonn’s path to biodiversity and the participatory Bonn4Future process. With a goal of climate neutrality by 2035 and a strong focus on citizen engagement, Bonn shows how local leadership can shape global climate and resilience solutions.

More
FEATURED LEADER

Alfredo Coro II, Mayor of Del Carmen, Philippines | Chair, ICLEI Global Executive Committee Biodiversity Portfolio

Now in his fourth term, Mayor Coro II has led transformative change through a partner-driven approach that reduced poverty by 20%, improved SDG indicators, and strengthened public engagement in environmental conservation. As Chair of ICLEI’s Biodiversity Portfolio, Mayor Coro promotes public health, sustainable water systems, and urban biodiversity. Del Carmen, home to the Philippines’ largest mangrove forest, serves as a model for integrating nature and development. He advocates globally for accessible, nature-based solutions that empower coastal communities across the equatorial belt to build resilience and sustainability.
