1. LMMS

LMMS ( Linux Multimedia Studio ) is a free, open-source, and multiplatform music-making software that makes music more accessible than ever before.

It is available on Windows, Linux, and macOS and enables users to sequence songs using a straightforward interface.

Use MIDI or typing keyboard for playback with full user-defined track-based automation control and computer-controlled automation sources while consolidating instrument tracks using its Beat+Bassline Editor.

Additionally, it provides a Piano Roll Editor for fine-tuning patterns, notes, chords, and melodies, along with the ability to import MIDI.

LMMS Features:

  • Song Editor for Composing Songs
  • Beat Bassline Editor for Creating Beats and Baselines
  • Easy-to-use Piano-Roll for Editing Patterns and Melodies
  • FX Mixer with 64 FX Channels
  • Includes Software Instruments & Effect Plugins
  • Automation Support
  • SoundFont2 Support
  • Import of MIDI Files & FL Studio Projects
  • Pattern Editor
  • Sequence, Compose, & Mix
  • 16 Built-in Synthesizers
  • Hard Disk Recorder
  • Built-In Compressor, Delay, Limiter, Reverb, and Bass Enhancer

2. Apple Garageband

Apple Garageband is an intuitive, modern music creation studio that offers something for every musician, regardless of skill level or experience.

It is free for every Apple Mac computer owner.

Everyone from beginner guitar players to advanced vocalists and music producers can use its comprehensive sound library, which includes 28 beat-making drummers, three percussionists, and 100 EDM- and Hip Hop-inspired synth sounds.

An easy-to-use interface also helps make making music a breeze – users can even create a song with up to 255 tracks across their Mac, iPhone, iPad, and other Apple devices.

Apple Garageband is a great tool with all the features you need to capture your musical inspirations and create amazing tunes quickly and easily.

3. Ardour Free Music Production Software

Ardour is an open-source music software that allows music creators to record, edit, and mix music on Linux, Mac OS, and Windows.

It features flexible recording, including the ability to master record or individual tracks, and provides support for PCI, USB, and Firewire audio interfaces.

With this powerful software, musicians can enjoy non-destructive, non-linear editing capabilities with unlimited undo/redo’s and a built-in audio mixer, inline plugin control, and drag-n-drop plugins. Includes automation features.

Suitable for all levels of experience, from amateur to professional musicians.

Ardour also supports AU (OS X), VST (PC/Linux), and LV2 formats, meaning users can access many high-quality plugins and effects.

Ardour’s memory management also allows you to recover disk space instantly – completing multipass operations such as multichannel exports or stem exports hassle-free.

Features

  • Records and edits music on multiple platforms
  • Supports PCI, USB, and Firewire interfaces
  • Non-destructive, non-linear editing with unlimited undo
  • Built-in audio mixer with inline plugin control
  • Supports AU, VST, and LV2 formats for plugins

Pros

  • Flexible recording options for different needs
  • Allows multichannel and stem exports easily
  • Suitable for amateurs and professional musicians
  • Memory management recovers disk space quickly
  • Drag-n-drop plugin feature simplifies workflow

Cons

  • It may be complex for complete beginners
  • Requires compatible hardware interfaces
  • Some features need advanced knowledge
  • It could be resource-intensive on older computers

4. Audacity – Audio Editing

Audacity is an excellent solution for anyone looking to record, edit, and mix audio.

From live recordings to multitrack projects, Audacity offers a wide selection of audio tools to help you work on music projects.

The software manages multiple recordings with level meters that display volume levels and audio clipping.

Those desiring more precision can record from microphones, instrument inputs, and USB devices and dub over existing audio tracks.

If this isn’t enough, you can create multitrack recordings and record audio at low latencies and 24-bit depth.

Additionally, you can make use of VST, LADSPA, LV2, Nyquist, and Audio Unit plugins while having the ability to generate tones and unlimited undo/redos.

Its comprehensive features are creatively designed for use in any situation, from home recording to audio mastering.

7. Waveform Free ( Free Version )

Waveform Free is an impressive free digital audio workstation from Tracktion Corporation, available for Mac, Windows, and Linux users.

With features comparable to Pro level recording, editing, mixing, and more, Waveform Free provides access to a wide range of features previously unavailable in free software for music creators.

Its impressive features include 15 effects, VST and AU support, an unlimited track count, MIDI pattern generators, and constant save technology – all without watermarks.

In addition to its expansive track mixing capabilities is a slew of powerful editing tools, such as the Retrospective Record feature, which allows users to capture track notes after they have been recorded while simultaneously preserving a history timeline.

With an intuitive user interface, the ability to manage sample libraries, powerful virtual synthesizers, and dialogue markers, Waveform Free may be a great option for musicians seeking a top-tier but cost-effective solution.

The free version has feature limitations. There is a paid full-version upgrade with more options.

19. Qtractor Digital Audio Workstation

Qtractor is an Audio and MIDI multitrack music software program.

  • Multitrack audio sequencing and recording
  • Traditional multitrack tape recorder control paradigm
  • Audio file formats support OGG (via libvorbis), MP3 (via libmad, playback only), WAV, FLAC, AIFF, and many, many more (via libsnd file).
  • Standard MIDI files support (format 0 and 1).
  • Non-destructive, non-linear editing
  • An unlimited number of tracks per session/project.
  • A total number of overlapping clips per track.
