SEASON PREVIEW: Confident New Orleans Saints hope their time is now

  • Updated: Aug. 19, 2011, 11:05 a.m.
  • |Published: Aug. 19, 2011, 10:05 a.m.
By
  • Mike Herndon | mherndon@al.com
First-round draft pick Mark Ingram of Alabama is expected to give the Saints some needed punch in the offensive backfield. (Bill Haber/Associated Press)

The New Orleans Saints entered the offseason looking to improve their defensive line, add depth in the secondary and lock down a long list of free agents that promised to draw attention from other NFL clubs.

Check, check and check.

In a year in which the nucleus of New Orleans' 2009 Super Bowl team could have been decimated with free-agent defections, the Saints re-signed key contributors such as left tackle Jermon Bushrod, safety Roman Harper, tight end David Thomas, linebacker Scott Shanle and receiver Lance Moore.

While they lost center Jonathan Goodwin, defensive tackles Anthony Hargrove and Remi Ayodele and defensive back Usama Young, they replaced them by signing free agents such as former Bears center Olin Kreutz, former 49ers defensive tackle Aubrayo Franklin and former Browns defensive tackle Shaun Rogers and defensive backs Fabian Washington and Paul Oliver.

Quarterback Drew Brees credited coach Sean Payton and general manager Mickey Loomis for making New Orleans an attractive destination for free agents.

"I think that what we have built here, our organization, is a culture that guys want to be a part of," Brees said. "Back in 2006, we were in a situation where you had to over-pay or you had to figure out creative ways to get guys here. This place didn't have the best reputation. Guys weren't jumping to come to a city that was under water for the past six months (after Hurricane Katrina).

"I look at what we've been able to accomplish as a team, and the types of guys we've brought in here, and this family atmosphere that we have built and I think guys take notice around the league. Guys want to be a part of that."

Of course, winning a Super Bowl two years ago has certainly helped, particularly when a six-time Pro Bowler like Kreutz is looking for a final stop in a long and productive career.

"I'm going into my 14th year, so money wasn't really important to me. It was about finding the right fit, and this is the right fit," Kreutz said. "Just from being around them ... it feels like they're always in a 'win now' mode."

Kreutz will be one of two new faces on the starting offensive line, as veteran right tackle Jon Stinchcomb was released and will be replaced by either Zach Strief or Charles Brown.

On the defensive front, first-round draft pick Cameron Jordan will join newcomers Franklin and Rogers in solidifying the line, an area of emphasis for a Saints team that had no player record more than six sacks last season. While Jordan will help the pass rush, Franklin and Rogers give the Saints more beef and versatility inside.

"I think we're bigger right now and I think that's going to help us in a lot of ways," Payton said. "With any good front, you never see the tackles playing 60 snaps. With Rogers, I see a guy who can play nose tackle and the three. Sedrick Ellis is a guy that can play the three and nose tackle. Aubrayo is a guy that plays nose tackle. I see those guys handling a ton of work inside."

The Saints also transformed their backfield, drafting 2009 Heisman Trophy winner Mark Ingram of Alabama and offsetting the trade of Reggie Bush with the free-agent acquisition of former San Diego scatback Darren Sproles.

"When you look at those (two) guys' skills, I think they are very similar in a lot of ways," said Brees, who played with Sproles in San Diego before coming to New Orleans. "As I look at our offense and the way that we would plug him in, we will plug him in the exact same way we would Reggie and even be able to do more things."

With Atlanta adding first-round playmaker Julio Jones and free-agent defensive end Ray Edwards and Tampa Bay drafting a pair of impact defensive players in ends Adrian Clayborn and Da'Quan Bowers, the Saints needed to improve or get left behind in a competitive NFC South.

With Kreutz, Franklin and Rogers all signed to one-year deals, the thinking in New Orleans is clear -- the time is now.

"If a guy has something to prove, and wants to prove it here this year and hit free agency the next, we completely understand that. We have been successful with guys like that," Payton said. "Really all we are focused on is now, but we do feel like we have a young team, a young nucleus.

"Hopefully these (free-agent signees) come in here and they get adjusted quickly and get acclimated. All of them have a number of skins on the wall and have played successful football. And now, having them do that for us is the key."
