Getting to Know BUR Airport

Welcome to the Hollywood Burbank Airport Online Guide. As a gateway to the vibrant city of Los Angeles and its surrounding areas, BUR offers a seamless travel experience to its passengers with its two efficient terminals - Terminal A and Terminal B. BUR is recognized for its close proximity to major Hollywood studios, making it a popular choice amongst the entertainment industry. Airport News

Hollywood Burbank Airport is set to launch a new terminal modernization project. The airport authorities have approved the replacement of the 90-year-old terminal with a new and improved 14-gate terminal. The project will not only enhance the overall passenger experience but also meet the latest seismic design standards for safety. Moreover, the new terminal will feature a consolidated rental car facility, public art installations, and improved access to public transportation. In addition to the terminal upgrade, BUR has also announced new direct flights to Nashville and Austin, providing more travel options for passengers. The construction of the new terminal is expected to start in 2023 and be completed by 2024 (sources: Los Angeles Times, Burbank Leader, Hollywood Burbank Airport Press Release).

Navigating BUR's Terminals

Hollywood Burbank Airport has two terminals - Terminal A and Terminal B. Terminal A has 5 gates and is used by Southwest Airlines, while Terminal B has 7 gates and is used by Alaska Airlines, American Airlines, Delta Air Lines, JetBlue Airways, and United Airlines.

Both terminals offer a variety of services and amenities for travelers, including restaurants, cafes, shops, and lounges. Some of the popular dining options include The Habit Burger Grill, Wolfgang Puck Pizza, and Peet's Coffee. There are also several shops where you can buy souvenirs, books, and travel essentials. If you need to relax before your flight, you can visit the Hollywood Burbank Airport Lounge, which offers comfortable seating, complimentary snacks and beverages, and free Wi-Fi.

To navigate the airport terminals, follow the signs that indicate the airline and gate numbers. Each gate area has seating, charging stations, and flight information displays that show departure and arrival times. If you need assistance or have any questions, you can ask one of the airport staff members who wear blue shirts and khaki pants.

Connecting Between Terminals

At Hollywood Burbank Airport, there are two terminals: Terminal A and Terminal B. The terminals are connected by a covered walkway, which takes about 5-10 minutes to walk between. If you have a lot of luggage or need assistance, there are shuttle buses available to take you between the terminals. The shuttle buses run every 10-15 minutes and are free of charge.

Picking Up and Dropping Off Passengers

At Hollywood Burbank Airport, passengers can be picked up and dropped off curbside at both Terminal A and Terminal B. However, there are certain restrictions on curbside parking. Drivers are not allowed to leave their vehicles unattended at any time, and vehicles cannot be parked or idled for more than a few minutes. Violators may be subject to fines or towing.

For those who need to wait for arriving passengers, there are cell-phone waiting areas located near each terminal. These areas provide a convenient place to wait for passengers without having to circle the airport or pay for parking. Once the passenger has arrived and collected their baggage, they can call the driver to let them know they are ready to be picked up curbside.

Parking Facilities & Options

If you're flying out of Hollywood Burbank Airport, you'll find a variety of parking options to suit your needs. The airport offers both covered and uncovered parking in multiple lots and garages, with shuttle service to the terminals. There are also designated cell phone waiting areas for those waiting to pick up arriving passengers.

For those with disabilities, there are designated accessible parking spaces located in all parking lots and garages. Additionally, the airport offers complimentary wheelchair service to and from the terminals.

If you're looking for a more economical option, off-airport parking is available nearby with shuttle service to the airport. This can be a great option for those looking to save money on parking fees.

Transportation Options

Hollywood Burbank Airport offers a variety of ground transportation options for travelers. Visitors can choose from car rental, taxi, shared ride services, airport shuttles, and public transportation.

  • Car rental facilities are located on the ground floor of the parking garage across from the terminal building. Major providers include Alamo, Avis, Budget, Enterprise, Hertz, and National. Visitors can book their rental cars in advance or at the rental car counters located in the garage.
  • Taxis are available at the designated taxi stand located outside of the baggage claim area. The fare to downtown Burbank is approximately $20. Visitors can also use ride-hailing services like Uber and Lyft to get to their destination.
  • Shared ride services like SuperShuttle and PrimeTime Shuttle are available for travelers who want to save money. The pickup area is located outside of the baggage claim area. However, shared ride services are not available for destinations outside of Southern California.
  • Airport shuttles are available for travelers who want to get to their hotel or other destinations in the area. The pickup area is located outside of the baggage claim area. Fares vary depending on the destination.
  • Public transportation options include the Burbank Bus and Metro Bus. The Burbank Bus provides service to downtown Burbank and other destinations in the area. The Metro Bus provides service to downtown Los Angeles and other destinations in the region. The bus stop is located on Hollywood Way, across from the airport. Fares vary depending on the destination.

Passenger Services

Hollywood Burbank Airport, also known as BUR, is a convenient airport located in the United States. It offers a variety of passenger services to ensure a comfortable and stress-free travel experience for all passengers.

Wifi access is available throughout the airport, allowing passengers to stay connected while waiting for their flights. Lost and found services are also available at the airport, so passengers can retrieve any lost items they may have left behind.

For families traveling with children, there are designated children's areas in both Terminal A and Terminal B. These areas are equipped with toys and games to keep children entertained while waiting for their flights.

Special-needs services are also available at Hollywood Burbank Airport. Passengers with disabilities or special needs can request assistance from airport staff, who are trained to provide support and ensure a comfortable travel experience.

Banking services are available at the airport, including ATMs and currency exchange. Medical help is also available at the airport, with first aid stations located throughout the terminals.

Flight Status Info

As an air traveler, it is important to stay updated on the status of your flight departure. At Hollywood Burbank Airport, passengers can easily check their flight status through various methods. One of the most common ways is by using the in-airport flight displays (FIDS). These displays are located throughout the airport and provide real-time updates on flight departures, delays, and gate information.

Another convenient way to stay informed is by downloading the mobile app of the airline you are flying with. Most airlines offer mobile apps that allow passengers to receive alerts about departure delays, cancelations, or gate changes via text, email, or push notifications. This is especially helpful for passengers who are on the go and may not have access to a computer or airport display.

Passengers can also check the status of their flight departure at Hollywood Burbank Airport by visiting the flight departures page on ifly.com. This website provides real-time updates on flight departures, delays, and gate information, making it a reliable source of information for air travelers.

Airport History

Hollywood Burbank Airport, formerly known as Bob Hope Airport, is a public airport located in Burbank, California. The airport has a rich history dating back to the 1920s. Here are some significant events in the airport's history:

  • 1928 - The airport was established as United Airport, serving as a hub for commercial airlines in the Los Angeles area.
  • 1930s - The airport was renamed to Union Air Terminal and became a popular location for Hollywood celebrities to arrive and depart.
  • 1940s - During World War II, the airport was used as a military airfield and was later returned to civilian use.
  • 1978 - The airport was renamed to Burbank-Glendale-Pasadena Airport and underwent major renovations to modernize its facilities.
  • 2003 - The airport was renamed to Bob Hope Airport in honor of the famous comedian and actor who lived in the area.
  • 2017 - The airport was renamed to Hollywood Burbank Airport to better reflect its location and to attract more tourists to the area.

Today, Hollywood Burbank Airport is a popular choice for travelers looking to avoid the crowds and congestion of larger airports in the Los Angeles area.
