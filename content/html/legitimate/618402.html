Structure Of A Major Record Label
jackiemason
Major record labels have various departments that work together to promote and manage artists. The A&R department discovers and works directly with artists, while marketing develops promotion plans. Other departments handle legal issues, sales to retailers, public relations, and ensuring the artist's music is heard on the radio and television. As the music industry has changed, some labels have renamed departments or reduced their focus on long-term artist career development.

Music Business Threats & Opportunities
Music Business Threats & Opportunities
Christopher Baker
The document discusses opportunities and threats in the changing music industry. Major record labels are struggling as physical music sales decline sharply. However, the overall music business is thriving through growing areas like live concerts, merchandising, and licensing. For the music business to succeed, a new model is needed that focuses on full artist brand management and exploitation across all income streams. It must also diversify revenue sources beyond recordings and align the interests of artists and labels. Emerging markets like China and India also provide major growth opportunities if infrastructure and legal issues are addressed.

Media technology has significantly impacted the music industry in several ways. It has changed how music is owned, produced, marketed, distributed and accessed by fans. Ownership has consolidated as major labels buy out smaller producers. Production has largely moved to digital formats like MP3s. Marketing now focuses more on online downloads than physical copies. Distribution is primarily through online stores rather than physical retailers. Fans now stream and download music instead of buying CDs or attending live shows. These changes have benefitted the industry through new revenue streams but also caused losses through piracy.
