Monday, March 14, 2011

March Madness

So, as the men of US colleges compete to be ranked number 1 in basketball this year, I am striving to hit personal bests in some of my personal fitness challenges. I would like to hit a 10 minute mile. I would like to hit 3 miles of continuous running (no mater the speed). I would like to be able to do 1 min intervals on at least 8mph for 10 rounds.

I hope to make strides in all these goals as the NCAA tournament gets underway. Who knows, maybe this summer I will be fit enough to start playing some real hoops with some friends. We shall see. Important part is for me to keep going to the gym and running.

Also, I have a new iPhone, so I hope that I can track easier the runs that I get in as well as the nutrition that I eat (and adjust accordingly).

Cheers for now.

Friday, January 7, 2011

Tried something new

So on Wed this week on my day off, I decided to work out a little longer. I did a mile of running, then moved to the free weight area. I did the leg press machine (45 degree pushing movement). I worked up to 6 45lbs plates and hit that hard. Then did some other legs, chest, arms movements, then went back and finished up with more cardio on the bike.

So that was cool, but starting the next day, and continuing to today, I cannot get up from a chair without winching from the tightness and soreness. So this morning, I decided to try something new. I went to the gym, cuz no looking back, right? I went to swim laps in the heated pool, hoping to loosen up the muscles. Went well, even though it was cold as hell. Felt good, but I was (and still am) really sore. Hopefully it goes away in a day or two, cuz I want to get a 6 mile run in soon.

O, and on a side note, I am still not losing weight, just getting better at working out. So I must be eating something wrong, or just too much of the good stuff.

I am still planning to run a half marathon this summer as well. There is the SD one in June, SF in July, or Disneyland on in Sep. Which do you think I should do?

Tuesday, December 28, 2010

Ah, the holidays

Well, the big get together days are behind me and with is hopefully the buffet style eating that I have been partaking in recently. I hit the gym this morning on my day off, with the goal of 5 miles running, however long it took. In the back of my mind I was telling myself that when I got to 5 miles, I would just push for one more, making a 6 mile run -- something that I have never done.

Well, that's not exactly how things went down. I warmed up, then set out. I ran one mile, then had to back off. I recovered and starting running again. But I only made it another half mile. I was getting this weird tingling in the back of my brain. Sounds freaking writing it. So I backed off and power walked. I continued walking/running to 3 miles. I looked down and I had been at it for 45 minutes!! I was huffing badly, so I called it a workout.

I am hoping the steps back I have experienced here will not take long to overcome and come back from; I cannot imagine they will be. So, in a few words, O well... the elusive 6 mile run will have to wait for another day!

Sunday, December 19, 2010

Looking toward 2011

So it came to me in the sauna this morning at the gym, I need a long term goal, that is easily broken down into attainable chunks... so here is what I thought of:

In 2011, I will run 500 miles.

Sounds like a ton to me, but if you break it down to weeks, it's only ~10 miles per week. That's three running workouts, two 5Ks and a 4 miler (or other combos) per week. I think that to be totally do-able and I look forward to getting a tally going as soon and the calendar turns to Jan!

Oh, and I plan to only count treadmill and outside running, no elliptical, no bike, no stair stepper. Those will still be used, just on off days as recovery and cross-training tools.

Thursday, December 16, 2010

Starting point and goals

So, I started 12-2. I weighed 290 lbs if you can believe that, and I could run at a ~5mph speed for maybe 8-10 minutes before getting too winded to walk.

It is now the 16th, two weeks in: I am down to 285lbs, and feeling better already! I have ran a few 5Ks on the treadmill, and I am down to a 38 minute best. Currently I enjoy the treadmill most, I feel like it works more overall muscle. I plan to continue running at least 3 days per week until I make it under 30 minute 5K. At that point, I will introduce more weight lifting to continue my progress and on and on down the road.

As of now, I hope to lose 5 lbs per week, and hope to be at ~250lbs by the end of Feb. Think I can do it? :)

As for tonight's workout, got on the treadmill, set for 1 hour, and got off 4.5 miles later and (551 calories) later. Feeling good after it, legs a little rubberly, but in a good way. Feel like I could have kept going...

That's all for today

First Post

As of December 2nd, 2010, I have decided (yet again) to try and lose some weight. Too long have I had to take a deep breath to close a pair of pants, looked disappointedly at tying my shoes every morning, or getting winded from moving from one side of the yard to the other too fast. I have started this weight loss escapade many times, but I hope that this time it will stick. When most people in the world are gaining 5 pounds over the holidays, I hope to lose 5 lbs and keep losing!

I have started the blog to just get some things down and elaborate on thoughts, feelings, injuries (hopefully few of those!). I realize that it can get redundant to post endless facebook updates of my progress. This both muddies the waters for my friends to wade through, and some people honestly don't care, and that's fine. So those of you that do care, come here from time to time and check up on my progress. I really appreciate the accountability and support. Happy Fitness!
