    I sometimes take the old highway to Ottawa. About 10 km inside Ontario, exit onto highway 17 towards Hawkesbury. In places, this highway hugs the Ottawa river and provides some views. Stop for some frites or poutine in Alfred.

    But don't expect anything great either way, this is flat, boring mixed farming country.

    I took highway 17 from Ottawa to Hawkesbury a week ago. There was a bit of road construction (restoring shoulders), but nothing serious.

    Hi Benamed,

    GOOGLE MAPS will show you that the shortest route is the 417 Highway (combined with Autoroute 40 in Quebec) at about 200 Kms and 2 Hours Driving Time.... but coming from Ottawa you certainly have several other choices that are far more interesting and historic.

    FYI... Any 400 Series Hwy in Ontario is a divided Hwy similar to a US Interstate.

    Alternatively, to the quick and boring route, as already stated you could travel Hwy 174 / 17 which is sometimes referred to as the "old road" as it was the primary route before the 417 was built.

    Using the "old road" to Hawkesbury and then easing back onto the 417 and onto the Provincial line (Quebec Border) will run you pretty much about the same time, but it certainly is more scenic than just the Interstate that cuts across Farmland.

    On the otherhand, you could reach Montreal via the Northshore of the Ottawa River... which would be a much longer route (by 1 or 2 hours depending whether you continue thru on the Quebec side to Montreal, or cut back across the River and finish up on the Hwy 417 / Autoroute 40 Route). But the Quebec side is for sure a zillion times more interesting than the Ontario side.

    If you take a look at GOOGLE MAPS you'll see you could travel from Gatineau to Grenville (part of this route on divided Highway... Autoroute 50) and the rest along the Ottawa River shoreline thru small Pulp & Paper towns (Buckingham, Thurso, etc). Plus a great opportunity to stop at Chateau Montebello or Park Omega.

    Once you reach Grenville, you could cross the bridge over the Ottawa to Hawkesbury and continue on the previously outlined 417 route.

    OR you could continue on the Quebec side, from Grenville thru to St Andrews East and then onto Oka Quebec, crossing the River at Hudson (ferry) and then onto Autoroute 40 on into Montreal.

    This route, is by far very interesting geographically and historically.

    I will send you a PM with a detailed look at all the alternatives.

    Cheers!
