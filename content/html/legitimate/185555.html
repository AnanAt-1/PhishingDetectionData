WHAT ARE STEM CELLS?

Stem Cells are cells found in the body in early stages of development. Stem cells are the basis of all other cells as they can change into all types of cells in our body. As we get older our body has fewer stem cells, as they are not needed to the same extent as when we are younger. These cells could be a vital part of research into cures for diseases such as diabetes, spinal cord injuries, Parkinson's Disease and maybe even cancer.
