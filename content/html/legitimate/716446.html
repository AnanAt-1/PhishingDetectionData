Spectrum Internet Availability

With over 120 million Americans tuning into cable TV each year, watching cable is almost as American as apple pie and baseball. And where there’s a TV connection, there’s a chance for cable Internet. Because of this existing infrastructure, Spectrum serves over 32 million people in 41 states, making it the second-largest cable Internet provider in the U.S.

Much like digging the holes for those azaleas you bought a couple of months ago, most companies don’t like to spend time or money burrowing and building new infrastructure for fiber-optic cables. For this reason, Spectrum offers many people other Internet companies cannot reach.

Even without fiber-optic cables running to residential customers, Spectrum cable is still wicked fast, offering download speeds up to 1 Gbps (wireless speeds may vary). As for affordability, Spectrum Internet prices are economical for any budget (even if you get takeout all of the time like we do), with prices starting at $30 per month for one year.

Spectrum Internet Contracts and Data Caps

Except for a Faustian bargain with the devil over your mortal soul, no agreement stokes more fear in people’s hearts than signing a contract with an Internet provider. Cable contracts can contain hidden fees and lock you into multiple years of Internet servitude. With Spectrum, however, you can put your pen away and preserve your Internet liberties, as Spectrum has no contracts.

The no-contracts policy from Spectrum is a breath of fresh air compared to other Internet providers. You can try out Spectrum, and if for whatever reason you’re unsatisfied, you can always cancel your service and move on to another Internet provider.

When it comes to Spectrum, get out your forks and knives because your Internet buffet awaits without a closing time or a limit on the number of times you can go back for seconds. That’s because Spectrum doesn’t utilize data caps to restrict customers from accessing the Internet after reaching certain thresholds.

The Value of Spectrum Internet

Spectrum is a fitting name for this service, as it offers the full spectrum of Internet services. After going over all these things individually, let’s take a step back and review the pros and cons of Spectrum Internet:

ProsCons
  • Gigabit download speeds
  • Quick speeds at every plan level
  • Internet, TV, and phone options
  • Expansive availability
  • No data caps or contracts
  • Middle-of-the-road customer service ratings 
  • Few promotions for existing customers
  • Expensive activation fee for self-installation

All in all, we found Spectrum to be an excellent all-around Internet provider that excels in most areas, affordable bundle options, broad Internet coverage and even unlimited Internet access without data caps. What more could you ask for? If Spectrum is available in your area (which it probably is), then we recommend signing up today to get an Internet service that checks all your boxes while staying within budget.
