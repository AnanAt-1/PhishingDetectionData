All Chess. One Home.

The Saint Louis Chess Club has long been the driving force behind the U.S. Chess Championships, hosting the nation’s most prestigious chess events and showcasing the country’s top talent. U.S. Chess Champs FAQs

What is U.S. Chess Champs?

The U.S. Chess Championship is the premier national tournament, bringing together the country’s strongest players to compete for one of the most prestigious titles in American chess. Featuring a diverse field of Grandmasters, International Masters, National Masters, and Woman FIDE Masters, the championship showcases top-tier competition, strategic brilliance, and intense battles on the board. With a rich history and a legacy of legendary champions, the event continues to highlight the depth of talent in the US chess community.

How Is Saint Louis Chess Club affiliated with U.S. Chess Champs?

The Saint Louis Chess Club has always been the home of the U.S. Chess Championships, and this merger brings everything together in one place. It makes it easier to access event updates, player information, and championship history—all under one site.

Where can I find information on upcoming U.S. Chess Championship events?

All details on upcoming championships, schedules, and player lineups can now be found right here on the Saint Louis Chess Club website. Check out our upcoming invitational tournaments to see all U.S. Chess Champ events.

Will past championship content still be available?

Yes! You’ll still have access to historical records, past winners, games, and tournament coverage, now integrated into the STLCC site. View our past tournaments to catch up on previous events.

How can I watch live games and follow the championships?

Live broadcasts, commentary, and game analysis will be available directly on the Saint Louis Chess Club website, with links to streaming platforms and event coverage.

Does this change the structure of the U.S. Chess Championships?

No—the U.S. Chess Championships will continue as the premier national event, hosted by the Saint Louis Chess Club as always. The only change is how and where you access the information.

Our Mission

It is the mission of the Saint Louis Chess Club, an educational organization, to maintain a formal program of instruction to teach the game of chess and to promote and support its educational program through community outreach and local and national partnerships to increase the awareness of the educational value of chess.

Non-Discrimination Policy

The Saint Louis Chess Club does not discriminate on the basis of race, color, religion, national origin, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, age, ancestry, citizenship, genetic information, veteran status, marital status, pregnancy, disability, and any other category protected by applicable federal, state, or local laws or regulations in administration of its educational policies, admissions policies, scholarship and loan programs, events, programs, or activities.

Policy on Relationships & Interactions with Minors

The integrity of Saint Louis Chess Club’s relationship with minor players is paramount to the Chess Club’s educational mission and success. All individuals employed by or working under the direction or authority of the Saint Louis Chess Club are expected and required to maintain appropriate boundaries and relationships with minors and are strictly prohibited from engaging in sexual misconduct, hazing and harassment.

Reporting Concerns

We encourage anyone with information regarding policy violations or inappropriate conduct involving individuals employed by or working under the direction or authority of the Saint Louis Chess Club, or occurring on Saint Louis Chess Club property or at Saint Louis Chess Club events, to report such concerns to the General Manager, Joy Bray, jbray@saintlouischessclub.org – 314.361.2437 and/or Administrative Director, Tad Middleton, tmiddleton@saintlouischessclub.org -314.361.2437. You may also make a report confidentially, and if you wish, anonymously, through our confidential ethics reporting hotline managed by Navex at (833)221-3624.

Any other concerns may be reported to US Chess Safe Play and/or FIDE Ethics and Disciplinary Commission.
