Genji: Days of the Blade

Fair
How Our Ratings Work

The only massive damage in this game is to the wallets of those who dare to pick it up. Read Review

Expanding on the gorgeous PS2 action title, this next-gen follow-up continues the mystical tales of ancient Japan by game designer Yoshiki Okamoto's Game Republic studio. Set three years after the last chapter of Genkuro Yoshitsune's life, the game continues to expand on the historical accounts of feudal Japan. The game delived more than double the amount of gameplay, more diverse levels of fighting, and more playable characters -- all underlying an epic tale of a warrior's honor.

Features

  • Twice the content, twice the action -- over 15 hours of gameplay continuing the story of Yoshitsune and based on the historical battle between the Genji Clan and the Heishi Clan
  • Swap between four playable characters at any time -- take advantage of each of the four characters' unique fighting style or unleash devastating team-based fighting combos by interchanging characters between attacks
  • Gracefully articulated swordplay captured by Mitsuhiko Seike, Japan's finest sword fighter, provides the heart and soul of the intense authentic action
  • Masive battles -- lead Yoshitsune and his men against hundreds of warriors as you strive to reach and defeat the enemy warlord in epic battle sequences based on Japanese history
