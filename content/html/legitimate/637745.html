    Maria,

    There is Argosy Casino, Harrah's and Isle of Capri that are on the Missouri side. They are all "traditional casinos" in that they have slots and table games. There are loss limits and you do have to get a players card. You cannot share money like in Iowa. The casino cannot buy you drinks so there are a few differences than the casinos I have been to in Council Bluffs.

    There is one new casino in Kansas City, KS. It is the 7th street casino. I have not been there but I think it only has slot machines.

    I would recommend Argosy or Harrahs. They are the nicest. I think that Isle of Capri is disgusting. I prefer to play craps and blackjack and enjoy Argosy best. It has great table games and good restaurants. It also has a nice hotel now too.

    "Actually, the Argosy is in Kansas."

    No it isn't...it is on the north side of the Missouri River thus in Missouri (777 Argosy Parkway

    Riverside, MO 64150). We Kansans wish it was in our state for the revenue, but we'll have a casino hopefully within a couple years in Wyandotte County.

    So the casinos are; Argosy, Harrah's, Ameristar, Isle of Capri. The Argosy has the nicest buffet (IMO) and new hotel. Harrah's has 2 hotel towers, Toby Keith's, VooDoo Lounge, and the Range Steakhouse (just like Vegas). The Ameristar also has a hotel, nice Sunday brunch buffet, Arthur Bryant's BBQ, a diner, and Kid's Quest babysitting service so lots of kids compared to the other casinos (oh and kids aren't allowed in the casinos even to walk through like in Vegas) The Isle of Capri doesn't have a hotel and a mediocre buffet, and the air quality isn't very good.

    A couple Missouri laws state that you can only lose I mean buy-in) $500 every 2 hours (no problem for us), and you must have a signed players club card just to enter the casinos.

    10. Re: Want to know about casinos in Kansas City
    16 years ago

    FYI, the loss limits no longer apply in Missouri, they ended on Tue 11/4 with voters approving the amendment to eliminate them. Most casinos have already flipped the switch and removed the loss limit and ID verification processes. In some facilites, you will still be required to get a card, but it can now be anonymous only having to show ID for proof of age. The downside is that you will not earn comps using those cards. The old cards will still work and earn comps as they always did.

    My personal opinion on the casinos with a few notes:

    #1-Ameristar - Largest facilty (# of slots) with many restaurants and extra activities such as an 18 screen theater and arcade. This property has the most market share which can make it quite crowded at times.

    #2-Argosy - Argosy and Harrahs fight over 2nd place in the market, but I rank Argosy above Harrahs due to their staff. Argosy is closer to Kansas, has a new hotel tower and several restaurants. Drawback to Argosy is there is virtually nothing around it so you have to travel a bit to find any restaurants or shops. Casino is the newest (as in newly built, Argosy was the first property to open in KC once gambling was legalized, they had a riverboat for a number of years but now use a barge facility) in the area with good variety of games.

    #3-Harrahs - Harrahs has a two level casino, along with an entertainment district containing several restaurants and the VooDoo lounge. Hotel consists of two towers, Atlantic City & Las Vegas. Las Vegas is the newer tower (and nicer rooms). Casino is somewhat cramped in my opinion, but not bad during the week.

    #4-Isle of Capri - This property will likely go out of business once the Hard Rock opens by the speedway. This is the only property without a hotel and has very limited restaurants and a much smaller casino. Some of their machines still run on coins, which all the other casinos have eliminated completely. Location is ok, right off of 35, but construction on the new bridge will affect that in the next few years. Casino is the smallest in the area, but often has the highest payout % as well.

    #5-7th St Casino - While you can technically call this a casino, it is not a true casino. They use Class II gaming machines which work on a bingo based platform. While they play like Class III machines (all the other casinos listed are fully licensed for Class III), they are not the same. This is also the only facility in Kansas currently (in the KC area).
