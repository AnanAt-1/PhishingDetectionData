A warm and humid day so far today. After early drizzle, weather dried up for the late morning to early afternoon. Passing downpours are possible this evening. They aren't expected to be widespread, but a few locations might be unlucky these evening when they begin to form. There could be some rumbles of thunder too.

Tomorrow, after a dry start with patchy valley fog, sunshine moves in again for the afternoon, but by the evening, a weakening line of storms passes into CNY. These have the potential of being slow-moving, so the biggest risk is flooding in poor drainage areas under these. There could be some stronger winds too. We'll be watching the radar throughout the day.

Saturday will favor scattered showers in the morning, followed by sun in the afternoon and then another round of potentially strong storms in the evening. This system will cool things down into next week, with below average temperatures in the 40s, 50s, and low 60s for highs.
