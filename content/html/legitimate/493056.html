Hercules: The Legendary Journeys is a gripping fantasy adventure series that was originally broadcast by Syndicated from 1995 to 1999. The show draws its inspiration vividly from Greek mythology, presenting a fascinatingly inventive re-imagination of the age-old tales revolving around Hercules, essentially to engage the modern-day audience. The main characters are brought to life with remarkable talent, such as Kevin Sorbo starring as Hercules himself, Michael Hurst playing the role of Iolaus, Hercules's loyal companion, and Karl Urban appearing in multiple roles throughout the series run.

The series pours light on the mighty strength and insurmountable courage of Hercules, the demi-God son of Zeus, who roams through the danger-filled lands of ancient Greece. The portrayal of his character dances on the delicate line between humanity and divinity, infusing the episodes with a sense of relatability. With his overwhelming strength and often wit, Hercules battles an array of mythological beasts, unforgiving warlords, ruthless kings, and even vengeful Gods in his eternal quest for justice, truth and the well-being of the innocent. Hercules always stays humble, spiteful only to the wrongdoers, and remarkably empathetic towards the everyday people, who are often the victims in these power struggles.

The series also delves into the relationship between Hercules and Iolaus, showing an intricate comradery and priceless, unshakeable bond of friendship. Iolaus, although human, nearly matches Hercules in bravery and the penchant for doing what's right. Engaging, humorous, and often full of heartwrenching emotion, their camaraderie is a central part of many episodes, giving the audience relatable figures amidst the mythical narrative.

Hercules: The Legendary Journeys wouldn't be complete without the inclusion of the Gods' meddling in mortal affairs. Gods such as Ares, Hera, and Aphrodite are frequent characters in the series. Their appearances create gripping, high-stakes story arcs that often test Hercules' strength, wisdom, and morality.

Markedly different from other shows in the same genre, Hercules: The Legendary Journeys cleverly merges high fantasy elements with moments of light-hearted comedy, believable romance, and even elements of present day pop-culture references, successfully creating a genre of its own. At the same time, it does not shy away from discussing complex themes of mortality, morality, and the nuances between good and evil, thus making it a deeply thought-provoking and at times philosophical series.

The show's success is also attributed to the dynamic chemistry between Kevin Sorbo and Michael Hurst. Their brilliant performances are complemented by incredible supporting actors, precise screenwriting, and engrossing storylines. It's also worth noting the strength of New Zealand’s landscapes as a supporting character. The show takes advantage of its location to create an immersive experience for the viewer, transporting you to the extraordinary landscapes of Parnassus, Athens, and the underworld itself.

The production values were grand for its time, with many forms of special effects, ranging from practical effects to early CGI. While the effects may seem dated by today's standards, they are packed with a kind of nostalgic charm, adding a unique facet to the show's style that many fans love.

The show's fantastic soundtrack, composed by top-notch music scores that infuse each scene with emotion and energy, also deserves special mention. From dramatic orchestra pieces that highlight tense, action-packed moments to light, almost playful tunes accentuating the humorous ones, the soundtrack plays a crucial part in setting the mood and tone of the series.

All these combined have made Hercules: The Legendary Journeys a flagship series, remembered even decades after it ended by those who are fans of the genre or simply lovers of good storytelling. It offers a well-crafted blend of myth, action, humor, and drama, serving as an enthralling escape into the epic world of Greek mythology. Especially for those interested in classics and folklore brought to life, the series promises a thrilling journey filled with adventure, heroism, and legendary journeys to the heart of good and evil.

Hercules: The Legendary Journeys is a series categorized as a canceled. Spanning 6 seasons with a total of 111 episodes, the show debuted on 1995. The series has earned a moderate reviews from both critics and viewers. The IMDb score stands at 6.5.
