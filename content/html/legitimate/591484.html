Eight Days: Sept. 16-23

  • Updated: Sep. 16, 2011, 1:27 p.m.
  • |Published: Sep. 16, 2011, 12:27 p.m.
By
  • Gloucester County Times

FRIDAY, SEPTEMBER 16

Special Events

FREE MOVIES IN THE PARK: "MEGAMIND," Thompson Family Park, 40 E. Wolfert Station Road, Mickleton. Film will be shown at dusk (8 to 8:30 p.m.) Saturday rain date. For rain out information, call 856-589-0047. www.gloucestercountynj.gov.

"HAPPY DAYS — A NEW MUSICAL," United Methodist Church, 201 Mantua Boulevard, Mantua. The musical — with Richie, Joanie, Potsie, Ralph, Pinky, the Fonz and all the gang — premieres tonight at 8. General admission tickets are $16 in advance, $20 at the door. Tickets for seniors 60 and up and children 12 and under are $14 in advance, $18 at the door. Performances run through Sept. 25. info@spotliters.com. 856-589-8665.

Music

BRET MICHAELS, Atlantic City Hilton, Boston Avenue and The Boardwalk, Atlantic City. Show time is 8 p.m. Tickets range from $50 to $100. www.ticketmaster.com.

BLONDIE, Music Box at Borgata Hotel Casino & Spa, One Borgata Way, Atlantic City. Show time is 9 p.m. Tickets range from $39.50 to $49.50. TheBorgata.com. 866-900-4TIX (4849).

MOTION CITY SOUNDTRACK, Theater of the Living Arts, 335 South St., Philadelphia. The band will perform songs from all four of their studio albums (chronologically) beginning tonight and concluding on Sunday, Sept. 18. Tickets are $29.50 and $50 for both nights. LiveNation.com, Ticketmaster.com, 800-745-3000.

SATURDAY, SEPTEMBER 17

Special Events

GLOUCESTER COUNTY'S 27TH ANNUAL KIDS FISHING CONTEST, Wilson Lake at Scotland Run Park, Academy Street, Clayton. The fishing contest, held rain or shine, will take place from 8:30 to 11:30 a.m. Registration held morning of contest. No cost to enter. Awards presented at 11:30 a.m. at boat launch off Fries Mill Road. Age groups are: 7 years and under; 8 to 13 years; and 14 to 18 years. There is a boating and non-boating category. Everyone 16 years and older must be licensed. Contestants should bring their own bait and are limited to one rod and reel. 856-881-0845.

UPTOWN PITMAN CRAFT SHOW, Craft show from 9 a.m. to 4 p.m. Held along Broadway, Pitman and Ballard avenues, it will feature hundreds of vendors with crafts, food and art. uptownpitman.com.

1ST ANNUAL JACK PEAK 5K RUN AND 1-MILE FAMILY FUN RUN/WALK, YMCA of Salem County, 204 Shell Road, Carneys Point. The race, held in memory of Jack Peak, will take place from 9 a.m. to noon. A member of the YMCA for more than two decades, Peak volunteered his time and talents to help the Y fulfill it's mission to help the less fortunate in the community. All monies raised will be used to further the Y's mission for youth development. Dogs welcome. www.myymca.net. 856-299-1493.

LECTURE AND BOOKSIGNING, Salem Historical Society, 83 Market St., Salem. A lecture and booksigning with Charles Harrison will be held from 4:30 to 5:30 p.m. Harrison will be discussing his new book, A History of Salem County, New Jersey Tomatoes and TNT. Free. www.visitsalemcountynj.com.

COMEDY NIGHT, LOL:) Comedy Club at King Pin Bowling Lanes, 6622 Black Horse Pike, West Egg Harbor. Jeff Pirrami, who has been seen at The Borgata and Tropicana in Atlantic City and Las Vegas will be headlining. Ed McGonical, a comic heard on WYSP's Kidd Kriss Show and seen at Helium Comedy Club in Philadelphia and Robert Hritz, from Helium and Catch A Rising Star, plus Big Rick Cahall will also perform. Host will be Eric Potts, winner of the 2011 Sarcasm Comedy Competition. Show time is 9 p.m. Tickets are $15. www.lolcomedyclub.net.

1ST WMGK BREW BLAST, Battleship New Jersey, 62 Battleship Place, Camden. More than 50 of the finest craft beers in the nation will be available for sampling aboard the tented deck of the battleship. The event will be held from 1 to 5 p.m. WMGK DJs will be in attendance throughout the day and the newly crowned WMGK House Band, All That Matters, will play live. Authentic Oktoberfest franks, sausages and artisan cheeses provided by Dietz & Watson. Participating beers and brands include: Blue Moon Harvest Pumpkin & Belgian White, Blue Point, Brooklyn Lager & Brooklyn Post Road Pumpkin, Crispin Ciders, Flying Fish Octoberfish, Great Lakes Oktoberfest & Great Lakes Elliot Ness, Harpoon Octoberfest and Pumpkin, Lagunitas, Long Trail Harvest, Magic Hat Hex & Magic Hat No. 9, Magners Cider, Ommegang Belgian Pale Ale & Ommegang Hennepin, Oskar Blues, Palm, Samuel Adams Cherry Wheat & Samuel Adams Oktoberfest, Saranac Pumpkin, Shiner Bock & Oktoberfest, Shipyard, Sierra Nevada Tumbler, Sixpoint, Stoudts, Troegs, Unibroue, Victory, Weyerbacher, Woodchuck Private Reserve Pumpkin and Woodchuck Fall Limited Series, Yards and more. Tickets are $45. Each ticket includes a voucher for a free battleship tour. www.wmgk.com.

26TH ANNUAL OCEAN CITY AIRPORT FESTIVAL, Airport, 26th and Bay Avenue, Ocean City. The festival, with a wide range of airplanes on display — including a rare Mitchell World War II B 25 Bomber — will be held from 10 a.m. to 3 p.m. A kite display by Air Circus of Ocean City will be presented on the airport's runway from 11:30 a.m. to 12:30 p.m. The N.J. Pine Barrens RC Club will present a model airplane display at 12:30 p.m. A paper airplane making and flying contest is planned for 11 a.m. It will feature categories for children ages 4 to 12. An antique auto display with a runway parade is planned for 11:20 a.m. Helicopter and airplane rides for a fee. An awards ceremony for the most outstanding aircraft on display will take place at 2 p.m. The B-25 will take off from the airport at 3 p.m. Refreshments will be available. 609-525-9300.

EXHIBIT: "DINOSAURIUM," The Discovery Museum, 2040 Springdale Road, Cherry Hill. An exhibit dedicated to dinosaur exploration and discovery. It features hands-on activities and exhibits for children ages 10 and under. www.discoverymuseum.com. 856-424-1233.

SCARECROW FESTIVAL AND EXHIBIT, Tyler Arboretum, 515 Painter Road, Media, Pa. Simple, the Roving Garden Artist will demonstrate how he creates giant grass sculptures like the ones displayed at Tyler. Construct a personalized scarecrow for the "Scarecrow Exhibit." Festivities also include crafts, games and music. The exhibit will be displayed through the end of November. The event will take place from 10 a.m. to 2 p.m. Free with regular admission. www.tylerarboretum.org.

SUNDAY, SEPTEMBER 18

Special Events

IMAGINE AFRICA WITH THE PENN MUSEUM, Penn Museum, 3260 South St., Philadelphia. This 12-month project investigating community perspectives, launches today, with a public celebration featuring music, dance, arts, gallery tours and talks from 1 to 4 p.m. Drawing upon its African collection, the museum presents more than 50 objects framed around eight broad topics. Visitors are asked to provide feedback on the objects, content seen and to discuss what would make an engaging exhibition — from their point of view. www.penn.museum.

AEROBATIC AIR SHOW, Over the Beach from 6th to 14th streets, Ocean City. The display will be visable from 1 to 3 p.m. It will feature 12 acts, including individual performances and groups flying in formation. Performances from aerobatics world champion, Rob Holland, and Jane Wicker, a daredevil wing walker, are planned. Bill Gordon flying an Aurora 450 Stearman; Jim Beasley in a Spitfire Mk. XVIII; the Fire Birds Aerobatic Team; Randy Devere piloting CJ-6 Aerobatics; the NE Raiders Formation Team; stunt pilots Kevin Russo, Kendall Simpson and Jack Knutson and a Vintage T-6/SNJ Flight will also be featured. 609-525-9300.

WIX AND WAX CANDLEMAKING, James and Ann Whitall House at Red Bank Battlefield Park, 100 Hessian Ave., National Park. Make candles from noon to 4 p.m. Free. www.gloucestercountynj.com.

MONDAY, SEPTEMBER 19

Special Events

EXHIBIT: "MAGICAL HABITATS," Tyler Arboretum, 515 Painter Road, Media, Pa. The family-friendly exhibit celebrates the domain of fairies, gnomes and their enchanted friends with 23 unique custom exhibits, displays and structures created by local and regional artisans, woodworkers, sculptors, potters, builders and landscape architects. The arboretum is open Monday through Friday, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. and Saturday and Sunday, 9 a.m. to 6 p.m. Exhibit runs through Oct. 30. Adults, $9; seniors, $8 and children 3 to 15 are $5. Children under 3 are free. www.tylerarboretum.org.

TUESDAY, SEPTEMBER 20

Special Events

ANDREW LLOYD WEBBER'S "ASPECTS OF LOVE," Walnut Street Theatre, 825 Walnut St., Philadelphia. Set against the background of 1940s France and Italy, Alex Dillingham, a young student traveling through France, falls in love with the alluring actress Rose Vibert. As the pair embarks on a passionate affair, the unexpected arrival of Alex's uncle changes their lives forever. Tonight's performance is at 8. Tickets range from $10 to $95. Performances run through Oct. 23. www.walnutstreettheatre.org. 215-574-3550.

WEDNESDAY, SEPTEMBER 21

Special Events

CHOIR AUDITIONS, First Presbyterian Church, 10 Wildwood Ave., Pitman. The Gloucester County Children's Choir, under the direction of Sylvia Richardson, is holding auditions today for its 2011-12 season. Prior musical training is not necessary. Auditions for voice placement for children aged 9 to 17 are by appointment only. To schedule an audition, call 856-589-5149.

Music

THE HUMAN LEAGUE, Keswick Theater, 291 Keswick Ave., Glenside, Pa. The glam pioneers of electro-pop will perform tonight. Special guests will be Men Without Hat. Show time is 8 p.m. Tickets are $33.50 and $39.50. www.keswicktheatre.com. 215-572-7650.

THURSDAY, SEPTEMBER 22

Music

SUMMER SERIES THURSDAY NIGHT CONCERTS IN THE PARK: "KELLY RICKETS TRIO," MLK Park, Market Street and MLK Drive, Salem City. A free concert, featuring the Kelly Rickets Trio, will be presented from 6 to 8 p.m. Bring chair or blanket and a cold beverage. Some chairs available on a first come basis. The Salem City Café will be serving food outdoors. www.visitsalemcountynj.com. 856-935-8800.

UMPHREY'S MCGEE, House of Blue Music Hall at Showboat Atlantic City, 801 Boardwalk, Atlantic City. The jam band will perform at 8 p.m. Tickets are $26.25. www.ticketmaster.com.

FRIDAY, SEPTEMBER 23

Special Events

SOUTH JERSEY'S HOME SHOW EXPO, St. Matthew's Rec Center, 245 Glassboro Road, (Route 322), Williamstown. Hundreds of products and services for home renovations, home décor, improvements and more will be offered. Expo held today from 4 to 8 p.m. and Saturday, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. Open to the public. Free. www.alwayspositivegroup.com. 856-401-9111.

A POST-FRINGE DOUBLE FEATURE, NOSPACE, 2315 E. Hazzard St., Fishtown, Philadelphia. "Meagan and David's Low-Cost Creativity Workshop" is a fringe hit by Go Horses, a New York City-based comedy team. "The Beautiful Refrigerator Is Empty" is a demonic drag cabaret by No Face Performance Group, Philadelphia. It tells the story of Teena Geist, a precocious 14-year-old awaiting the arrival of her prom date. Performances are tonight and Saturday, both at 8 p.m. Tickets are $5. www.brownpapertickets.com/event/199161.

Music

GET THE LED OUT, House of Blue Music Hall at Showboat Atlantic City, 801 Boardwalk, Atlantic City. The Philadelphia-based group recreate the music of British supergroup Led Zeppelin. Show time is 8 p.m. Tickets range from $17 to $23. www.ticketmaster.com.

ENRIQUE IGLESIAS WITH SPECIAL GUEST PITBULL AND PRINCE ROYCE, Mark G. Etess Arena at Trump Taj Mahal, 1000 Boardwalk at Virginia Avenue, Atlantic City. Doors open at 7 p.m. Show time is 8 p.m. Tickets are $77.50 and $97.50, with a $2 facility fee applicable on all show tickets. www.enriqueiglesias.com. www.ticketmaster.com. 800-736-1420.
