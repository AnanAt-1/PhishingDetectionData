Veterans Day Observation

On November 11-13, Brig. General Steve Ritchie came to Ascension Parish to help us honor our veterans.  General Ritchie is the first Air Force “ace” in the Vietnam War , having shot down five Mig 21s during the early 1970’s.  Listening to General Ritchie speak about the missions he flew, and about our country in whole was both inspiring and impressive, however, his story was outdone by his wife Marianna.

Marianna Ritchie grew up in communist Romania, during the time when Romania was locked solidly behind the iron curtain.  She told us, that growing up, the only thing she longed to be was an American.  She had learned of America through her grandfather being able to tune into radio free Europe.

Often, we as Americans, take our liberty and freedom for granted, because we have known no other lifestyle.  However, when one who has lived under an oppressive regime speaks about how oppression seeks to break the human spirit, and defeat the human will, freedom and liberty take on a new light in appreciation.  Marianna Ritchie spoke of oppression, and how she was on a watch list from the Romanian government for do nothing more that having a relative who was a priest.  As a child, she prayed for American planes to attack her country, and destroy the communist regime.  She did not care of she died in such attacks, because if she could not be free, she did not want to live.

Most compelling was her wish for the liberating force of her country to be American.  She knew that America and Americans have always fought for freedom, and against tyranny and oppression.

I was moved to hear this story.  The fact that our country is filled with men and women who willingly lay down their lives for our freedom is part of our true American spirit.  Since our founding days, Americans have stood for freedom and defended the freedom and liberty that we hold dear, on our own soil and on foreign soil.  To know that that belief is held in other parts of our world, where freedom and liberty are oppressed is simply amazing.

The belief epitomizes the very reason why our country is truly the best in the world.  Humans crave freedom, humans excel when they are free, the natural course of humanity is freedom, not oppression.  Our nation was founded on that belief, and our veterans have defended with zeal that belief. 

Listening to Marianna Ritchie speak deepened my admiration and respect for all of our military, and our veterans.  Contrary to what some may say or think, our military, and all those who served, have been a force of good, not only for our country, but for the world.

 

Brig. General Steve Ritchie with Matthew Pryor for Judge

July 4, 1776

Today, the 4th of July has come to signify a grand celebration.  The flag flying, bunting adorning every outpost, hamburgers, hot dogs, and fireworks.  In the revelry that has come from celebrating the birth of our country, it is hard to imagine the “first” 4th, and the sacrifice that the men who brought it made.

Each member of the Continental Congress was a wealthy landowner.  When the congress adopted Thomas Jefferson’s final draft of the Declaration Independence, those men placed in jeopardy everything they had, their life, their families, their land and their fortune.  By adopting the Declaration of Independence, each member of the Continental Congress committed high treason against his king.

That was no small matter.  For the members of the Continental Congress, as well as for all the colonialists who sought independence, this meant war; war against the most powerful army in the world, waged by sparsely trained militia and farmers.

The act of declaring independence, the act of risking everything on a set of ideals, the act of declaring a revolution and war against their sovereign, epitomized a spirit that was (and is) truly American.  Our Founding Fathers were ready to give up everything they had, including their lives for the ideals of liberty and freedom.  They brought to life the cry of Patrick Henry “give me liberty or give me death!”

For the past 235 years, America and Americans have lived up to that spirit.  Where tyranny has threatened world peace, America and Americans have stepped in to stop it, often after the other nations of the world failed or refused to do so.  When tyranny has threatened our shores, America and Americans have fought back with a vengeance.

America’s bravery and gallantry on the field of battle has not been the only area where the American spirit has reigned.  In 235 years, (a blink in the annals of world history) the United States of America went from a new and vulnerable country to the greatest, wealthiest, and most industrialized nation in the history of the world.  We are a nation where one’s life is not predetermined by race, creed, color, or the circumstances of one’s birth. Anyone, regardless of those factors can pursue their own form of happiness, wealth and success.  The greatness of our country comes exclusively from the power of the human spirit and what can be accomplished when that spirit is free and liberty abounds.

Our founding fathers knew this, and were willing to give up everything they had to this end.  As we celebrate this 4th of July, let us not forget what it is that has made our country so great.  Let us not forget those men and women, from our founding fathers on forward, who would rather be homeless, penniless or dead than live in a county where freedom is lost and tyranny reigns.

Leadership Ascension — Serving the Community

On Thursday, June 16, I had the privilege of attending and graduating the 2011 Leadership Ascension Class.  It was a tremendous honor for me, and we all have reason to be proud of this year’s class.

Leadership Ascension was started in 1996 to train emerging leaders in all aspects of the expanding and complex economy that is Ascension Parish.  From the political to the economic to the historical dynamics of our parish, each class is emerged in all things Ascension.  In 2001, a new dimension was added to Leadership, the class projects.  Each class is divided into three to four separate groups, and each group must complete a project of its choosing that benefits the community.  The best project is announced each year at graduation.

This year, we had three awesome projects.  The first was Early Learning is Fundamental or ELF.  ELF discovered that illiteracy is still a problem that plagues our community.  Astonishingly, there are many children who never own one book until they attend school for the first time at age five or six.  These children are already behind and have little hope of catching up.  ELF partnered with the Dolly Parton Imagination Library and was able to fund all of the head start kids in Donaldsonville to receive one book per month until they achieve five years of age.  Illiteracy is a serious problem, and the ELF group took a big step in combating it.

The second project was the Waverunners 5K.  For the past year or so, two inspiring mothers in Ascension Parish have been heading up Project Kidz Kove.  This will become a playground where any child, regardless of his ability can go and play.  As it stands, there is only one playground within 60+ miles where children with disabilities can play.  All children love to play, its what they live for.  With the Kidz Kove playground in Gonzales, all kids will have that option, after all “Play is Universal”.  The Waverunners adopted project Kidz Kove and held a 5k road race, the Waverunners 5k, to raise money to help start Project Kidz Kove, and money they raised….over $30,000.00 gross!  Wow!

The final project was the Ascension Fire House.  This group took an abandoned, run down house owned by the Parish and renovated it into the Ascension Fire House.  The house will be a refuge for families who lose everything in a house fire. Such a family can go to the fire house and live for up to five days in the comfort and peace of a home in order to start rebuilding their lives.  For a family who loses all of there worldly possessions, the Ascension Fire House offers a place where hope and faith are restored.

Although the Fire House won, all of the projects will have a lasting, positive effect on our community, and epitomize the ideals of a servant leader. Well done class of 2011!!!.  You make us proud!

Burnside Terminal — Back in Business

Yesterday, I had the honor of attending a lunch sponsored by Trafigura.  Trafigura and its acquisition arm Impala, is a global concern that just completed the purchase of the Burnside Terminal.  For those of you who may not know, the Burnside Terminal is a defunct bauxite terminal located on Hwy 44 on the river.  Due to its past duty as being a terminal for bauxite, the whole facility, including the surrounding fence, has a nice burnt orange hue.

Trafigura is currently in the process of refurbishing the terminal, at an estimated cost of about $75 million.  By the end of the year, they hope to have the terminal up and running again, as a rail to ship, ship to rail, barge to ocean-going, ocean-going to barge terminal for coal and bauxite, expecting shipments from all the coal basins in North America.

The good news for Ascension Parish is that in the short term there will be over 200 new construction jobs, and once operational, over 100 operating jobs.  Its a good economic boost for Ascension Parish.

I got to attend as a representative of the Ascension Chamber.  The principles of Trafigura wanted to know what concerns the community might have, and how they can become a good Ascension neighbor.  I was impressed, here we have a company ready to take over an eye sore, invest a bunch of money into our community, add a bunch of new jobs, and asking what more they can do!  It is exciting to see economic growth that will benefit everyone in our parish by a company ready to be a responsible member of our parish.
