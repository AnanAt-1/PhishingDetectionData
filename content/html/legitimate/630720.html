graphoanalysis

graph`o`an`al´y`sis

    (grăf`ô`an`ăl´ĭ`sĭs)
n.1.The art of judging of a person's character, disposition, and aptitude from his handwriting; also called graphology. As a discipline, the modern form was developed by Milton Newman Bunker in the period after 1915.
Webster's Revised Unabridged Dictionary, published 1913 by G. & C. Merriam Co.
