The DigiBarn Friends:
Jef Raskin: A Life of Design

Jef Raskin passed away peacefully on Saturday February 26th, 2005.
Our condolences go out to Jef's family, friends and wider community.
We invite you to view and listen to our January 2005 interviews with Jef
as well as see slides featuring his life in design and other resources
pertaining to Jef's life and work.


A Memorial Service for Jef Raskin was held March 13, 2005
(our photos and audio of the service are included here)

NEW: Remembrances of Jef Raskin by Richard Karpinski and by Larry Tesler and others,
the Design Sense and Life of Jef Raskin, and Recent Contributions about Jef


Jef featured on Google home page, Feb 28-March 2, 2005

Jef Raskin was a great friend of the DigiBarn project having provided us with some historic items to exhibit including the Canon Cat, the first joystick made for an Apple computer, a prototype of the Swyft and other wonders. Jef has also provided us some wonderful writing and other documents including his story Meeting Merlin about his early "ah ha" moment that led him to go into computing and a provokative collection of Early Documents about the birth of the Macintosh.

We will miss you Jef!

If you have any stories, images, video or audio or artifacts pertinent to telling Jef's story, please do not hesitate to contact us.

January 29, 2005 visit, photos and interviews

On January 29, 2005, a month before his passing we visited Jef and his family. Jef gave us a tour of some of his amazing artifacts from the birth of personal computing. Photos and audio interviews of this visit follow. See much more on this page about Jef's life that has been contributed by his friends and family.
