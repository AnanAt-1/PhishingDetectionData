    Baylor Coach Stands by His Recruiting Methods

    By Thayer Evans

    NEW ORLEANS — Baylor Coach Scott Drew makes no apologies for his dogged recruiting philosophy.

    That’s despite it drawing the ire of Big 12 coaches, most notably Texas Coach Rick Barnes.

    On Friday, Drew was asked by a reporter if he recruits the way he does because he had to rebuild the Baylor program. The Bears were on probation for five years through this season for N.C.A.A. violations that were discovered in an investigation prompted by the 2003 murder of a player, Patrick Dennehy, by a former teammate.

    “I think it’s a personal style,” said Drew, who has a 107-102 record in his seven seasons at Baylor, including 26-7 this year. “We’re one of those people if we see a good basketball player, we’re going to recruit them. We’re not going to say, ‘Well, he’s not going to come to our school or he’s going to go to this school or that school.’ Our job is to recruit elite players and bring them to our school.

    “If we do that, we’re going to be successful. So we’re definitely going to do that. We’re smart enough to know that if you don’t have good players, you’re not a good coach.”

    With his team’s opening-round win Thursday against Sam Houston, Drew posted Baylor’s first N.C.A.A. tournament victory since its Final Four appearance in 1950. The Bears get 11th-seeded Old Dominion on Saturday in the South Region.

    Asked if he feels that his fellow Big 12 coaches are not supportive of him and his methods, he said he received text messages of “Congratulations”, “Good luck”, and “We’ll be cheering for you” before and after the game.

    “So I think sometimes something might get out, and now all of a sudden people think perception is reality,” Drew said.

    Barnes Has Stern Words for Baylor

    By Thayer Evans

    OKLAHOMA CITY — Baylor’s loss in Saturday’s Big 12 tournament championship game might have been a disappointment to the casual college basketball fan.

    Until their 73-60 setback to third-seeded Missouri, the ninth-seeded Bears had been all the rage here at the Ford Center. But among Big 12 coaches, there have been rumblings about Bears Coach Scott Drew.

    On Saturday, I wrote an article about Baylor’s hiring of Dwon Clifton in July as its director of player development, a newly created position. He is the former A.A.U. coach of John Wall, a point guard from Raleigh, N.C., who is the nation’s top high school player in the 2009 class.

    Wall is considering Baylor along with Duke, Kansas, Memphis, Miami and North Carolina State. Drew did not want to talk about Clifton’s hiring and Baylor declined requests to interview Clifton.

    Texas Coach Rick Barnes spoke openly about his adversarial relationship with Drew and his disapproval of A.A.U. coaches of top recruits being hired by universities, a practice that is becoming more common.

    “There’s a line that he knows that he can’t cross with me,” Barnes said of Drew. “He knows that. He definitely knows that.”

    Drew has a reputation in college basketball circles for recruiting by talking negatively about other schools.

    “If they’re saying things about us, I would think they wouldn’t,” Barnes said. “I’ve had that discussion that it’s going to be above board in terms of how things need to be done.”

    Asked if he had anything else to say about Drew’s recruiting practices, Barnes said, “I wish I could.”

    Those are strong words from an even-keeled and well-respected coach in the Big 12.

    Baylor Fulfills Its Potential, Finally

    But by beating No. 8 Nebraska on Wednesday and No. 1 seed Kansas on Thursday, the Bears had silenced any speculation.

    “He’s done a great job since he’s been here,” the senior guard Curtis Jerrells said. “Nobody but him could come in here and do things that he’s done. He was man enough to come in here and take it.”

    The scrutiny of Drew had intensified because of his ninth-seeded team’s poor play in the regular season, which included a six-game conference losing streak.

    On the heels of receiving an at-large bid to the N.C.A.A. tournament last season for the first time since 1988 and with four returning starters, there were high expectations for the Bears this season.
