10 Best Protein Powders in 2024, According to a Dietitian

Whether you're looking to build muscle, lose weight, or boost your nutrition, these protein powders have you covered.

Finding time to make and eat a meal can feel impossible when you have a packed schedule. That's why so many of us turn to snacks and fast food for quick eats to stop the tummy grumbling. But if you're looking for a more balanced way to boost your nutrition, supplements like protein powder can be a game-changer.

Not only are protein powders popular for building muscle and aiding in weight loss, but they also support general nutrition for those who need something quick and easy to fill the gaps between meals. Finding a protein powder that fits your tastes, goals, and budget can be daunting. Ultimately, the best protein powder for you is the one you use consistently.

To make your search easier, we've rounded up some of the best protein powders for general health to help you find one that meets your needs.

Benefits of Using Protein Powder

Protein powders are a convenient way to meet protein needs. Your body needs protein to make and repair cells, support immune health, and maintain muscle. Research suggests healthy adults need about 0.8 to 1.2 grams of protein per kilogram of body weight.

Using protein powders to support daily needs may:

  • Support weight management: Protein is more satiating than carbs and fat, keeping you full longer. Adding protein powder to your morning coffee may stop you from hitting the vending machines when hunger strikes during your mid-morning break.
  • Preserve lean muscle mass: Using protein powders may preserve and improve muscle mass, even if you don't work out. A 2023 study published in Translational and Clinical Pharmacology found a significant increase in muscle mass in sedentary people taking a protein supplement compared to those taking a placebo.
  • Enhance energy: Protein powders may enhance energy and endurance by supporting muscle mass, making it easier to keep up with the day's demands.

Types of Protein

Protein powders come in many forms, each offering benefits. Understanding the different types of proteins used in the powders can help you decide what supplement works best for you.

  • Whey protein: Whey protein is a milk protein and one of the most studied and popular protein powders.
  • Casein protein: Casein protein is a milk protein that digests slowly and a popular pre-sleep supplement for supporting muscle repair.
  • Collagen peptides: Though not a complete protein, collagen peptides are popular for skin and joint health.
  • Pea protein: People who follow a vegan or vegetarian diet may prefer pea protein, a high-quality plant-based supplement that supplies all of the essential amino acids.
  • Plant protein blends: Some plant-based protein powders use different plants, such as brown rice, pumpkin seed, and hemp seed, to create a powder with a more balanced amino acid profile.

How We Selected the Best Protein Powders

Protein powders are dietary supplements that don't require FDA approval before hitting the shelves. It's up to the manufacturer to ensure their supplement is safe and effective. We used the following criteria to select the best protein powders:

  • Natural ingredients: Protein powders may contain a long list of ingredients, including artificial colors, flavors, and sweeteners. We looked for protein powders that featured more natural ingredients.
  • Provides at least 20 grams of protein: A good protein powder should supply at least 20 grams of protein per serving. The International Society of Sports Nutrition suggests getting 20 to 40 grams of high-quality protein at regular intervals throughout the day is key to maintaining and promoting lean muscle mass.
  • Third-party tested: Safety is a priority when it comes to recommending dietary supplements, and we looked for protein powders that were third-party tested. This means the powder was independently tested by a lab, ensuring the supplement contains only the ingredients listed on the label and in the reported amounts.
