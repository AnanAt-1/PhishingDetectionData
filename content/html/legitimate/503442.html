As some of you may know I have been debating/researching the drum kit I will be getting soon. I should say I am on the road a lot (OTR truck driver) and don't have the time to "go shopping" for a kit and am finding it hard to compare kits other than by videos online, spec sheets, reviews and the comments and advice of you good people on this forum. The good news is I have narrowed it down to either the Sonor Force 3007 kit or the Mapex Meridian Maple kit and have not ruled out the Ludwig Epic Funk kit but am favoring the first two I mentioned. I guess I should also mention I mainly play funk, jazz and rock... mainly.

Which kit do you like? The finishes that are shown aren't what I would choose with the exception of the Ludwig finish, I like that.

Let the comments flow!

Just going by the photos and specs, I'd go with the Mapex set. I also like the look of the Mapex set much more than the Sonor set--nice lugs, too.

Having both 14- and 16-inch floor toms is really nice and give you more flexibility. I've used a 14x14 floor tom on many occasions and find that I like it just as much as a 16x16--and it's easier to carry.

I also prefer the simpler Mapex tom holder. I've used the Mapex type of holder and it has more than enough flexibility without the added weight and complexity of the extra tubes, memory locks, and wing nuts for the height tubes. (I also have a much more flexible tom holder with adjustable tube heights and I much prefer the simpler holder.) I'm also not a fan of how that Sonor tom holder looks from the front of the set.

My $.02

Good luck.

Mapex and the Sonor Force are made in the same factory (two different companies sharing manufacturing facilities), so there's not a whole lot of difference in the drums apart from the hardware. Both make great drums. I would go with the Sonor simply because I really like the hardware more than than the Mapex. Also, the look of the Sonor's is very close to the higher-end, and far more expensive, German-made S-Classix, Delites, and SQ-2's.

Also, I'm no fan of bass drums deeper than 16", and for funk, the preferred bass drum sound is deep, quick, with lots of attack. Not sure how a 20" deep bass drum would fit that requirement better than an 18 or 16" depth.

I don't really see a disadvantage of the Mapex tom hardware as compared to the Sonar. Though the Mapex doesn't have individual risers, you can still move each of the toms up and down a couple of inches on the individual posts and that would be enough adjustment for me and most other folks, I'd think. The Mapex tom holder is more compact too and less likely to get out of whack when it's thrown in a hardware bag and gets jumbled around with a bunch of other hardware.

Now my Slingerland tom mount is really lacking. With that one you pretty much have to move both the toms on it around as a pair with no adjustment as far as how close they are together or how high or how they're angled within relation to each other. I sure wish that someone would come up with an affordable aftermarket tom tree to fit those old Slingies. The more adjustable version of that piece of hardware seems to be pretty rare, expensive and overly cumbersome in it's design. Until I get my hands on something like that though, I'm going to be using a 1 up config... which I'm pretty much OK with. It would be nice though to play around with using that 13" I have next to the 12".
