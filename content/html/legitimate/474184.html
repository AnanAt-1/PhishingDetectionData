Jimmy Rollins Baseball Stats | Baseball Almanac

Jimmy Rollins was born on Monday, November 27, 1978, in Oakland, California. Rollins was 21 years old when he broke into the major leagues on September 17, 2000, with the Philadelphia Phillies. Jimmy Rollins stats, height, weight, career totals, schools, positions, and more historical research are in the Baseball Almanac.

James Calvin Rollins was a Major League Baseball player with the Philadelphia Phillies (2000-2014), Los Angeles Dodgers (2015), and Chicago White Sox (2016). Did you know that Curtis Granderson and Jimmy, his nickname, were only the third and fourth Major League players in baseball history, respectively, to record twenty or more doubles, triples, home runs and stolen bases during the same season? In 2007, Granderson and Rollins joined Wildfire Schulte of the 1911 Chicago Cubs (30 2Bs, 21 3Bs, 21 HRs, 23 SBs), and Willie Mays of the 1957 San Francisco Giants (26 2Bs, 20 3Bs, 35 HRs, 38 SBs).

Did you know that J-Roll is the Philadelphia Phillies all-time leader in at bats (8,628), hits (2,306), and doubles (479); second in games played (2,090 | Mike Schmidt had 2,404) and stolen bases (453 | Billy Hamilton had 508); and third in runs scored (1,325 | Mike Schmidt had 1,506 & Ed Delahanty had 1,367), and triples (111 | Ed Delahanty had 157 & Sherry Magee had 127)?

During the 1996 American League Championship Series a fan in the stands (Jeffrey Maier) reached over the fence and caught a Derek Jeter fly ball that was ruled a home run. Umpire Rich Garcia was asked to call fan interference because a leaping attempt was made by the outfielder, who also happened to be Rollins' cousin! Do you remember who it was? [Answer]

Did you know that Jimmy Rollins led the National League in triples four times (2001, 2002, 2004, 2007), a feat bested by just one senior circuit player — hall of famer Stan Musial (1943, 1946, 1948, 1949, 1951)!

Jimmy Rollins (716 at-bats in 2007) was the fourth player in baseball history with 700-or-more at-bats during a single season, joining Willie Wilson (705 at-bats in 1980), Juan Samuel (701 at-bats in 1984), and Ichiro Suzuki (704 at-bats in 2004). No player, before or since, has ever had as many at-bats during a single season! [At-Bats Records]

Only five players have ever taken the field at shortstop more often than Jimmy Rollins, who finished his career with 2,227 games played at short; they are hall of famer Cal Ripken, Jr. (2,302), hall of famer Ozzie Smith (2,511), hall of famer Luis Aparicio (2,581), future hall of famer Derek Jeter (2,674) and future hall of famer Omar Vizquel (2,709).
