    Mali Visa Policy

    According to the current visa policy of Mali, all visitors coming to the country must apply for a visa in advance at any of their foreign missions, on arrival or online, before they arrive in the country. The only exception is those who come from visa-exempt countries; therefore, they don't need visas for entry.

    Mali has a visa exemption list for countries whose nationals don't need visas, and it is dominated by fellow West African countries that are members of the Economic Community of West African States. ECOWAS citizens can visit Mali visa-free for noncommercial purposes for 90 days without visas, the same way Malians can visit other member states visa-free for the same period. A few other non-West African countries complete the list.

    Furthermore, holders of service and diplomatic passports from Cuba, China, Brazil, Russia, and a few other countries also enjoy 90 days of visa-free access. In comparison, other nationalities who hold these passports can stay in Mali visa-free for 90 days. The list of visa-free passports keeps changing as new diplomatic alliances are made, so it is always dynamic.

    Mali Visa On Arrival

    The visa on arrival is an option for visitors who don't qualify for visa-free entry. Only a few countries, including Rwanda and Kenya, can have their citizens receive visas once they arrive in Mali. Once the visitor arrives at any of the ports of entry, their passports and other documents are examined and stamped with a sticker visa attached to the passport. This visa allows them to stay in the country for 30-90 days.

    Note: Syrian passport holders are not allowed to enter Mali as transit passengers even if they remain onboard throughout the time the plane is in Malian jurisdiction.

    Mali e Visa

    Mail's online or electronic visa is a short-term visa for temporary visits. This visa is designed for tourists, business visitors, and transit passengers. Visitors who are ineligible for visa-free entry may visit an online platform like VisaHQ to apply for this electronic visa. It allows the holder to stay in the country for a period of 90 days, and it becomes as active from the moment the visitor sets foot in any part of Mali.

    E visas were introduced to reduce the pressure on Malian foreign missions and to hasten the visa facilitation process. If you plan to stay in Mali for a longer period, get a consular visa from the embassy instead.

    Key Information About Mail e Visa and Travel Information and Tips

    Apply At The Right Time: When applying for an e-visa, always apply for it early enough and only when outside Mali. This e-visa is processed within 72 hours after the government receives your online application, but there may be delays by a few days. That is why applying for it at least a week before your planned trip is only fair. Also, remember that the visa must be released to you before your arrival.

    Be Security Conscious: Mali has had a prolonged conflict with terrorist groups that infiltrated the country from up north, but the government has brought the situation under control in recent times. Despite the new development, you should always be careful when outdoors. Avoid regions known for previous terrorist activities and stay away from crowded places as much as possible. Also, ensure that you stay in the middle of city centers with a strong presence of military and para-military forces.

    Never Overstay: As a visitor, do not overstay your welcome in the country. Plan your itinerary properly and leave before your visa expires. Holders of e-visas can only stay in Mali for up to 90 days.

    Not For Work: As we pointed out in a previous section, this visa doesn't qualify you to hold employment as an expatriate. If you must work, apply for a work visa. E visas are for noncommercial endeavors, and they are temporary.

    Use Approved Routes: Only travel in and out of Mail through approved routes for your safety. Approved routes are known to have a strong law enforcement presence, such as airports and legal border crossings. Please plan your trip accordingly.

    Passport And Validity: The validity of your e-visa and passport are linked. Once your passport expires, the visa also expires, even if it has a few more days or weeks to expire. Applicants are advised to apply with passports with at least 6 months of validity. They must also travel with the same one they used in applying for the visa.

    Visa Extension: Extension of visas is possible, but one has to visit the office of the immigration department in the city they reside in. The number of extra days or weeks given will be at the discretion of the immigration officer in charge.
